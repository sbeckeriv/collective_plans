# 🤖 Bride of Pinbot - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1991 — Designed by John Trudeau & Python Anghelo, Software by Brian Eddy*

> **"Shoot the face. Light the heart. Score the billion."**

Bride of Pinbot (Williams, 1991) is a WPC-era machine featuring the Bride — a robotic woman whose rotating face on the upper playfield changes as you advance (from skull to winking robotic face to human). The game revolves around lighting all the letters in BRIDE via the spinning left ramp wheel, completing the mini-playfield bagatelle, and reaching the famous 1 Billion jackpot shot. Our goal: **500M–1B** by lighting the Big Wheel jackpot and exploiting multiball.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Shoot the left ramp (Big Wheel)** to spin the wheel and advance BRIDE letters
- [ ] **Complete B-R-I-D-E** via the wheel to light the 1 Billion shot
- [ ] **Feed the upper mini-playfield** via the skill shot plunger to the bagatelle
- [ ] **Hit the spinning Heartbeat Ramp** (center) — it diverts either to the inlane or back to the plunger depending on mini-playfield path
- [ ] **Light and collect multiball** via the right ramp lock
- [ ] **Complete the BRIDE face animation** (skull → robot → human) for progressive bonuses

---

## 💍 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot (Elevated Plunger)

The elevated plunger lane launches the ball into the pop bumper area via a kicker — not a conventional skill shot:
- **Full plunge:** Ball drops to the kicker, goes to pop bumpers
- **Soft plunge:** Ball enters the mini-playfield bagatelle directly (higher value path)

The mini-playfield skill shot is worth attempting — the bagatelle awards bonus multiplier steps.

### Big Wheel (Left Ramp)

The Big Wheel is a large spinning disc on the left ramp. Shooting the left ramp spins the wheel:
- The wheel's lit section determines which BRIDE letter is lit
- The wheel slows and lands on a letter: B, R, I, D, or E
- Each letter collected advances the Bride's face animation

**Completing B-R-I-D-E lights the 1 Billion shot** — the most famous single shot in 1990s pinball.

### The 1 Billion Shot

When all five BRIDE letters are collected:
- A specific shot (operator-configured — typically the left loop or right ramp) is lit for 1 BILLION points
- This is a unique moment; no other era machine offered this explicitly
- Post-collection, the sequence can repeat at a lower value

### Heartbeat Ramp (Center — Dual Path)

The center ramp splits into two paths:
- **Main path:** Routes the ball to the right inlane for left flipper play
- **Mini-playfield path:** Routes through the bagatelle (if the ball takes the upper diverter path)

The mini-playfield bagatelle awards random bonuses, multiplier steps, and ball saves.

### Bride's Face (Progressive Animation)

The mechanical face in the upper playfield rotates through stages:
- **Skull:** Starting state
- **Winking robot:** After first BRIDE completion
- **Human face:** After second BRIDE completion

Each stage change awards a progressive jackpot bonus. Reaching "human" = the machine's cosmetic wizard state.

## 🎳 Multiball (2-Ball)

### Bride Multiball

**How to start:** Lock 1 ball in the right side lock (lit after specific wheel completions) → 2-ball multiball releases.

**Jackpot structure:**
- 2-ball multiball (the machine's only multiball)
- Both balls active simultaneously
- **Jackpot:** Shoot the left ramp (Big Wheel) during multiball — the wheel spins and awards a jackpot based on landing position
- **Best outcome:** Wheel lands on the lit super jackpot position → 25M–100M+

---

## 🔄 Progression Systems

- **BRIDE letters:** Accumulate within the game; each full set changes the face and raises jackpot values
- **Face progression:** Skull → Robot → Human is a game-wide progression; reaching Human is the meta-goal
- **Mini-playfield bagatelle:** Awards random bonuses; some positions add extra ball lights

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Left ramp (Big Wheel) | BRIDE letter + jackpot | Low |
| Right ramp (lock) | Ball lock + jackpot | Low |
| Right loop (small wheel) | Bonus advance | Low |
| Left loop | Bonus + 1B shot (when lit) | Low |
| Heartbeat ramp (center) | Dual path + bonus | Medium |
| Mini-playfield bagatelle | Random bonus | Low |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Right outlane** — fast exit from Heartbeat Ramp; primary drain path
- **Post-wheel eject** — the Big Wheel ramp ejects the ball back to the right flipper; be ready

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Left ramp × 4 (advance BRIDE — wheels are random but 4 shots usually gets 4 letters)
2. Right ramp × 1 (lock attempt)
3. Heartbeat ramp for bagatelle

### Ball 2
1. Complete BRIDE → 1 Billion shot when lit
2. Start 2-ball multiball (Big Wheel jackpot during multiball)
3. Second BRIDE completion for robot face

### Ball 3
1. Second multiball
2. Human face completion (third BRIDE set)
3. Post-multiball wheel harvesting

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 50M–150M |
| Solid | 200M–500M |
| Strong (1B shot + MB) | 500M–800M |
| Elite (Human face) | 1B+ |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The Big Wheel is random but not chaotic.** After 4–5 shots you'll typically have 4 letters. Track which are missing and note that the wheel has landing weight — learn where it tends to stop on your machine.

> **The 1 Billion shot is the most famous in pinball history.** When it lights, take a breath and aim carefully. It's one specific shot; don't rush it.

> **Mini-playfield bagatelle is worth chasing via the soft plunge.** The multiplier steps it awards compound significantly at ball end.
