# ✨ Stardust - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1971 — Designed by Norm Clark*

> **"Five bumpers. Two kick-outs. One spinner. Find the flow."**

Stardust is a 1971 Williams EM with five pop bumpers (unusually high for the era), two kick-out holes, a playfield spinner, an up-post between the flippers, and right-side ball return gates. The dancing theme is reflected in the light show design, but the game plays as a pure bonus-collect machine: build base bonus via spinner and bumpers, collect via kick-out holes, and survive long enough for the up-post to save you. Our goal: **20,000–40,000** (EM 5-digit scoring) through repeated kick-out collections and spinner farming.

## 💫 Core Mechanics

### Spinner (Playfield)

The center/side spinner advances bonus base with each spin. Fast passes through the spinner add 5–15 bonus units per orbit, making it the primary scoring route early in each ball.

### Kick-Out Holes (2)

Two kick-out saucers on the playfield. Each landing:
- Collects the accumulated bonus (base × multiplier)
- Ejects the ball back to a flipper

**Strategy:** Build bonus via spinner and bumpers, then aim for a kick-out hole to cash in. Repeat the cycle — two collections per ball is achievable with good play.

### Pop Bumpers (5)

Five pop bumpers is exceptional for a 1971 machine — the central bumper cluster is dense. Each bumper hit scores points and builds bonus base. Extended bumper sessions are extremely valuable; don't exit the bumper zone prematurely.

### Up-Post (Between Flippers)

The up-post rises from between the two flippers to block the center drain gap. When lit (via specific shots or timed events), it's a passive ball saver. It doesn't stay up permanently — watch for when it activates and play accordingly.

### Ball Return Gates (Right Side, 2)

Two gate mechanisms on the right side return the ball back to the playfield from would-be drain paths. They're geometric lane features — the ball rolls through them and curves back toward the flippers. They're not operator-controlled; they're always active.

### Standup Targets (4)

Four standup targets advance the bonus base and light features. Complete all four for a bonus increment. They don't reset between collections.

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **5 bumpers is exceptional.** Don't fight to escape the bumper zone early — each bumper pass adds to your base. Let the ball bounce 8–10 times before redirecting.

> **Two collections per ball.** The kick-out cycle (spinner → bumpers → kick-out) can be run twice per ball with good play. Prioritize the cycle over chasing standup targets.

> **Up-post is a bonus, not a strategy.** Play as if it doesn't exist; if it saves you, great. Don't position shots around hoping it activates.
