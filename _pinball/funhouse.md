# 🤡 Funhouse - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1990 — Designed by Pat Lawlor & Larry DeMar*

> **"Put Rudy to sleep."**

Funhouse is a Pat Lawlor classic built around Rudy, the talking animatronic doll head, and a simple-but-deep core loop: most shots advance a clock toward midnight, and the Mirror feature offers genuinely unlimited extra balls if you can complete it every ball. Our goal: advance the clock to midnight, shoot Rudy's open mouth to start Midnight Multiball, and complete the Mirror's six awards for Super Frenzy.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Don't worry about deliberately advancing the clock** — most shots on the playfield do this automatically just through normal play
- [ ] **At 11:30, the lock lights** — lock 2 balls sequentially (11:30 → 11:45 → midnight) to progress toward Midnight Multiball
- [ ] **At midnight, Rudy falls asleep and opens his mouth** — shoot the ball in for **1,000,000 points** and start **Midnight Multiball**
- [ ] **Focus your real strategic attention on the Mirror** — it's completable once per ball and offers **unlimited extra balls**, meaning a skilled player could theoretically extend a single game indefinitely
- [ ] **Re-light the Mirror shot by hitting Rudy**, or it auto-relights briefly when the ball passes the outer right inlane
- [ ] **Avoid tilting during Super Frenzy** — its points are collected during bonus, so a tilt there forfeits everything

## 🪞 Core Mechanics

### The Clock & Midnight Multiball

- **Most shots on the playfield naturally advance the clock** — there's little need to chase this deliberately, since it happens as a byproduct of normal play
- **At 11:30**, the **lock lights**
- **Lock balls sequentially**: 1st lock → 11:45, 2nd lock → midnight
- **At midnight**, Rudy falls asleep and snores, opening his mouth
- **Shoot the ball into his open mouth** for **1,000,000 points** and to start **Midnight Multiball**
- During multiball, a **trapdoor opens** for a **2-million-point jackpot**, accessible via the **inner loop from the upper flipper**

### The Mirror (Your Real Priority)

Hit the **scoop** while the **white Mirror Value arrow is lit** to collect one of six awards. The shot is reachable directly from the right flipper, or as a **bank shot off the Superdog** from the left flipper.

**Re-lighting the shot:** hit **Rudy**, or it auto-relights briefly when the ball passes through the **outer right inlane**.

**Six Mirror Awards:**

| Award | Effect |
|---|---|
| Light extra ball | Collected at the lock |
| Light Million Rudy shot | High-value Rudy hit |
| Superdog | High points at red standup targets |
| Quick Multiball | 2-ball multiball worth 1 million Rudy hits |
| Open Steps | Playfield progression feature |
| Pop bumper advancement | Boosts pop bumper value |

**Super Frenzy:** Collect all six Mirror awards to light **Super Frenzy**, where **all switches score big points**. ⚠️ **Frenzy points are collected during bonus** — so a tilt during this phase forfeits everything you've earned.

> 💰 **Pro Tip — The Whole Game in One Sentence:** Since you can complete the Mirror **once per ball** and it offers **genuinely unlimited extra balls**, a sufficiently skilled player could theoretically extend a single game of Funhouse indefinitely. Focus almost entirely on the Mirror and the Clock; nearly everything else is secondary.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Tilting during Super Frenzy** | Forfeits all frenzy points since they're collected during bonus | Play extra carefully once Super Frenzy is lit — no aggressive nudging |
| **The ramp's diverter** | Routes the ball to either the left inlane or left outlane for a skill shot attempt — a miscalculated read can mean an unexpected drain | Understand the diverter's current routing before committing to the ramp |
| **Trapdoor loop shots during multiball** | Mistimed upper loop attempts can be wasted | Only attempt when you have a clean shot at the inner loop |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1)

1. Play naturally — the clock advances on its own through normal shots
2. Complete your first Mirror cycle for an early extra ball or other award
3. Build toward your first lock at 11:30

**Target Score:** 5M–15M

### Mid Game (Ball 2)

1. Push the clock toward midnight via locks
2. Complete the Mirror again — remember, it's completable once per ball
3. Shoot Rudy's mouth at midnight for 1M and Midnight Multiball

**Target Score:** 15M–50M

### Late Game (Ball 3+)

1. Keep cycling the Mirror every ball for extra balls — this is your primary scoring/survival lever
2. Collect all six Mirror awards when possible to light Super Frenzy, then play extremely carefully (no tilting)
3. Run Midnight Multiball and grab the trapdoor jackpot via the inner loop

**Target Score per ball:** 20M–80M+ (potentially unbounded with skilled Mirror cycling)

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 6.5°–7.0° | Standard for early-90s Williams solid-state machines |
| **Mirror scoop mechanism** | Verify reliable registration | Central to the entire extra-ball engine of the game |
| **Rudy animatronic mechanism** | Check mouth-open timing and hit registration | Core to both the Mirror re-light and Midnight Multiball start |

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Every ball:** Complete the Mirror's six awards for extra balls and Super Frenzy access, let the clock advance naturally, lock toward midnight, shoot Rudy's mouth for Midnight Multiball.

**Target Outcome:** Completing the Mirror every ball and stacking extra balls represents true mastery of Funhouse — this is one of the few pinball machines where a sufficiently skilled player can extend a single game almost indefinitely.
