# 🎱 Eight Ball - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1977 — Designed by George Christian*

> **"Sink your balls. Pocket the eight."**

Eight Ball was a genuine industry milestone — the best-selling pinball machine of all time at 20,230 units — and the first Bally Solid State game to carry player progress from ball to ball via on-board memory. The theme mirrors real pool: Players 1 & 3 shoot solids (1-7), Players 2 & 4 shoot stripes (9-15), and everyone's chasing the same 8-ball payoff. Our goal: complete your full ball set, sink the 8 ball, and cash in Super Bonus on the next ball.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Know your ball set**: Players 1 & 3 shoot solid balls 1-7; Players 2 & 4 shoot striped balls 9-15
- [ ] **Complete balls 1-4 via the top lanes**, balls 5 & 6 via the left/right standup targets, and ball 7 via the right return lane
- [ ] **Each completed ball is worth 3,000 in bonus** — this and the spinner are your main point sources
- [ ] **Once all 7 of your balls are made, the 8-ball target lights** — hit it for the big payoff
- [ ] **Making the 8 ball lights the right outlane for Special** and qualifies you for **Super Bonus on your next ball in play**
- [ ] **Grind the spinner and candy cane shot** between ball completions for steady supplemental scoring

## 🎯 Core Mechanics

### The Pool-Themed Ball Set

| Player | Ball Set |
|---|---|
| 1st & 3rd | Solids (1-7) |
| 2nd & 4th | Stripes (9-15) |

Each of your 7 balls maps to a specific playfield shot:

| Ball # | How to Complete |
|---|---|
| 1, 2, 3, 4 | Top lanes |
| 5 | Left standup target |
| 6 | Right standup target |
| 7 | Right return lane (the only lane for ball 7) |

### Bonus System

- **Each completed ball is worth 3,000 in bonus**
- This carries across balls thanks to the game's pioneering **on-board memory** — your progress doesn't reset when play passes to another player
- **Bonus and the spinner are your primary point sources**, with the candy cane shot adding supplemental value

### The 8-Ball Payoff

1. Complete **all 7** of your balls (solids or stripes, depending on your player position)
2. The **8-ball target lights**
3. Making the 8 ball:
   - **Lights the right outlane to score Special**
   - **Qualifies you for Super Bonus on your following ball in play**

> 🎱 **Pro Tip:** The 8-ball completion is the single biggest strategic milestone in the game — everything before it is setup, and the Super Bonus reward on your next ball makes finishing your full ball set worth real prioritization over chasing the spinner.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Pop bumper unpredictability** | Standard late-70s bumper chaos | Treat as incidental scoring, not a shot to actively chase |
| **Left outlane** | Has a kickback, but don't rely on it being lit at all times | Check kickback status before assuming protection |
| **Candy cane shot misses** | Can deflect unpredictably if mistimed | Approach with controlled, deliberate shots |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball

1. Start working your ball set via top lanes (1-4) first — they're the most numerous and straightforward
2. Pick up the left and right standup targets for balls 5 and 6
3. Grind the spinner for steady supplemental points along the way

### Mid Ball

1. Complete ball 7 via the right return lane to light the 8-ball target
2. Shoot for the 8 ball once lit — this is your biggest scoring opportunity
3. Keep the spinner and candy cane shot in your rotation between attempts

### Late Ball

1. If you made the 8 ball, remember **Super Bonus carries to your next ball in play** — don't waste that qualification
2. Bank accumulated bonus (3,000 per ball completed) at end of ball
3. Take the right outlane Special if it's lit from a prior 8-ball completion

**Early:** Work top lanes for balls 1-4, pick up standups for 5-6.
