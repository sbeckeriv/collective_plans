# ⚡ Flash (Williams, 1979) - Strategy Guide

*Williams Electronics, 1979 — Designed by Steve Ritchie*

> **"Hit the drops. Cash the kicker. Don't tilt."**

Flash (Williams, 1979) is Steve Ritchie's first pinball design and one of Williams' earliest solid-state machines. It's a fast, straightforward game built around two drop target banks, a spinner, a kick-out hole, five star rollovers in the lanes, and three flippers (including a center post flipper). The playfield uses underfield flash lamps for visual feedback. Score cap is 999,990. Our goal: **400,000–900,000** by completing drop target banks repeatedly and cashing the kick-out hole bonus.

## 🎯 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

Flash has no true skill shot — the ball launches across the playfield to the top lanes via a curved shooter lane. Roll through lit star rollover lanes at the top to advance the bonus multiplier.

### 5-Bank Drop Targets (Left)

The primary scoring sequence:
- Complete all 5 targets: scores a completion bonus and resets the bank
- Each target down: base points
- Repeated completions in a single ball: each completion raises the completion bonus value
- The 5-bank is the highest-value repeating sequence on the machine

### 3-Bank Drop Targets (Right)

Secondary drop target bank:
- Complete all 3: scores a smaller completion bonus and resets
- Resets independently from the 5-bank
- Good target of opportunity when approaching from the right flipper

### Kick-Out Hole

The kick-out hole (center-right) is lit based on drop target progress:
- When lit: collect the accumulated bonus
- The bonus is built by completing drop target banks and rolling through star lanes
- The kick-out ejects the ball back toward the left flipper

### Spinner (Center)

The center spinner:
- Each rotation: fixed point award
- A full-power shot through the spinner at speed accumulates meaningful points
- The spinner is accessible from both left and right flippers

### Star Rollover Lanes (Top — 5 lanes)

Five rollover buttons at the top of the playfield:
- Each lit rollover collected: advances bonus multiplier (1× → 2× → 3× → 5×)
- Max multiplier (5×) applies to all drop target completions and the kick-out collect
- Rolling through unlit lanes still scores base points
- The center flipper can redirect balls toward specific top lanes

### Standup Targets (2)

Two standup targets on the right side:
- Fixed point awards
- Lower priority than drop banks

### Pop Bumpers (3)

Three pop bumpers in the upper playfield:
- Passive scoring during ball travel
- Active during high-traffic sequences through the top lanes

- **Left outlane** — after a hard right-to-left cross; this machine runs fast for 1979
- **Center drain** — the center flipper reduces center drain risk but can't catch everything
- **Tilt** — early SS machines have sensitive tilt mechanisms; avoid nudging aggressively

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Roll through lit star lanes (multiplier build)
2. Clear the 5-bank × 2 (two completions)
3. Collect kick-out hole when lit
4. Pass through spinner on transitions

### Ball 2
1. Continue star lane completions toward 3× or 5× multiplier
2. 5-bank × 3 completions (completion bonus scaling)
3. 3-bank × 2 completions

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The center flipper is your friend.** Flash's third (center) flipper can direct balls that would otherwise drain down the middle. Use it actively — press the button the moment the ball descends past the slingshots.

> **Multiplier first, targets second.** Rolling through the 5× star lane set before cycling the drop banks dramatically increases your completion bonus value. Prioritize getting to 3× at minimum before grinding the 5-bank.

> **The 5-bank is the game.** Steve Ritchie's first design is a pure drop target machine. Everything else is secondary. Master the approach angle to the 5-bank from the left flipper and the game opens up.

> **Score cap is 999,990.** Flash is a 6-digit machine — a realistic high game on a well-set-up machine is in the 600,000–900,000 range.
