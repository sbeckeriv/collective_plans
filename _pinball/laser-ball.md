## 🎮 Core Mechanics

### Playfield Layout

Laser Ball is a **wide-body** machine with **three flippers**:
- Two standard lower flippers
- One upper-right flipper (use sparingly!)

**Key Features:**
- 10 numbered top lanes (1-10)
- 10 star rollovers in bowling-pin pattern
- L-A-S-E-R drop target bank (5 targets)
- B-A-L-L drop target bank (4 targets)
- Left bullseye standup target
- Right saucer (kickout hole)
- Two spinners (left and right orbits)
- Pop bumpers
- Outlane kickback arcs

### 🎱 Plunge Options

| Plunge Type         | Result                                            |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Standard Plunge** | Ball enters top lanes, scores 1-2 numbers         |
| **First Plunge**    | Automatically scores TWO numbers if you need both |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** There is NO lane change feature! After your first plunge, you must **nudge** the ball toward lanes with numbers you still need.

## 🔢 The Number System (1-10)

The core mechanic revolves around collecting all 10 numbers:

**Ways to Collect Numbers:**
1. **Top Lanes** - Score one or two numbers per pass
2. **Star Rollovers** - 10 rollovers in bowling-pin pattern in center playfield

**Completing All 10 Numbers:**
- Increases your **AE Value** by 10,000 points
- AE Value starts at 10K → maxes at **60K**
- AE Value is scored by completing B-A-L-L targets

| 1st completion | 20,000       |
| 2nd completion | 30,000       |
| 3rd completion | 40,000       |

## 🎮 Advanced Strategy: UTAD (Up The Apron, Dummy!)

The optimal strategy for Laser Ball is surprisingly simple:

> **Shoot the two orbits to the top repeatedly.**

**Why This Works:**
1. Collects numbers for AE value
2. Ball falls through bumpers → randomly hits BALL targets
3. Ball randomly enters right saucer → advances multiplier
4. Spinners score big when lit
5. Avoids dangerous center playfield shots

## 🔧 Upper Right Flipper Usage

> **⚠️ Expert Tip:** Avoid using the upper right flipper if possible!

**Better Approach:**
- Hold the flipper UP as a deflector
- Let the ball bounce off it safely
- Only flip if absolutely necessary

The upper flipper has too much side-to-side variance for reliable drop target shots.
