# 🤠 Texan - Strategy Guide

*Gottlieb, 1960 — Designed by Wayne Neyens*

> **"Gobble holes, relay kick-outs, and a roto-target. Welcome to 1960."**

Texan is a 1960 Gottlieb EM replay machine with three relay kick-out holes, two gobble holes (which swallow the ball permanently for a score, then end that ball's possession), a roto-target, six passive bumpers, four standup targets, and a 1,999-point score cap. The gobble holes are the defining mechanic — feeding them awards large points but ends your ball. Our goal: **1,800–1,999** by balancing gobble hole risk against cumulative target and bumper scoring.

## 🤠 Core Mechanics

### Gobble Holes (2) — The Risk/Reward Core

Gobble holes are physical holes in the playfield that permanently swallow the ball:
- If the ball enters a gobble hole, you score the hole's value (typically 200–500 points) AND lose possession of that ball
- The next ball launches fresh
- **Strategy:** Avoid gobble holes on your last ball (Ball 3 or Ball 5). On earlier balls, a well-timed gobble hole can be worth it if the score gain justifies the ball loss.

> ⚠️ **A gobble hole on ball 5 (last ball) ends the game.** Always know which ball you're on.

### Relay Kick-Out Holes (3)

Three kick-out holes that score points and return the ball to play (unlike gobble holes):
- Each ejects the ball back after scoring
- Score values vary (100–300 points each)
- Completely safe — no possession loss
- Prioritize these over gobble holes for consistent mid-ball scoring

### Roto-Target (1)

A spinning rotating target in the upper playfield that shows different values on its faces:
- Hit it when a high-value face is showing for maximum score
- Standard EM roto-target behavior — watch for a moment before shooting
- The highest available repeatable score on the machine per hit

### Passive Bumpers (6)

Six passive bumpers (ringless stationary bumpers that score when the ball rolls through) plus 3 pop bumpers. The passive bumpers line the upper area:
- Each passive bumper scores on contact (10–50 points)
- Extended orbits through the upper area collect multiple passive bumpers per pass
- This is the machine's primary "cruise control" scoring method

### Standup Targets (4)

Four standup targets spread around the lower playfield:
- Complete all 4 to light replay (free game)
- Individual target score: 50–100 points each
- Completing a set resets the sequence for a second completion attempt

### Replay Wheel (Maximum 26)

Like all 1960 Gottlieb replay machines, the replay wheel tracks your replay credits. Each relay kick-out and target completion advances the wheel. Earning replays extends your total game time.

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### 3-Ball Play
- Ball 1: Roto-target + relay kick-outs; avoid gobble holes
- Ball 2: Standup target completion (replay) + roto-target
- Ball 3: **No gobble holes.** Passive bumpers + kick-outs + roto-target only

### 5-Ball Play
- Balls 1–2: Gobble hole one time if the score gain justifies it; roto-target farming
- Balls 3–4: Standup target completions; relay kick-out cycle
- Ball 5: Same as Ball 3 — zero gobble holes

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The gobble holes are the game's central design tension.** They offer the best single-ball point gain but cost you that ball. On balls 1–4, a well-timed gobble hole can be a calculated risk. On ball 5, they are forbidden.

> **Six passive bumpers is exceptional accumulation.** Every orbit through the upper area hits 3–6 bumpers. Route the ball there deliberately between kick-out approaches.

> **Know your ball number.** On a 4-player machine in 1960, the ball counter was mechanical and easy to misread. Always verify which ball you're on before approaching a gobble hole.
