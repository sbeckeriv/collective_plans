# 🤠 Buckaroo - Strategy Guide

*Gottlieb, 1965 — Designed by Wayne Neyens*

> **"Light the numbers. Ride for the replay."**

Buckaroo is a classic woodrail-era EM built around two roto-targets that light a 1-through-7 number sequence on the playfield. With no flashy multiball or wizard mode to chase, success here is purely about hitting the roto-targets accurately and stringing together adjacent numbers. Our goal: light as many of the up-to-four replay awards as the sequence allows.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Hit the two roto-targets repeatedly** — each hit lights the next number in the 1-through-7 sequence
- [ ] **Light any 4 *adjacent* numbers** in the sequence to award a replay — and you can do this **up to 4 times** in a single game
- [ ] **Use the top A-B-C-D standup/rollover lights** to light the top center rollover for additional value
- [ ] **Expect short, precise shots** — this layout rewards accuracy and good ball control over power
- [ ] **Watch the backbox** — the mechanical horse-kicks-cowboy animation is just for show, but it's a fun confirmation you're making progress

---

## 🐴 Core Mechanics

### Roto-Targets & the 1–7 Number Sequence

Two roto-targets on the playfield light numbers **1 through 7** as you hit them. The numbers display in sequence on the playfield, and your goal is to build **adjacent runs**:

| Achievement | Reward |
|---|---|
| Light any **4 adjacent numbers** in the 1–7 sequence | **1 Replay** |
| Repeat the feat (up to 4 times total in one game) | **Up to 4 Replays** |

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Because the award is based on *adjacent* numbers, don't just hit roto-targets randomly — pay attention to which numbers are already lit and aim for combinations that complete or extend a run of 4 in a row.

### A-B-C-D Lights & Top Center Rollover

Hitting the top **A-B-C-D** lights opens the **top center rollover** for additional scoring — a secondary objective to work on between roto-target attempts.

### Pop Bumpers & Slingshots

Three pop bumpers and two slingshots provide standard incidental scoring and keep the ball lively in the upper and middle playfield — there's no special strategy here beyond letting them do their job while you reset for your next roto-target shot.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Shot | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Roto-target shots in general** | This is a "short shots" layout demanding accuracy — overly aggressive flipper hits can send the ball astray | Favor controlled, accurate shots over full-power slaps |
| **Ball reflection off flippers** | The woodrail-era flipper geometry requires good reflection control | Practice consistent flipper timing rather than relying on raw power |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball

1. Start hitting roto-targets to begin lighting the 1–7 sequence
2. Work the A-B-C-D lights opportunistically for the top center rollover
3. Track which numbers are lit so you can plan toward a 4-adjacent run

### Mid Ball

1. Complete your first 4-adjacent-number run for a replay
2. Keep building further adjacent runs — the sequence can be re-lit for additional replay awards
3. Use pop bumper and slingshot time as incidental scoring, not a primary focus

### Late Ball

1. Push for as many of the up-to-4 replay awards as you can manage
2. Stay accurate on roto-target shots — this is a precision game, not a power game
3. Bank whatever top center rollover value you can pick up along the way

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 5.5°–6.0° | Standard for mid-60s Gottlieb woodrail machines |
| **Roto-target mechanism** | Verify smooth rotation | Central to the entire scoring system — a sticky roto-target breaks the core loop |
| **Flipper rubber condition** | Keep fresh | This short-shot layout is sensitive to flipper rebound consistency |

---

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Throughout the game:** Hit roto-targets with precision, track the 1–7 sequence, and aim for 4-adjacent-number runs for replay awards — up to 4 are available per game.

**Target Outcome:** Earning **2 or more of the 4 available replays** in a single game represents a strong, well-played run on this classic woodrail machine.
