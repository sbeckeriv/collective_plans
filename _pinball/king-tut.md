# 👑 King Tut - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1969 — Designed by Ted Zale*

> **"Spell the King's name. Use the post wisely."**

King Tut is a straightforward late-60s EM built around spelling the game's own name and lighting rollover lanes for bonus multiplier advancement. With fixed stationary targets and a simple ruleset, success here comes down to consistent rollover lane coverage and smart use of the center up-post. Our goal: light all rollover lanes, max your bonus multiplier, and use the center post to set up clean shots at the N and G targets.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Spell K-I-N-G-T-U-T** by hitting the relevant standup targets across the playfield
- [ ] **Light all rollover lanes** for additional points and to advance your bonus multiplier
- [ ] **Use the center up-post strategically** to line up shots toward the **N and G** targets specifically — this is the recommended technique for this machine
- [ ] **Use the left outlane kickback** for drain protection
- [ ] **Take advantage of the right outlane ball return gate** to keep balls in play longer

## 👑 Core Mechanics

### Spelling KING TUT

Complete the standup targets that spell out **K-I-N-G-T-U-T** across the playfield — the central, straightforward objective of the game.

### Rollover Lanes & Bonus Multiplier

**Lighting all rollover lanes** scores additional points and can **increase your bonus multiplier** — a key secondary system to track alongside your KING TUT progress.

### Center Up-Post (Key Technique)

The **center post between the flippers** is best used for **lining up shots specifically toward the N and G targets** — these two letters are noted as the ones where deliberate post positioning pays off the most.

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Don't think of the center post purely as a defensive tool — use it offensively to set up precise N/G target shots, since these are call out specifically as where post positioning makes the biggest difference.

### Outlane Features

- **Left outlane kickback**: provides drain protection
- **Right outlane ball return gate**: helps return balls to play rather than draining outright
