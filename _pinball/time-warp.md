# ⏳ Time Warp (Williams, 1979) - Strategy Guide

*Williams Electronics, 1979 — Designed by Barry Oursler*

> **"Banana flippers and 5 pop bumpers: time warp to a different kind of flipper feel."**

Time Warp (Williams, 1979) is an early solid-state machine notable for its **banana (curved) flippers** — a short production run of this machine shipped with flippers that curve slightly at the tip, producing non-standard ball trajectories that reward players who learn the idiosyncratic angles. The game has a dual drop-target bank system, 5 pop bumpers, and 2 kick-out holes for bonus collection. Our goal: **200K–600K** by completing the drop target banks to advance the bonus multiplier and collecting via the kick-out holes.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Learn the banana flipper angles** — they shoot differently than standard flippers; what looks like a center shot may go wide
- [ ] **Complete the 5-bank drop targets** (left side) for the major bonus multiplier advance
- [ ] **Hit the 3-bank drop targets** (right side) for the secondary multiplier step
- [ ] **Shoot the kick-out holes** when lit for bonus collection (triggered by drop target completion)
- [ ] **Feed pop bumpers** for passive point accumulation when no targets are lit
- [ ] **Standup targets (2)** score flat points; not your priority

## 🎮 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Pop bumper feed** | Controlled plunge to upper cluster | Pop bumper chain + bonus advance |
| **Standard** | Full plunge | Ball to pop bumpers |

### Banana Flippers

The banana/curved flippers are this machine's defining physical trait:
- The slight curve at the tip redirects shots compared to straight flippers
- Left flipper banana tip: shots aimed at the 5-bank drop targets may require a slightly lower hold than expected
- Right flipper: the 3-bank targets sit closer to center — easier to hit
- **Practice low-power shots.** The curved tip allows mid-power shots to curve into position rather than require precision full-power

### 5-Bank Drop Targets (Left Side)

Five drop targets in a horizontal bank on the left playfield:
- Each target hit scores 500 points
- **Completing all 5:** Advances the bonus multiplier by one step AND lights the left kick-out hole
- Targets reset after completion — each cycle advances multiplier further
- Priority target: the leftmost target is hardest to reach from the right flipper; shoot with the left

### 3-Bank Drop Targets (Right Side)

Three drop targets on the right:
- Faster to complete (3 hits vs. 5)
- **Completing all 3:** Advances multiplier one step AND lights the right kick-out hole
- Secondary priority after the 5-bank

### Kick-Out Holes (2)

Two kick-out holes collect the bonus:
- Left kick-out: lit by 5-bank completion
- Right kick-out: lit by 3-bank completion
- Collecting either hole pays the accumulated bonus × current multiplier, then resets it
- **Collect before draining.** The bonus reset means uncollected bonus is lost.

### 5 Pop Bumpers

Heavy pop bumper cluster in the upper playfield:
- Each hit: 100 points (low individual value but high frequency)
- The banana flipper tendency to feed high makes the pop bumpers accessible
- Passive bonus accumulation while hunting targets

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Right flipper → 3-bank completions × 2 (fast multiplier start)
2. Left flipper → 5-bank completion (major multiplier)
3. Kick-out collection (bonus × 2–3×)

### Ball 2
1. 5-bank → 3-bank → 5-bank sequence
2. Kick-out at 4–5× multiplier
3. Let pop bumpers run for passive accumulation between target resets

### Ball 3
1. Max multiplier: 5-bank + 3-bank completions per ball
2. Collect via both kick-outs before each drain
3. Banana flipper precision shots to the 5-bank ends

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The banana flippers are the entire game.** Spend the first ball learning where the curved tips send the ball on each target bank. The "feel" is genuinely different from any other machine — muscle memory from other Williams games doesn't transfer directly.

> **Kick-out holes first after each completion.** Drop targets reset immediately; collecting the bonus before chasing the next bank is always the right move.

> **Pop bumper speed builds bonus.** With 5 pop bumpers, a ball that stays in the upper area for 15–20 seconds accumulates meaningful bonus before you even hit a target.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 6.5° — Williams 1979 standard |
| Banana flippers | Verify both are properly seated; replacement straight flippers change the machine's character |
| Kick-out hole coils | Test both eject cleanly — misfire means uncollected bonus |
| Drop targets | Verify all 8 targets reset cleanly (5-bank + 3-bank) |
