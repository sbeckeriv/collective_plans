# 🏄 Surf Side - Strategy Guide

*Gottlieb, 1967 — Designed by Ed Krynski*

> **"Roto-targets, multiplier ladders, and a 9,999 score cap. Pure EM chaos."**

Surf Side is a 1967 Gottlieb EM with four flippers, two roto-targets (spinning targets that show different values), six standup targets that advance dual multiplier ladders, and a carry-over multiplier system that persists between players until the 100× value is scored. Score cap is 9,999 — virtually all scoring comes from maximizing the roto-target value via the multiplier ladder system. Our goal: **9,000–9,999** by maxing the multiplier ladders before hitting the roto-targets.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Hit the four standup targets** (lower playfield) to advance both multiplier ladders
- [ ] **Roll through top rollovers** to advance each ladder by the roto-target value shown
- [ ] **Monitor the roto-target display** — it shows the current bonus value; higher = more per hit
- [ ] **Shoot the roto-targets** to collect their displayed value × your multiplier
- [ ] **Aim for the Star roto-target** for an extra ball (value = 1, but the extra ball is the prize)
- [ ] **Remember:** multiplier ladders carry over between players until 100× is scored — this is a shared resource in multiplayer games

## 🌊 Core Mechanics

### Multiplier Ladders (2)

Two multiplier ladders track on the left and right sides of the playfield. Both ladders advance together:
- **Standup targets (4, lower):** Advance both ladders 1 step each hit
- **Top rollovers:** Each rollover advances its corresponding ladder by the current roto-target value shown

The ladders control the multiplier applied when the roto-targets are scored.

### Roto-Targets (2)

Two rotating targets that spin to show different values. The current displayed value:
- Determines the roto-target's score when hit
- Also determines how many steps a top rollover grants the ladders

**Higher roto-target value = higher score per hit AND faster ladder advancement.**

The Star position on the roto-target (value = 1) awards an extra ball.

### Carry-Over Multiplier (Competitive Quirk)

The multiplier ladders carry over from player to player within a game and do NOT reset until the 100× value is scored. In a 2-player game:
- Player 1 builds the ladders
- Player 2 inherits them and can score at the elevated multiplier
- Once 100× is scored by either player, both ladders reset

In competitive play, this creates a strategic meta: do you try to score the 100× yourself, or let the other player inherit a partially reset state?

### Red and Yellow Standup Targets (Lower)

The 6 standup targets include both red and yellow variants. Hitting any of the 4 primary targets advances both ladders; the specific red/yellow standup targets in the lower playfield additionally score the current roto-target value (the same value shown on the rotating display).

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Standup targets (4) | Ladder advance | Low |
| Top rollovers | Fast ladder advance | Low (passive) |
| Roto-targets | Score × multiplier | Medium — moving target |
| Red/yellow standups | Roto value score | Low |
| Outlanes | Score roto value (yes!) | Very high risk |
| Pop bumpers (3) | Incidental | None |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Outlanes** — they score the roto-target value (!) but draining is almost never worth it
- **Roto-target approach** — the targets spin; shoot when a high value faces you, not randomly

### Four Flippers

The upper flipper pair accesses the top rollovers more directly than the lower flippers. Use upper flippers to deliberately route through the rollovers for faster ladder advancement.

---

## 💡 Multiplayer Strategy Note

> **Know where the ladders are before your turn.** If Player 1 has advanced both ladders to the 20× range, Player 2 can hit the roto-targets immediately for massive points. Don't waste inherited ladder position by shooting standups.

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Single Player
1. Standup targets × 4 (advance ladders)
2. Top rollover pass (more ladder advance via roto-value)
3. Check roto-target displayed value — shoot when high
4. Repeat until ladders are high, then farm roto-targets
5. Star roto-target for extra ball when convenient

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Time your roto-target shots.** These targets spin continuously — wait for a high-value face before shooting. Shooting at value 1 when value 10 is coming in 0.5 seconds is a wasted opportunity.

> **The top rollovers are multiplicative.** If the roto-target shows 5, each rollover advances the ladder by 5 instead of 1. Routing through rollovers when the roto value is high is enormously efficient.

> **Carry-over is the game's most unique mechanic.** Study it, exploit it, and in multiplayer games, respect that your ladder work benefits both players.
