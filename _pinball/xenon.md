# 🔮 Xenon (Bally, 1980) - Strategy Guide

*Bally Manufacturing, 1980 — Designed by Greg Kmiec, Voice by Suzanne Ciani*

> **"The first pinball machine to speak to you in a female voice — and it's Suzanne Ciani."**

Xenon (Bally, 1980) is a landmark machine: **the first pinball to feature speech synthesis with a female voice** (provided by avant-garde synthesizer artist Suzanne Ciani). The playfield is organized around a right-to-left elevated tube ramp, 4-bank drop targets for multiplier, 2 kick-out holes for bonus collection, and an early **2-ball multiball** — rare for 1980. The infinity lights in the backglass and Ciani's otherworldly voice cues make this one of the most atmospheric early SS machines ever built. Our goal: **3M–9M** (near the 9,999,990 cap) via multiplier stacking, tube ramp bonus cycles, and the 2-ball multiball.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Complete the 4-bank drop targets** to advance the bonus multiplier
- [ ] **Shoot the right ramp** → ball travels through the elevated tube → exits left lane (one of pinball's first ramp-tube combos)
- [ ] **Hit the 2 kick-out holes** when lit for bonus collection
- [ ] **Trigger the 2-ball multiball** by locking a ball in the designated spot
- [ ] **Listen for Suzanne Ciani's voice cues** — they announce mode changes and bonus milestones
- [ ] **Target the spinning target** for per-revolution scoring during lull periods

## 🎮 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Tube ramp feed** | Full plunge → right ramp | Ball travels tube → left lane award |
| **Top lane** | Timed medium plunge | Top lane bonus step |

### 4-Bank Drop Targets

Four drop targets in a bank are the primary multiplier driver:
- Each target hit: 1,000–5,000 points
- **Completing all 4:** Advances bonus multiplier one step AND lights kick-out holes
- Reset after completion; each cycle advances the multiplier further (up to 5×)
- **Priority target set.** Get here before other shots.

### Right Ramp → Elevated Tube → Left Lane

This is the machine's most distinctive physical feature:
- Shooting the right ramp sends the ball up through an elevated clear tube that arcs across the playfield
- Ball exits the tube at the left side, dropping into a reward lane
- The tube exit awards: tube bonus + any lit lane value
- One of the first ramp-tube combinations in pinball history
- **Aim for the tube on every available shot after drop targets are lit.** It's the safest continuous scoring route.

### 2 Kick-Out Holes

Two kick-out holes collect the bonus:
- Lit by completing the 4-bank drop targets
- Each collection: current bonus × current multiplier, then bonus resets
- Left hole: lower playfield → ejects toward upper-right
- Right hole: mid-right → ejects toward left flipper
- **Collect both before pursuing the next drop target bank.**

### 2-Ball Multiball

Early multiball mechanic (very rare for 1980):
- Lock one ball in the designated ball-lock spot (left side saucer)
- Second ball releases → 2-ball multiball begins
- During multiball: jackpot shots via the right ramp and kick-out holes
- Suzanne Ciani voice announces multiball start

### 4 Mini-Rollover Buttons

Four small rollover buttons in specific lanes:
- Each completes a lane for bonus progress
- Passive accumulation — the ball naturally hits them during playfield traversal

### Spinning Target

Center-area spinner/spinning target:
- Per-hit or per-revolution scoring
- Value increases if the 4-bank is currently lit

## 🎳 Multiball

### 2-Ball Multiball

**How to start:** Shoot the left saucer (ball lock) once → second ball releases from the trough.

**Jackpot structure:**
- 2-ball simultaneous play
- Kick-out hole shots = elevated bonus collection
- Tube ramp during multiball = 2× tube bonus
- **End of multiball:** Remaining ball continues single-ball play

This was state-of-the-art in 1980 — treat it as the high-value window it is.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| 4-bank drop targets | Multiplier advance | Medium |
| Right ramp → tube | Tube bonus + safe travel | Low |
| Left kick-out | Bonus × multiplier | Low |
| Right kick-out | Bonus × multiplier | Low |
| Left saucer (lock) | Multiball start | Low |
| Spinning target | Per-rev points | Low |
| Pop bumpers (4) | Passive | None |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Left outlane** — after kick-out eject from right hole; ball travels fast
- **Post-tube exit** — the tube drops the ball into the left lane at speed; be positioned for the catch

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Right ramp (tube): 2–3 passes to start the combo rhythm
2. 4-bank drop targets × 2 completions (2× multiplier)
3. Kick-out collection at 2× bonus
4. Ball-lock attempt → multiball if possible

### Ball 2
1. 4-bank × 3 (3–4× multiplier)
2. Multiball: ramp + kick-out farming
3. Kick-out collection at peak

### Ball 3
1. Max multiplier (5×) via 4-bank cycles
2. Tube ramp during every multiplier window
3. Dual kick-out collection before draining
4. Push toward 9,999,990 cap on strong games

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 300K–1M |
| Solid | 1.5M–4M |
| Strong (5× + MB) | 4M–7M |
| Elite | 7M–9,999,990 |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The tube ramp is the safest repeating shot.** Once you know the right ramp angle, you can loop tube shots for consistent bonus building while waiting for kick-out lights. It's Xenon's "default" shot.

> **Ciani's voice tells you what's happening.** Learn what each call-out means — she announces bonus milestones, the multiball lock, and multiplier completions. It's the most informative audio system in any 1980 machine.

> **2-ball multiball in 1980 is extraordinary.** Don't miss the lock shot when it's available. The elevated bonus windows during multiball push scores significantly.

> **The 9,999,990 cap is real.** On a perfect game with max multiplier and repeated kick-out collections, Xenon can hit the cap. It resets to 0 beyond that — know where you are.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 6.5° — Bally 1980 SS standard |
| Elevated tube | Inspect for cracks and ball path obstructions; damaged tube derails the machine's primary shot |
| Suzanne Ciani voice board | Verify speech synthesis is functional; silence loses the atmosphere completely |
| Infinity backglass lights | Check LED strip continuity (or bulb set if original) |
| Ball-lock saucer | Verify release mechanism for reliable multiball trigger |
