# ♠️ World Poker Tour (Stern, 2006) - Strategy Guide

*Stern Pinball, 2006 — Designed by Steve Ritchie & Keith P. Johnson, Software by Keith P. Johnson & Dwight Sullivan*

> **"All in. The jackpot is the pot. Don't fold before multiball."**

World Poker Tour (WPT, Stern 2006) is a Whitestar machine based on the Travel Channel/WPT TV poker series. Steve Ritchie (High Speed, Star Trek TNG, AC/DC) brings his trademark fast-ramp design philosophy to the poker license: left and right ramps build your poker hand, a card deal mechanism awards random hands, orbits advance the tournament, and WPT Multiball is triggered by locking balls in the poker chip stack. Our goal: **200M–700M** by building strong poker hands, cashing them out via the center scoop, and triggering WPT Championship Multiball.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Shoot the left ramp** repeatedly — it's the primary card dealing/hand building shot
- [ ] **Complete the right ramp** to advance the tournament bracket and unlock cash-out multipliers
- [ ] **Hit the left orbit** to cycle through available card suits and claim community cards
- [ ] **Shoot the center scoop** to cash out your current hand when it's a winning combination
- [ ] **Lock balls in the Poker Chip stack** (right VUK) to progress toward WPT Multiball
- [ ] **Monitor the hand display** — the game shows your current 5-card hand on the DMD at all times

## 🃏 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Card deal** | Time plunge to lit top lane | High card dealt into hand |
| **Left ramp** | Power plunge through left ramp | Suited connector + tournament advance |

### Left Ramp (Card Dealing — Primary Shot)

The left ramp is the primary hand-building shot:
- Each left ramp completion: one card dealt to your 5-card hand
- The card dealt depends on current table conditions (random draw from the displayed deck)
- **Flush/straight building:** Successive left ramp shots in sequence can build toward suited cards
- Left ramp loop combo: 2 consecutive left ramps = "Re-deal" bonus (swap lowest card for a new draw)

### Right Ramp (Tournament Advance)

The right ramp advances the WPT Tournament bracket:
- Each right ramp: one tournament elimination (moves you closer to the final table)
- Reaching the Final Table: unlocks the Championship round and elevates all jackpot values
- Also contributes a card to the community pool (Texas Hold'em style: community cards available to all hands)

### Left Orbit (Community Cards)

The left orbit accesses the community card pool:
- Each left orbit shot: reveals or claims one community card that can be used in your hand
- Community cards are shared — using them strategically completes hands faster than raw deals

### Center Scoop (Cash Out / Hand Evaluation)

The center scoop evaluates and cashes out your current poker hand:
- **Pair:** 5M–15M
- **Two Pair:** 15M–35M
- **Three of a Kind:** 35M–60M
- **Straight:** 60M–100M
- **Flush:** 100M–150M
- **Full House:** 150M–200M
- **Four of a Kind:** 200M–350M
- **Straight Flush / Royal Flush:** 350M–800M+
- After cash-out: hand resets, new deal begins

### Poker Chip Stack (Right VUK — Ball Lock)

The right VUK is adjacent to a poker chip stack display:
- Each VUK shot: adds chips to your stack and locks a ball
- Lock 2 balls → WPT Championship Multiball begins
- The chip stack value carries into multiball as the initial jackpot value

### Ante Up (Standup Targets)

Three standup targets on the left playfield:
- Completing the trio = "Ante Up" award: raises the jackpot base value by 5M
- Repeated Ante Up completions stack before multiball for larger jackpot floors

## 🎳 Multiball

### WPT Championship Multiball (3-ball)

**How to start:** Lock 2 balls in the chip stack VUK → 3-ball multiball. Advancing to the Final Table first raises jackpot values significantly.

**Jackpot structure:**
- 3-ball multiball
- Left ramp = card jackpot (hand value × chip stack multiplier)
- Right ramp = tournament jackpot (tournament position value)
- **Grand Jackpot (All In):** Complete a Royal Flush during multiball via center scoop → 500M–1B+
- Ante Up ante-builds from single-ball carry into multiball base

### Heads Up Mini-Multiball

A brief 2-ball mode triggered by completing all 4 suit lanes (clubs, diamonds, hearts, spades):
- 2-ball with the suited jackpot lit
- Higher value if the active hand is a suited combination (flush or better)

## 🔄 Progression Systems

- **Tournament bracket:** 6 eliminations reach the Final Table; Final Table + multiball = Championship
- **Chip stack:** Carries forward — higher chip stack = higher multiball jackpot floor
- **Hand quality over time:** The game tracks your best hand each ball; end-of-ball bonus is proportional to the best hand achieved

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Left ramp | Card deal + hand build | Low |
| Right ramp | Tournament advance | Low |
| Left orbit | Community card | Low |
| Center scoop (cash out) | Hand value | Low |
| Right VUK (chip lock) | Multiball lock | Low |
| Standup targets (Ante Up) | Jackpot floor raise | Low |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Right outlane** — after left orbit at speed; Steve Ritchie machines often have tight right outlanes
- **Post-VUK eject** — chip lock release ejects at center-left; be ready on both flippers

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Left ramp × 5 (build toward Flush or better)
2. Right ramp × 3 (tournament: 3 eliminations)
3. Center scoop → cash out (Two Pair or better)
4. Ante Up × 2 (jackpot floor build)

### Ball 2
1. Left orbit × 2 (community cards to complete hand)
2. Right ramp × 3 more → Final Table
3. Chip lock × 1 (multiball seed)
4. Best hand cash-out before multiball

### Ball 3
1. Chip lock × 2 → WPT Championship Multiball
2. During multiball: left ramp for Royal Flush build
3. Grand Jackpot attempt (Royal Flush + center scoop)
4. Heads Up mini-multiball if suited

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 30M–100M |
| Solid | 150M–350M |
| Strong (Final Table + MB) | 400M–650M |
| Elite (Grand Jackpot) | 700M+ |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Left ramp is king.** Steve Ritchie's signature is the fast left ramp as the primary scoring loop. WPT is no exception — master the left ramp approach and the game opens up.

> **Cash out at Three of a Kind or better, don't wait for the perfect hand.** The temptation to hold for a Royal Flush during single-ball play almost always ends in a drain before the cash-out. Take the sure thing.

> **Final Table first, then lock balls.** The multiplier difference between pre-Final Table and Championship multiball is substantial. Reach the Final Table in single-ball before triggering multiball whenever possible.

> **Ante Up is underrated.** Three quick standup hits add 5M to the jackpot floor. Do this naturally between ramp cycles — the targets are accessible from the left flipper backhand.
