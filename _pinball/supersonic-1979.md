# ✈️ Supersonic - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1979 — Designed by Greg Kmiec*

> **"Break the sound barrier. Break the score barrier."**

Supersonic (Bally, 1979) is a Concorde-themed solid-state machine featuring a 5-bank drop target set, 4 standup targets, a kick-out hole, a spinner, and a right outlane ball return gate. Score cap is 999,990. The game is built around the Bally SS bonus multiplier loop: complete the drop target bank to advance multiplier, collect via the kick-out hole, repeat. Our goal: **500,000–900,000** through disciplined drop target cycling and kick-out collection at peak multiplier.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Complete the 5-bank drop targets** to advance your bonus multiplier
- [ ] **Collect the kick-out hole** when lit — it pays out accumulated bonus × multiplier
- [ ] **Hit the spinner** on every orbit pass for base bonus building
- [ ] **Hit the 4 standup targets** to light extra ball and feature bonuses
- [ ] **Watch the right outlane** — the ball return gate can rescue a draining ball
- [ ] **Repeat the drop target cycle** — each reset gives you another multiplier step up to 5×

---

## ✈️ Core Mechanics

### Bonus Multiplier (5-Bank Drop Targets)

| Completion | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| 1st completion | 2× |
| 2nd completion | 3× |
| 3rd completion | 4× |
| 4th completion | 5× (max) |

The 5-bank resets after each completion. **Four completions per ball to reach 5×** — achievable with deliberate play.

### Kick-Out Hole

The kick-out hole lights when:
- Specific standup target combinations are hit
- A certain number of drop target completions are reached

When lit, it collects the accumulated end-of-ball bonus mid-ball. **At 5× multiplier, this is the most valuable single action in the game.** Chase the kick-out only at 3× or higher.

### Spinner

Located on an orbit lane, the spinner builds bonus base rapidly. Each spin = bonus unit. 10 fast spins can add significant base value — hit it every pass.

### Standup Targets (4)

Four standup targets:
- Complete all 4 → extra ball lit (collect via kick-out)
- Individual target hits → bonus base + light patterns
- Some configurations: completing specific targets lights the kick-out hole

### Right Outlane Ball Return Gate

The right outlane has a gate that can deflect a draining ball back onto the playfield. It's a geometric feature (not a timed event) — when the ball rolls through the correct lane angle, the gate routes it back. Unlike a kickback, it's always available but requires the ball to approach at the correct angle.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| 5-bank drop targets | Multiplier advance | Low |
| Kick-out hole | Mid-ball bonus collect | Medium |
| Spinner | Bonus base | Low |
| Standup targets | Extra ball + base | Low |
| Pop bumpers (3) | Passive base | None |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Left outlane** — no gate on left side; primary drain risk
- **Post-drop-target rebound** — 5 targets create varied deflection angles; cover both flippers after a bank hit
- **Kick-out eject** — verify your machine's exit angle; most eject toward left or right flipper

---

## 🔄 Progression Systems

- **Multiplier** is the only persistent in-ball progression — resets to 1× on drain
- **Standup target completion** lights extra ball (collectable once per game)
- **Bonus base** accumulates from spinner, pop bumpers, and targets; paid at ball end × multiplier

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Drop targets × 2 (multiplier to 3×)
2. Spinner orbit × 3 passes
3. Kick-out when lit (3× payout)
4. Standup targets (progress toward extra ball)

### Balls 2–3
1. Drop targets to 5× (four completions)
2. Kick-out at 5× — peak collection
3. Standup target completion → extra ball

### Extra Ball
1. Treat it like a full bonus ball — push for 5× multiplier again
2. Second kick-out collection at 5×

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Four drop target completions per ball sounds ambitious — it's not.** The 5-bank resets cleanly and the targets are grouped for efficient hitting. With deliberate aim, you can reach 5× on ball 1.

> **Don't collect the kick-out at 2×.** Wait. A 3× collection is worth 50% more, and 5× is 2.5× the value. Patience before collection is the core skill gap.

> **The right outlane gate is worth learning.** Watch how the ball approaches the right outlane — a ball moving slowly from the right slingshot area often enters the gate angle and returns. Don't panic-flip when you see it heading there slowly.
