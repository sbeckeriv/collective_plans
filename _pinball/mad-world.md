# 🎪 Mad World Strategy Guide
## Bally 1964 | The Original Multiball Pioneer

---

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT NOTE FOR MODERN PINBALL PLAYERS**
>
> Mad World is a **1964 electromechanical (EM) pinball machine** — over 60 years old. Standard EM caveats apply:
> - ❌ No Modes or Wizard Modes
> - ❌ No Stacking or Combos
> - ❌ No Digital Displays
>
> **Maximum possible score: 1,999 points** — classic early-60s scoring where every point is precious.
>
> However, Mad World is remarkable for one extraordinary reason: it features an **optional 2-ball multiball** that the player can choose to activate — in **1964**. This "Free Ball Alley" mechanic predates virtually all other multiball implementations in pinball history. Mad World is not just a novelty; it is a historically significant machine.

### 🎱 Plunge Strategy

- **Medium Plunge**: Routes through center rollover area toward bumpers
- **Hard Plunge**: Sends ball toward top rollovers and Alley gate area

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: The three yellow inserts (2 mushroom bumpers + 1 rollover lane) in mid-playfield control the Free Ball Alley ball advancement. Getting the ball to cycle through these repeatedly is key.

## 🎳 The Free Ball Alley — 1964 Multiball

This is Mad World's defining and historically remarkable feature. Here's how it works:

### Step 1: Opening the Alley Gate

- The A-B-C-D rollovers are in two pairs: **A+C** and **B+D**
- Scoring a lit **A or C rollover** lights the **right outlane Special**
- Scoring a lit **B or D rollover** lights the **left outlane Special**
- The two **designated rollovers** (when lit) open the **Free Ball Gate** on the upper-right

### Step 2: The Ball Enters the Alley

When the ball-in-play hits an open Free Ball Gate rollover:
1. Ball enters the Alley and locks in position 1 of 5
2. Gate closes behind it
3. Designated rollovers no longer light until Alley empties
4. A **new ball** is served from under the apron
5. **"SHOOT AGAIN"** lights on backglass

### Step 3: Advancing the Alley Ball

The locked ball advances one position in the Alley each time your ball-in-play hits **any two of three lit yellow inserts**:

| Yellow Insert | Location       |
| ------------- | -------------- |
| Yellow insert | Mushroom bumper 1 |
| Yellow insert | Mushroom bumper 2 |
| Yellow insert | Mid-playfield rollover lane |

- Hit any 2 of these 3 (in any order) → locked ball advances one position
- All 3 yellow inserts re-light after each advance

### Step 4: Freeing the Ball (Position 5)

When the locked ball reaches position 5 and is hit again:
- Scores **100 points** (significant in a 1,999 max game!)
- Locked ball is delivered to the shooter lane
- **"SHOOT AGAIN"** lights — you can now play 2 balls simultaneously

### Step 5: Player Choice — Multiball or Save

When the freed ball enters the shooter lane:
- **Option A**: Shoot immediately while current ball is still in play → **2-ball multiball**
- **Option B**: Wait until current ball drains → shoot as a free replacement ball

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: The freed ball is a carry-over feature — it persists from ball to ball and player to player until someone frees it from position 5. If you inherit an opponent's partially-advanced Alley ball, you can complete it and claim the 100-point payout.

## 🎮 Multiball Strategy (The "Mad" Part)

When you choose to shoot the freed ball into live play:

| Active Balls | Effect                              |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------- |
| 2 balls live | Double drain risk, double scoring   |
| Both drain   | Ball unit advances normally         |
| One drains while other is live | Only the player who freed the ball controls timing |

**Multiball Approach:**
- With only 1,999 max points, the 100-point Alley payout is enormous — roughly 5–10% of a winning game
- 2-ball multiball is chaotic but can rack up mushroom bumper and pop bumper hits quickly
- The mushroom bumpers are your highest-repeating scorers in multiball

> ⚠️ **Warning**: In multiball on a 60-year-old machine, drain risk doubles. The game's "madness" earned its name. You may end your ball quickly if both drain rapidly.

## 🎯 A-B-C-D Rollovers & Specials

| Rollover | Effect When Lit                        |
| -------- | -------------------------------------- |
| A or C   | Lights **right outlane Special**       |
| B or D   | Lights **left outlane Special**        |

- Special in outlane scores **replay or points** (operator-set)
- Specials last only until end of ball in play
- Completing A+C and B+D in the same ball can light both outlane Specials simultaneously

> 💡 **Note**: The A-B-C-D sequence itself is not a completion target — there is no award for collecting all four. Each pair independently lights its corresponding Special.

## 📈 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1)
1. 🎳 Focus on hitting the mushroom bumpers and yellow inserts to advance the Alley ball
2. 📊 Collect lit rollovers (A/C or B/D) to light outlane Specials
3. 🎰 Work toward opening the Free Ball Gate

### Mid Game
1. 🎳 Keep advancing the Alley ball — carry-over means progress accumulates
2. 💰 When Alley ball reaches position 5, decide: free it immediately or play it safe
3. 🎪 If freed ball available: choose multiball for maximum chaos scoring

### Final Ball
1. 💰 **Priority**: Free the Alley ball if at all possible — 100 pts is enormous
2. 🎪 If using multiball, play aggressively — nothing to save
3. ⚠️ **DO NOT TILT** — game-ending penalty
