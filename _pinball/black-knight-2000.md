# ⚔️ Black Knight 2000 - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1989 — Designed by Steve Ritchie*

> **"Trial by combat!"**

Black Knight 2000 is the legendary split-level sequel to the original Black Knight, built around lowering the drawbridge to access the upper Skyway ramp, a magnetic Magna-Save ball-rescue mechanic, and a brutal RANSOM letter chase that culminates in King's Ransom — a timed wizard mode with everything on the playfield lit. Our goal: spell RANSOM, survive King's Ransom, and stack all three multiball types in a single game.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Hit the three drawbridge drop targets** to lower the drawbridge and open the **Skyway ramp** — your gateway to the upper playfield
- [ ] **Use Magna-Save deliberately** — the side cabinet buttons activate a magnetic ball-save on the upper playfield, but it has limited uses, so save it for genuine emergencies
- [ ] **Lock 3 balls behind the drawbridge / up the Skyway ramp** for the main 3-ball multiball — whoever locks the third ball gets it
- [ ] **Chase RANSOM letters across the whole game** — they're retained between games and are the hardest, highest-value challenge here
- [ ] **Expect right-flipper dominance** — most of the game's key shots favor the right flipper, so prioritize building right-flipper control
- [ ] **Don't panic on the upper playfield** — it's smaller and trickier, but Magna-Save exists specifically to bail you out there

---

## 🏰 Core Mechanics

### Drawbridge & Skyway Ramp

Hit the **three drawbridge drop targets** to lower the drawbridge, opening access to the **Skyway ramp** on the upper playfield — one of the most valuable and central shots in the entire game. Most major progression (locks, RANSOM letters, multiball qualification) routes through this ramp.

### Magna-Save

A **magnetic ball-save mechanism** on the upper playfield, activated by buttons on the cabinet sides. It catches a ball that would otherwise drain on the trickier upper section.

> 🧲 **Pro Tip:** Magna-Save typically has a limited number of charges per ball — don't burn it reflexively on minor close calls. Save it for moments where losing the ball would cost you real progress (mid-multiball, mid-RANSOM chase, etc.).

### RANSOM Letters (The Real Challenge)

Spelling **R-A-N-S-O-M** is widely considered the hardest and most rewarding feature in the game. Letters **carry over between balls** (and even between games on some settings), so progress isn't wasted on a drain. Completing the full word starts **King's Ransom**.

---

## 🎁 Multiball Modes (Three Distinct Types)

### Double Knight's Challenge (2-Ball)

A smaller-scale 2-ball multiball, typically easier to qualify than the full 3-ball version — a good warm-up before chasing the main multiball.

### Regular Multiball (3-Ball)

1. Lock balls **behind the drawbridge and up the Skyway ramp**
2. Lock **3 balls** to start multiball
3. **In multiplayer games, whoever locks the third ball gets the multiball** — the other players who contributed locks get nothing for their effort, so be mindful of lock-stealing dynamics in competitive play

### King's Ransom (3-Ball Timed Wizard Mode)

1. **Requirement:** Spell RANSOM
2. **Gameplay:** A timed mode with **everything on the playfield lit** and an active ball saver throughout
3. This is the actual wizard mode payoff for the game's hardest ongoing challenge — treat it as your main end-game target

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Shot | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Upper playfield (post-drawbridge)** | Smaller, faster, less forgiving than the main playfield | Lean on Magna-Save for genuine emergencies; otherwise play it more conservatively than the lower playfield |
| **Right-flipper dominance** | Most key shots favor the right flipper, leaving the left flipper underused and shot selection imbalanced | Deliberately practice right-flipper precision since you'll rely on it disproportionately |
| **Multiball lock-stealing (multiplayer)** | Locking the 1st or 2nd ball for someone else to "steal" the multiball can feel like wasted effort | Try to be the one to lock the third ball when in a tight multiplayer race for the same multiball |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball (Ball 1)

1. Work the drawbridge drop targets early to open the Skyway ramp
2. Start chasing RANSOM letters wherever convenient — they never go to waste
3. Consider an early Double Knight's Challenge if available, as a lower-risk multiball warm-up

**Target Score:** Build steadily; this is a shot-volume game more than a single big-number game

### Mid Ball (Ball 2)

1. Push toward locking 3 balls for the main multiball — be the one to lock the third ball if you're in a multiplayer race
2. Continue RANSOM letter progress
3. Use Magna-Save sparingly, saving charges for upper-playfield emergencies

### Late Ball (Ball 3+)

1. If RANSOM is spelled, light and run **King's Ransom** — your biggest scoring opportunity in the game
2. Stack any remaining multiball opportunities you haven't used yet
3. Lean on accumulated upper-playfield familiarity for confident Skyway ramp shots under pressure

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 6.5°–7.0° | Standard for late-80s Williams machines |
| **Magna-Save calibration** | Verify magnet strength and timing | A weak or mistimed Magna-Save fails to save balls it should catch |
| **Drawbridge mechanism** | Check for smooth raise/lower action | Central to nearly every major shot in the game — a sticky drawbridge disrupts the whole ruleset |

---

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Ball 1:** Open the drawbridge, start chasing RANSOM letters.
**Ball 2:** Lock toward 3-ball multiball, keep building RANSOM progress.
**Ball 3+:** Run King's Ransom if RANSOM is spelled, stack remaining multiball types.

**Target High-Score Ballpark:** Strong play centers on completing RANSOM and reaching King's Ransom at least once per game — that single achievement defines a great game on this machine far more than any specific point total.
