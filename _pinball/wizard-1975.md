# 🎸 Wizard! (Bally, 1975) - Strategy Guide

*Bally Manufacturing, 1975 — Designed by Greg Kmiec, based on the rock opera "Tommy" by The Who*

> **"See me, feel me, touch me, TILT me."**

Wizard! (Bally, 1975) is an electromechanical machine inspired by The Who's rock opera "Tommy" — Tommy Walker, the "pinball wizard" from the album. The playfield features 8 standup targets, 8 star rollovers, 4 Bally "flip flags" (posts the ball flips over), a kick-out hole, and a spinner. No multiball, no digital displays — 3 chimes + a knocker, relay-driven scoring reels, and a 199,990 point maximum. Our goal: **80,000–199,990** by building the star rollover bonus chain and collecting via the kick-out hole at maximum multiplier.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Hit the star rollovers (8)** to build end-of-ball bonus — each lights a bonus step
- [ ] **Complete the standup targets (8)** for the multiplier advancement
- [ ] **Shoot the kick-out hole** when lit for in-game bonus collection
- [ ] **Trigger the flip flags (4)** — ball-top posts that spin when hit; each awards a bonus step
- [ ] **Spin the spinner** for points during lull periods between target sequences
- [ ] **Watch for 199,990** — the score reels max out and wrap to 0 if you exceed the cap

## 🎮 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Star rollover** | Partial plunge to top rollover | Bonus step advance |
| **Standard** | Full plunge | Ball to pop bumpers |

### Star Rollovers (8)

Eight star-shaped rollover buttons are embedded in the lanes and playfield:
- Each rollover hit: advances the end-of-ball bonus by one step
- 8 stars = maximum bonus for the ball
- Stars are passive — the ball rolls over them naturally during play
- Star rollovers near the top lanes are most efficiently accessed via a controlled plunge

### 8 Standup Targets

Eight standup targets scattered across the playfield:
- Each hit: 500 points
- Completing groups advances the bonus multiplier
- One standup target is recessed (harder to hit) — it's still worth the same points but contributes to a special sequence
- The recessed target is a deliberate challenge mechanic from Greg Kmiec

### Flip Flags (4)

Bally's "flip flag" mechanism: small posts with a plastic flag at ball height:
- Ball strikes the post → the flag spins/flips on its pivot
- Each flip: awards a bonus step (same as a star rollover)
- Flip flags are typically positioned at slingshot-adjacent areas; they trigger passively during aggressive ball deflections

### Kick-Out Hole

The single kick-out hole collects the accumulated bonus:
- When lit by standup target completions: collecting pays current bonus × current multiplier
- Bonus then resets — collect before draining
- Ball ejects back to the mid-playfield area

### Spinner

The spinner in the center lane:
- Per-revolution scoring (EM-style: typically 100 pts/revolution)
- Low value per spin but accessible during lull periods
- Useful when targets are all hit and awaiting the kick-out

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Partial plunge → star rollover (fast bonus start)
2. Standup targets × 5 (multiplier advance)
3. Pop bumpers for more star rollover coverage
4. Kick-out collection when lit

### Ball 2
1. All 8 standup targets (full multiplier)
2. Let stars accumulate via pop bumper passes
3. Kick-out at max multiplier → reset and repeat

### Ball 3
1. All 8 stars active + flip flags triggered
2. Standup targets for second multiplier cycle
3. Kick-out collection at highest achievable value
4. Push toward 199,990 cap

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 10,000–50,000 |
| Solid | 60,000–120,000 |
| Strong (max mult + stars) | 130,000–180,000 |
| Elite (cap chase) | 199,990 |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The rock opera theme is atmosphere, not rules.** The game is pure 1975 EM fundamentals — no Tommy-specific rules, just targets and rollovers. The theme is entirely in the art and the music (chimes).

> **Flip flags are a bonus, not a target.** Don't aim for them — they trigger passively when the ball is near the slingshots. Aggressive slingshot play increases flag activations.

> **The recessed standup target is the last one to complete.** Plan for it: it's harder to hit cleanly and may require a specific flipper angle. Don't ignore it — it contributes to the multiplier sequence.

> **The 199,990 cap is achievable on a good 3-ball game.** With max multiplier and all stars lit, each bonus collection can be 10,000–25,000 points. Three collections gets you close.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 6° — Bally 1975 EM standard |
| Chimes (3) | Verify all three chime bars sound cleanly; flat chime makes scoring feedback poor |
| Knocker | Test (replays and specials); knocker gives tactile confirmation |
| Flip flags | Check all 4 pivot freely; stuck flags miss triggers |
| Score reels | All 6 digits must advance cleanly; sticking reel will give wrong scores |
