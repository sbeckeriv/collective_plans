# 🏎️ Grand Prix Strategy Guide
## Williams 1976 | Race to 999,990

---

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT NOTE FOR MODERN PINBALL PLAYERS**
>
> Grand Prix is a **1976 electromechanical (EM) pinball machine**:
> - ❌ No Modes or Multiballs
> - ❌ No Stacking or Combos
> - ❌ No Digital Displays or DMD
>
> **Maximum possible score: 999,990 points** — unusually high for an EM, giving this machine one of the most expansive scoring ranges of its era.
>
> Grand Prix was designed by Steve Kordek, one of pinball's most prolific designers, and features **two spinning targets** alongside a full complement of drops and kick-out holes.

- **Medium Plunge**: Aims toward the spinning targets for immediate point scoring
- **Hard Plunge**: Sends ball toward drop target bank

## ⚠️ The Tilt Rollover — Critical Hazard

Grand Prix has a **unique tilt rollover** in the upper-left lane. This is not a standard scoring rollover — it triggers a **tilt** and ends your turn:

| Lane          | Effect                    |
| ------------- | ------------------------- |
| Normal lanes  | Points / bonus            |
| Upper-left lane | **TILT — ball lost**   |

> ⚠️ **WARNING**: Avoid the upper-left lane at all costs. A ball entering this lane triggers a tilt, ending your current ball with no bonus collected. This is the most dangerous feature on the playfield.

**How to Avoid It:**
- Never shoot for the upper-left quadrant unless you're certain of ball path
- Gentle nudges are safe — avoid aggressive nudging that could push ball toward upper-left
- Pop bumper exits near the left side carry the highest tilt risk

## 🎯 Drop Targets — 4-Bank

The 4-bank of drop targets is the primary scoring objective:

| Action            | Points   | Effect                    |
| ----------------- | -------- | ------------------------- |
| Single target     | 500 pts  | +1 bonus unit             |
| All 4 down        | Targets reset | Kick-out hole lit    |

**Drop Target Strategy:**
- Clear the bank to light kick-out holes for high-value awards
- Each reset gives another full cycle — repeat for maximum scoring
- Approach from the center flipper to minimize tilt rollover risk

## 🌀 Spinning Targets — Double the Speed

Two spinning targets make Grand Prix's scoring unusually fast for an EM:

| Target              | Points per Rotation | Best Approach      |
| ------------------- | ------------------- | ------------------ |
| Left spinner        | Points per spin     | Right flipper, center |
| Right spinner       | Points per spin     | Left flipper, center  |

**Spinner Strategy:**
- Both spinners reward clean, centered shots — avoid edge hits that slow the spin
- Hit spinners at max ball speed for the most rotations
- Natural ball return from spinners often feeds the pop bumper zone

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: On a well-maintained machine, a hard center shot through a spinner can add 5,000–10,000 points in a single pass. These are the fastest point earners on the playfield.

## 🕳️ Kick-out Holes

Three kick-out holes provide bonus collection and points:

| Kick-out Condition     | Award              |
| ---------------------- | ------------------ |

- Kick-out ejections generally feed back toward the center playfield
- With three holes, the ball is frequently captured during play
- Prioritize hitting holes when they're lit after a bank clear

## 📈 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1–2)
1. 🌀 Hit both spinning targets for fast early points
2. 🎯 Clear the drop target bank
3. 🕳️ Collect lit kick-out holes
4. ⚠️ Learn the tilt lane position — memorize it

### Mid Game
1. 🌀 Keep farming spinners on clean shots
2. 🎯 Repeat drop bank clears → kick-out collection cycle
3. 📈 Build bonus units toward maximum

### Final Ball
1. 📈 Maximize bonus units
2. 🌀 Two more spinner passes for quick points
3. 🕳️ Collect kick-out holes if lit
4. ⚠️ Stay especially cautious of tilt lane under pressure
