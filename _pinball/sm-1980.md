# 🕷️ The Amazing Spider-Man (Gottlieb, 1980) - Strategy Guide

*Gottlieb, 1980 — Widebody Solid State*

> **"With great power comes a great bonus multiplier."**

The Amazing Spider-Man (Gottlieb, 1980) is a Gottlieb System 1 widebody machine featuring the Marvel Comics license. It's built around a classic early-SS framework: drop target banks for bonus advance, kick-out holes for collection, and a widebody layout that rewards deliberate aiming. Our goal: **300,000–600,000** (Gottlieb SS scoring) through multiplier maximization and systematic drop target completion.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Complete the drop target banks** to advance your bonus multiplier
- [ ] **Collect the center kick-out hole** when lit for mid-ball bonus payouts
- [ ] **Roll through the top rollover lanes** to advance the bonus base
- [ ] **Use all four flippers** — the widebody requires upper flipper use for far target access
- [ ] **Avoid premature bonus collection** — wait for 3× or higher multiplier before chasing the kick-out

---

## 🦸 Core Mechanics

### Bonus Multiplier (Drop Targets)

The drop target banks are the multiplier engine. Each completed bank advances the multiplier:

| Completions | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| 1 | 2× |
| 2 | 3× |
| 3 | 4× |
| 4+ | 5× (max) |

Target banks reset after each completion.

### Center Kick-Out Hole

The central kick-out is your mid-ball bonus collector. When lit (via drop target completions or rollover sequences), shooting it:
- Pays out accumulated bonus × current multiplier
- Ejects the ball back to a flipper
- Immediately re-arms for another cycle

### Top Rollover Lanes

Rolling through the top lanes advances the bonus base. On a widebody, the top lanes are accessed via the upper flippers — routine upper-flipper play builds base passively.

### Drop Target Banks

Multiple banks spread across the widebody playfield:
- Each bank completion: multiplier step + feature advance
- Completing multiple banks per ball is the primary strategy
- Banks reset independently — complete one, then move to the next

### Widebody Layout

The extra playfield width means:
- Far targets require upper flipper shots
- Ball speed is slower across the wider playfield — more time to react
- Left and right outlanes are farther from the slingshots — marginally safer than standard-width machines

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Drop target banks | Multiplier advance | Low |
| Center kick-out | Bonus collection | Medium |
| Top rollover lanes | Base advance | Low |
| Pop bumpers | Passive | None |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Between upper and lower flipper pairs** — widebody gap is larger; a slow ball can thread between them
- **Right outlane** — far from slingshot but still the primary drain path

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Upper flippers → top rollovers (base building)
2. Drop target bank × 2 completions (3× multiplier)
3. Kick-out collection at 3×
4. Second bank cycle if time permits

### Balls 2–3
1. Drop targets to 5× multiplier (4 completions)
2. Kick-out at 5× — peak collection
3. Repeat cycle

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 50K–150K |
| Solid | 200K–400K |
| Strong | 400K–600K |
| Elite | 600K+ |

---

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **5× multiplier is the entire game.** Everything else is setup. Get to 5× as fast as possible (4 bank completions) and then collect.

> **Upper flippers are mandatory on a widebody.** Players who ignore them can't reach far targets and cap out at 2× multiplier per ball. Use them every ball.
