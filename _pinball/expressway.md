# 🛣️ Expressway - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1971 — Designed by Ted Zale*

> **"Spell the whole word. Earn the replay."**

Expressway is a clean, classic EM built around six mushroom bumpers, three pop bumpers, and an up-post between the flippers for extended ball control — all in service of one big goal: spelling the entire word E-X-P-R-E-S-S-W-A-Y to light Special. Our goal: complete the full spelling for a Replay, and push bonus to its 10,000-point maximum.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Work toward spelling the full word E-X-P-R-E-S-S-W-A-Y** — completing it lights **Special**, worth **1 Replay**
- [ ] **Use the up-post between the flippers** for extended ball control — a genuine advancement for its era
- [ ] **Use the left-side kickback lane** to save balls that would otherwise drain
- [ ] **Chain bonus by aligning shots to hit the swinging targets immediately after a bumper ricochet** — this is the core scoring technique on this machine
- [ ] **Push your bonus toward the 10,000-point maximum** — achievable, but genuinely requires skill and practice

## 🛤️ Core Mechanics

### Spelling EXPRESSWAY

The entire game revolves around spelling out the machine's name across its various lanes, targets, and rollover buttons. **Completing the full word lights Special**, which scores **1 Replay** — the single biggest achievement available on this machine.

### Mushroom & Pop Bumpers (Bonus Chain Technique)

With **6 mushroom bumpers** and **3 pop bumpers** on the playfield, the core scoring technique is chaining: **align your shots to hit the swinging targets immediately after the ball ricochets off a lit bumper** for bonus chain scoring. This requires real practice to execute consistently but is the primary path to a high bonus total.

### Ball Control Features

- **Up-post between the flippers**: a genuine innovation for 1971, allowing extended ball control and reducing center drains
- **Left-side kickback lane**: saves balls that would otherwise exit down the left side

### Bonus System

Bonus can be advanced up to a **maximum of 10,000 points** — but veteran players note this genuinely requires skill and practiced shot-chaining technique to reach, not just incidental play.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Forcing bumper-chain shots before you're ready** | Mistimed attempts at the swinging-target chain can lead to careless drains | Practice the timing in isolation before relying on it for serious scoring runs |
| **Standard EM-era bumper unpredictability** | Pop and mushroom bumpers can send the ball in unexpected directions | Treat incidental bumper time as passive scoring, not something to force |
| **Relying too heavily on the kickback** | It helps, but isn't a guaranteed save in every situation | Play controlled shots rather than assuming the kickback will bail you out |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball

1. Start working toward spelling EXPRESSWAY via available lanes and targets
2. Get comfortable with the up-post's positioning for ball control
3. Begin practicing the bumper-ricochet-to-swinging-target chain technique

### Mid Ball

1. Continue building toward a complete EXPRESSWAY spelling
2. Chain bonus shots whenever you have a clean ricochet opportunity
3. Use the kickback lane to extend ball life when needed

### Late Ball

1. Push for the full EXPRESSWAY completion and Special if not already lit
2. Maximize bonus chaining for a push toward the 10,000-point cap
3. Bank whatever bonus and replay value you've built
