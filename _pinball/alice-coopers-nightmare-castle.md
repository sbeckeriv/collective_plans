# 🧟 Alice Cooper's Nightmare Castle - Strategy Guide

*Spooky Pinball, 2018 — Designed by Charlie Emery*

> **"Welcome to my nightmare."**

Nightmare Castle sends you climbing a grid-shaped castle to battle eight monsters, one mode at a time, while two separate multiballs (Crypt and Frank) run alongside them. There's no clock pressure within a mode — they only end when you drain — so this is a game about committing fully to one battle and finishing it. Our goal: defeat as many monsters as possible per game and reach the **Billion Dollar Baby** wizard mode.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Take the skill shot** — plunge into a ramp, orbit, or the crypt lock without touching anything else first, for 2M+ and a lit Secret Passage / outlane ball save
- [ ] **Pick your mode before starting it** — hit the center targets to cycle which monster is "lit," since modes aren't timed once started
- [ ] **Favor the easier modes early**: Insect, Werewolf, Pit Creature, and Alice are the most approachable
- [ ] **Commit to finishing a mode once started** — modes only end on drain, and completion rewards a 25-second ball save plus a big victory-lap hurry-up
- [ ] **Don't walk away mid-ball** — the center magnet will fling drained balls back into play like a pinball GoldenEye save, so stay ready
- [ ] **Stack Crypt Multiball with anything** — it never gets harder and pairs with every mode

## 🏰 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

Plunge into a ramp, orbit, or the crypt lock **without hitting any other switch first**. Reward: **2M+ points**, plus it lights the Secret Passage and an outlane ball save.

### Starting a Monster Battle

Three ways in:
1. Hit the lit orbits 2–3 times
2. Hit the center 3-bank targets three times to light the **Secret Passage** (right of the left ramp), then shoot it
3. The first **Mystery scoop** award can start a mode automatically

**Change which monster is lit** by hitting the center targets again before you commit to starting.

> ⚠️ **Modes are not timed — they only end when you drain.** This means there's no rush, but also no safety net: pick a mode you can actually finish.

### Room Travel

You climb a grid-based castle layout via the orbits (which move you diagonally) and other shots. Progression slows after defeating 3 monsters, and after 4 total you move only one room at a time — later monsters take real commitment to reach.

## 👹 The Eight Monsters

| Monster | How to Defeat |
|---|---|
| **Werewolf** | Hit a lit (green) shot to load, then the center targets to fire. Previously-hit (red) shots damage you instead — avoid repeating them. Survive ~5 hits max. |
| **Alice** | Shoot past the guillotine on the upper playfield three times — the third capture triggers the "head chopped off" theatrics |
| **Insect** | Hit 10 random lit shots/targets |
| **Pit Creature** | Find which of the Mystery scoop / center scoop / Crypt is "hiding" the creature — timer only counts down while the ball is hidden from view |
| **Zombie** | Hit the roaming hurry-up shot (moves across orbits, ramps, center scoop, inner orbit) 5 times before the timer runs out |
| **Cold Ethyl** | 2-ball multiball: drop the ball into the Crypt via the rightmost lane, then shoot the lower drop target or rightmost lane again to release, then keep hitting drop targets/scoop for jackpots |
| **Igor** | Complete progressively larger sets of lit shots (3, then 4, then 5) |
| **Vampire** | Hit the back target on the upper playfield 4 times to stake/dismember |

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Insect, Werewolf, Pit Creature, and Alice are the most reliably completable modes for newer players — lead with these to bank monster-defeat progress before attempting the harder ones (Zombie, Igor, Vampire, Ethyl).

### Frenzy & Victory Lap

Defeating a monster triggers a **~25-second Frenzy**: unlimited ball save and heavily-lit scoring. This rolls into a **victory lap hurry-up** at the left target (above the scoop) — its starting value is **all points scored during Frenzy**, with a minimum base of **5M × monsters defeated so far**. Each subsequent monster battle also scores **20% higher** than the last, so later monsters are worth progressively more even before the hurry-up bonus.

## 🎁 Multiballs

### Crypt Multiball

1. Lock a ball in the Crypt (shoot it directly, or let it drain off the upper-playfield flipper into the lock)
2. Lock a **second ball** to start Crypt Multiball
3. Shoot the three scoops for jackpots
4. **Stacks with everything** — modes, Frank Multiball, anything — and never gets harder to re-trigger

> 🎲 **Note:** You can steal another player's Crypt lock in multiplayer games — keep an eye on lock ownership.

### Frank (Frankenstein) Multiball

1. Lock a ball on the right ramp
2. Shoot left or right ramp to reach the upper playfield
3. Shoot behind the **Lab Lock drop target** (2-bank) on the upper playfield to start
4. Shoot ramps for jackpots; after enough jackpots, the **Super Jackpot** lights at the guillotine
5. **Cannot be stacked with a monster mode** — but can run alongside Crypt Multiball

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Shot | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Steep ramp shots** | Both ramps are notoriously difficult to hit cleanly and consistently | Practice controlled, full-power flipper shots rather than weak taps that stall on the ramp |
| **Right orbit** | Frequently catches the right slingshot instead of feeding cleanly | Aim slightly left of the obvious line to avoid the sling deflection |
| **Left scoop kickout** | No visual warning of where it will eject the ball | Stay ready on the flippers any time the ball goes into the left scoop |
| **Walking away after a drain** | The center magnet can fling a drained ball back into play unexpectedly | Stay at the ready until the ball is fully settled or the next ball has launched |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1)

1. Take the skill shot for the ball save and Secret Passage light
2. Start with an easy mode (Insect, Pit Creature, Alice, or Werewolf) and commit to finishing it
3. Begin working toward Crypt Multiball locks opportunistically — it pairs with everything

**Target Score:** 10M–30M

### Mid Game (Ball 2)

1. Stack Crypt Multiball with whatever mode you're running for extra scoring
2. Push toward Frank Multiball if you have a clean shot at the upper playfield
3. Bank multiple monster victories — each is worth 20% more than the last

**Target Score:** 30M–80M

### Late Game (Ball 3+)

1. Go for the harder remaining monsters (Igor, Vampire, Zombie, Ethyl) now that you understand the shot patterns
2. Stack Crypt Multiball into every mode you run
3. If you've defeated most monsters, push for the final one to unlock **Billion Dollar Baby**

**Target Score per ball:** 40M–150M+

---

## 🏆 Wizard Mode: Billion Dollar Baby

**Requirements:** Defeat all 8 monsters.

This is the ultimate goal of the game — full rules vary by code version, but reaching it requires methodically working through the entire monster roster across a full game, banking Frenzy/victory-lap bonuses along the way.

> 🎯 **Competitive note:** Because modes only end on drain, your path to Billion Dollar Baby is really a path of "never give up mid-mode" — every drain you avoid while a monster battle is active is progress toward the final wizard mode.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 6.5°–6.8° | Spooky factory standard; affects ramp feed consistency |
| **Center magnet calibration** | Verify it's working correctly | Critical for the ball-save catch mechanic — a miscalibrated magnet changes risk on every drain |
| **Flipper strength** | Factory default | Steep ramps already demand precision; don't over-power |

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Ball 1:** Skill shot, easy mode, start Crypt locks.
**Ball 2:** Stack Crypt Multiball into modes, attempt Frank Multiball.
**Ball 3+:** Tackle harder monsters, chase full monster completion for Billion Dollar Baby.

**Target High-Score Ballpark:** **150–300 million points** is a strong, competitive game; completing all 8 monsters and reaching Billion Dollar Baby is the true measure of mastery.
