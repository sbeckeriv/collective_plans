# ⚔️ Galahad (Bally, 1970) - Strategy Guide

*Bally Manufacturing, 1970 — Designed by Ted Zale — Electromechanical*

> **"Two kick-out holes, four bumpers, and a gate. Keep it simple, keep it on the flipper."**

Galahad (Bally, 1970) is a two-player electromechanical machine themed around Arthurian legend — Sir Galahad's quest, rendered in the clean, colorful art of Christian Marche. As an EM machine, it has no software: all scoring and logic is handled by relay switches and mechanical reels. The playfield features four pop bumpers, two kick-out holes, three standup targets, a mini-post ball-return gate, and an up-post between the flippers. Our goal: **maximize bonus through kick-out cycling and bumper activity** within the score reel cap.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Direct the ball to the kick-out holes** (upper playfield) — primary scoring locations
- [ ] **Let the pop bumpers work** — four bumpers accumulate meaningful EM points
- [ ] **Hit the standup targets** for bonus advances
- [ ] **Use the up-post** (between flippers) to return center-drain balls to play
- [ ] **Watch the mini-post gate** — it can return outlane-bound balls

## ⚙️ Core Mechanics (EM)

### EM Scoring Conventions

Galahad is a 1970 electromechanical machine:
- Score reels display 5 or 6 digits; maximum score is capped at the reel limit
- No digital display — score reels click mechanically
- No multiball, no modes, no software logic
- All "bonuses" are end-of-ball relay-triggered payouts
- Chime unit provides audio feedback (3-tone + knocker)

### Kick-Out Holes (2)

The two kick-out holes are the primary scoring targets:
- Each kick-out: fixed point award + possible bonus relay trigger
- The ball is physically ejected back to the upper playfield for continued play
- Repeatedly feeding the kick-out holes is the core scoring loop on this machine

### Pop Bumpers (4)

Four pop bumpers in the upper/mid playfield:
- Each bumper activation: fixed EM point award
- Four bumpers = dense scoring zone; balls that enter the bumper cluster can score multiple activations per pass
- Let the ball bounce naturally through the bumpers rather than forcing it to the flippers immediately

### Standup Targets (3)

Three standup targets:
- Each target: point award + advances the end-of-ball bonus
- Completing all three targets may trigger a relay-based bonus multiplier (machine-specific configuration)

### Up-Post (Between Flippers)

An up-post rises mechanically between the two flippers:
- Activated by specific scoring events (hitting targets or bumpers past a threshold)
- When raised: deflects center-drain balls back up the playfield
- Cannot be player-controlled — it activates on relay triggers

### Mini-Post Ball Return Gate

A small gate on the outlane feeds the ball back toward the inlane:
- Passive — no player control
- Occasionally saves balls that would otherwise drain to the outlane

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Kick-out holes | High — primary target | Low |
| Pop bumpers | Medium — cumulative | Low (passive) |
| Standup targets | Medium — bonus advance | Low |
| Flippers (ball control) | N/A | Moderate — two flippers only |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Outlanes** — Bally 1970 cabinets run at steep pitch; outlane drains are common
- **Center drain** — the up-post helps but activates only conditionally; don't rely on it
- **Stuck kick-out** — on an older EM, a weak kick-out coil may not eject the ball cleanly; this is a maintenance issue

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### General Approach

1. On launch: direct the ball toward the kick-out holes in the upper playfield
2. Allow bumper cluster activity naturally on each pass
3. Hit standup targets when accessible from the flippers
4. Preserve ball control at the flippers — two-flipper machines from this era reward patient, controlled play over aggressive shooting

### Score Targets

| Performance | Notes |

| Beginner | Getting through 3 balls without tilt |
| Solid | Consistent kick-out feeds + 2+ target completions per ball |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Kick-out holes first, always.** On a 1970 Bally EM, the kick-out holes are the highest-value repeating shot. Every ball should be directed upfield toward them as the primary goal.

> **Don't fight the bumpers.** Let the ball bounce through the four-bumper cluster rather than catching it immediately. Each activation scores, and the bumper cluster can chain multiple hits per pass.

> **The up-post is a bonus, not a safety net.** It activates on relay triggers you can't fully control. Play as if it doesn't exist — it's a pleasant surprise when it fires, not a reliable save.

> **EM maintenance matters.** A Galahad from 1970 that plays well has had its relay contacts cleaned and coils rebuilt. On a poorly maintained machine, kick-out coils and bumper coils may be weak. Ask the location operator if the machine is regularly serviced.

> **Two-player only.** Galahad is a 2-player machine — score reels and relay logic are designed for two players. In a single-player game, only player 1 scoring is active.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 6.5°–7° — EM machines often play better at slightly steeper pitch |
| Kick-out coils | Verify both coils eject the ball cleanly to the upper playfield |
| Pop bumper coils | Check ring switch gap; weak bumpers won't ring on light hits |
| Up-post | Verify relay trigger fires correctly; a stuck up-post can block center drain entirely |
| Tilt | EM tilt mechanisms use a plumb bob; verify it's centered and sensitive appropriately |
| Score reels | Clean and test all reels spin freely; stuck reels give false scores |
