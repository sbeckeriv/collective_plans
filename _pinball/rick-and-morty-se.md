# 🛸 Rick and Morty - Strategy Guide

*Spooky Pinball, 2019 — Designed by Scott Danesi, Software by Eric Priepke & Bowen Kerins*

> **"Nobody exists on purpose. Nobody belongs anywhere. Everyone's gonna die. Now let's go score a billion."**

Rick and Morty (Spooky, 2019) is a deeply creative Spooky Pinball machine with 3 flippers, a captive ball Flooble Crank, a right-side magnet flipper, illuminating drop targets, and an absurdly deep ruleset that rewards players who explore across 40+ dimensions (episodes). Each episode is a timed mode with unique objectives. Our goal: **500M–2B** by stacking episodes, completing seasons, and triggering Drunk Rick multiball at peak episode values.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Hit the Flooble Crank** (captive ball along the right wireform) every pass — it's low risk, scores instantly, and advances Cronenberg progression
- [ ] **Shoot the scoop** to start an episode (dimension/mode)
- [ ] **Complete episode objectives** to finish the dimension and earn season progress
- [ ] **Use the right magnet button** (cabinet side button) to redirect draining balls from the right inlane
- [ ] **Complete Season 1** (4 episodes) to unlock Drunk Rick Multiball
- [ ] **Don't waste ball saves** — Rick will offer to save your ball but at a score cost; always accept early in the game

## 🌀 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Flooble skill shot** | Short plunge to hit the Flooble Crank end | Points + episode start credit |
| **Full plunge** | Standard plunge to horseshoe | Ball into play, episode selection begins |

### Episode Selection (Dimensions)

Shoot the center scoop to start a randomly assigned episode (dimension). Episodes are numbered like TV show seasons and episodes. Each episode has:
- A unique set of 2–5 objectives (specific shots, targets, timing)
- A time limit (varies by episode)
- A completion reward (scores + season progress)

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Some episodes have much simpler objectives than others. The game doesn't guarantee you good episodes — adapt your strategy to whatever you're dealt.

### Flooble Crank (Captive Ball)

The Flooble Crank is a captive ball on a long wireform on the right side:
- Hit the near end of the wireform → transfers momentum → captive ball trips the switch → instant score
- Very low risk, scores on every pass
- Advances **Cronenberg** mode requirements (the side progression)

### Right-Side Magnet (Third Flipper Button)

A magnet sits above the right inlane. Press the right cabinet button to activate it — it can rescue a draining ball from the right inlane and kick it back onto the playfield. **This is essentially a ball save on demand** (limited uses per ball; replenished by episode completions).

### Horseshoe Lane

The horseshoe at the top routes the ball in different ways based on the center up-post position:
- Post **up:** Ball exits same side it entered
- Post **down:** Ball crosses to opposite flipper
- The game controls the post as part of episode objectives — learn to anticipate which exit you'll get

### Drop Targets (Illuminating)

Two banks of illuminating drop targets light up in specific patterns depending on the active episode. Completing the lit pattern is often an episode objective. They reset after each completion.

## 🎳 Multiball

### Drunk Rick Multiball

**How to start:** Complete Season 1 (finish 4 specific episodes). Drunk Rick locks appear afterward — lock balls to start.

**Jackpot structure:**
- 3-ball multiball
- Jackpots via scoop and major shots, escalating per completion
- **Super Jackpot:** Complete a mini episode objective during Drunk Rick — the super jackpot reflects the episode's base value × multiball multiplier
- Drunk Rick's commentary provides audio cues for jackpot windows

> 🏆 **Peak stack:** Have a high-value episode running when Drunk Rick starts. The episode objectives during multiball award both episode progress AND Drunk Rick jackpots.

### Other Multiballs

Later seasons unlock additional multiballs with unique mechanics (Pickle Rick, Council of Ricks). Rules evolve with firmware updates — check the current code version.

---

## 🔄 Progression Systems

- **Seasons:** Every 4 completed episodes = 1 season. Each season unlocks new features and raises jackpot ceilings.
- **Cronenberg:** Flooble Crank hits accumulate separately — completing the Cronenberg sequence awards major bonuses and a unique mode.
- **Rick's Quotes:** Some modes trigger based on specific in-game events (not just shots); listen to Rick's callouts for cues.

## 🔀 Stacking & Combos

| Stack | Effect |
|---|---|
| Active episode + Drunk Rick | Episode objectives count toward both episode completion AND jackpot collection |
| Magnet save + high-value moment | Preserving a ball during a major jackpot opportunity can swing 100M+ |
| Flooble Crank during episode | Advances Cronenberg independently while you pursue episode objectives |

## 🧙 Wizard Modes

### Season Finale Modes

Completing each full season unlocks a "season finale" — a timed frenzy with all shots lit at 5× values. The finale for Season 2+ involves increasingly chaotic shot patterns.

### Total Rickall (Later Wizard Mode)

Complete all seasons across multiple games (or in one exceptional game) to access Total Rickall — the machine's ultimate mode with enormous jackpot potential.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Flooble Crank (captive) | Consistent + Cronenberg | Very low |
| Center scoop | Episode start/advance | Low |
| Left ramp | Episode objectives | Low |
| Drop targets | Episode objectives | Low |
| Right orbit/horseshoe | Episode objectives | Medium (horseshoe unpredictability) |
| Magnet save | Ball preservation | Skill-based |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Right inlane** — magnet save helps here, but timing matters
- **Post-horseshoe exits** — the up-post direction can send the ball to unexpected positions; watch the post state before shooting

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Always accept Rick's ball save offer early in the game.** The score cost is small compared to the ball time you'd lose. Later in ball 3, evaluate — but balls 1 and 2, always say yes.

> **The magnet is a flipper you earned.** Practice activating it at the right moment — it's not automatic. The timing window is about 0.3 seconds before the ball hits the outlane post.

> **Adapt to your episodes.** Some episodes ask for drop target patterns; others want ramp combos. Rick and Morty rewards flexible play, not rote shot repetition.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 6.5° — Spooky standard |

| Horseshoe post | Verify consistent up/down switching; stuck post breaks episode routing |
