# 🔔 Freedom - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1976 — American Bicentennial Theme*

> **"Power up the spinners. Spin the wheel. Double your bonus."**

Freedom is a clean Bicentennial-themed EM built around a 5-bank drop target, two powered-up spinners, and a luck-influenced spinwheel mechanism at the saucers and bottom outlanes. Our goal: clear the drop target bank for extra balls and replays, power up the spinners for 1,000-per-spin scoring, and double your bonus before the ball drains.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Knock down all 5 drop targets** for **1 extra ball**; knocking them down again awards **1 replay** each additional time
- [ ] **Light the spinners for 1,000 points per spin** — a huge upgrade over their unlit value
- [ ] **When spinners are lit, the bottom bumper also lights for 1,000 points** — a nice bonus tied to the same state
- [ ] **Hit targets to advance the bonus light**, with drop targets independently worth 100 points each
- [ ] **Use the Double Bonus feature** to double your accumulated bonus (max 19,000) before the ball ends
- [ ] **Don't over-rely on the saucers/outlanes wheel** — it spins somewhat randomly, so treat its awards as a bonus rather than a core strategy

---

## 🔔 Core Mechanics

### 5-Bank Drop Targets

- **Knock down all 5** for **1 extra ball**
- **Each additional time** you clear the bank, you earn **1 replay**
- Individual drop target hits also score **100 points** each, independent of the clear bonus

### Spinners (Power-Up Mechanic)

| State | Value |
|---|---|
| Unlit | Base value |
| **Lit** | **1,000 points per spin** |

When spinners are lit, the **bottom bumper also lights for 1,000 points** — meaning a single power-up state boosts two different shots simultaneously.

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Veteran players specifically call out the lit spinners as "really satisfying to hit" — once you've powered them up, prioritize spinner shots over almost anything else on the playfield.

### The Spinwheel (Saucers & Outlanes)

**Two saucers and the two bottom outlanes** all score via a **wheel value** mechanism. Be aware: this wheel **spins somewhat randomly** for its awards, which some players note makes tournament play here feel more luck-based than skill-based. Don't structure your strategy heavily around predicting wheel outcomes.

### Bonus System

- **Bonus light** advances from target hits across the playfield
- **Maximum bonus collection: 19,000**
- **Double Bonus** feature doubles your accumulated bonus — a meaningful boost worth activating before the ball drains

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Pop bumper unpredictability** | Standard mid-70s bumper chaos (3, or 4 on early production units) | Treat as incidental scoring, not something to chase |
| **Relying on the wheel for consistent scoring** | Its randomness means you can't count on specific outcomes | Treat wheel awards as a pleasant bonus, not your primary plan |
| **Drop target rebounds** | Standard EM-era unpredictability | Approach with controlled, deliberate shots |

---

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball

1. Start working the 5-bank drop targets toward your first clear (extra ball)
2. Build toward lighting the spinners
3. Pick up bonus light advances incidentally as you play

### Mid Ball

1. Once spinners are lit, prioritize them and the bottom bumper for steady 1,000-point scoring
2. Re-clear the drop target bank if reset, for additional replay value
3. Keep building toward your bonus maximum

### Late Ball

1. Activate Double Bonus before the ball ends to maximize your final score
2. Take advantage of any wheel spins from saucers/outlanes as incidental bonus
3. Bank your doubled bonus (up to 38,000 with Double Bonus applied)

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Early:** Clear the drop target bank, work toward lighting the spinners.
**Mid:** Grind lit spinners and the bottom bumper, re-clear targets if reset.
**Late:** Activate Double Bonus, bank your maximized score.

**Target Outcome:** Clearing the drop target bank at least once for an extra ball, with lit spinners providing steady scoring and a successful Double Bonus activation, represents a strong, well-played game on this Bicentennial-era classic.
