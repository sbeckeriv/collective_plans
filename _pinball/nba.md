# 🏀 NBA - Strategy Guide

*Stern Pinball, 2009 — Designed by Gary Stern, Ray Tanzer & John Borg*

> **"Sink the basket. Build the MVP."**

NBA launched during the 2009 playoffs with 20 licensed players across 20 teams, built around a genuinely creative basket-shot mechanism — a basketball-shaped spinning disc, a trajectory up-kicker that sends the ball airborne toward the hoop, and a "ramp-on-a-ramp" that elevates for a fast aerial shot. Our goal: work the MVP drop targets, complete basket shots through multiple distinct routes, and build toward multiball.

## 🏀 Core Mechanics

### Basket Shot Routes (The Game's Signature Feature)

| Mechanism | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Magnet-assisted ramp** | A metal ramp leads to the basketball goal; a magnet catches the ball and drops it into the basket |
| **Trajectory up-kicker** | Sends the ball airborne directly toward the hoop |
| **Ramp-on-a-ramp** | Elevates to send a fast ball airborne toward the hoop |

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Since this machine offers multiple distinct routes to score on the same basket, learning all of them gives you flexibility — if one route isn't lined up well from your current ball position, another might be.

### MVP Drop Targets

A 3-bank of drop targets themed around earning MVP status — a recurring objective alongside basket shots.

### Spinning Disc & Captive Ball

The **basketball-shaped spinning disc** at the center of the playfield and the **captive ball** both contribute to the game's basketball theme and provide secondary scoring opportunities as part of your normal shot rotation.

### Multiball

The game supports both **2-ball and 3-ball multiball**, built up through sustained basket-shot and target progress.
