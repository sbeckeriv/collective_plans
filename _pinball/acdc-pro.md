# ⚡ AC/DC Pinball Machine - Strategy Guide

*Stern Pinball, 2012 — Designed by Steve Ritchie*

> **"For those about to rock... we salute you."**

AC/DC is a song-selection deep-rules machine: instead of a fixed mode list, you pick from twelve AC/DC tracks, each tied to a specific shot, and build toward three independent multiballs (Jam, Album, Tour) while a swinging bell builds a dangerous-but-lucrative playfield multiplier. Our goal: **50–100 million points** as a strong target, with elite players pushing past 200M by stacking multiballs and milking song jackpots.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Take the Advanced Skill Shot** if you can hold the right flipper off the plunge — bigger payout and two VIP passes instead of one
- [ ] **Pick a song and stick with its key shot** until you naturally rotate — don't randomly chase every insert
- [ ] **Avoid the bell unless you mean it** — it's the single most dangerous shot on the table
- [ ] **Light Jam Multiball first** — it's the easiest of the three to start
- [ ] **Never let your ball drain mid-song** — you lose the entire accumulated song jackpot
- [ ] **Use VIP passes during multiball** for free Add-A-Ball spots on the five major shots

## 🎸 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shots (Plunge)

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | Let the ball kick out to the AXE lanes, flip to select the blinking lane | Points + bonus multiplier + 1 VIP pass |
| **Advanced** | Hold the right flipper as the ball exits the scoop, ride it to the flippers, make any major shot | Bigger points + bonus multiplier + 2 VIP passes |

**VIP passes** are banked and spent automatically as free Add-A-Ball spots once you're in multiball — collect as many as possible before you need them.

### Song Selection System

Twelve songs are selectable via the jukebox, each tied to one key shot. The core loop on every song: **"shoot the key shot, then shoot other stuff, repeat."**

| Song | Key Shot |
|---|---|
| For Those About To Rock | FIRE lanes |
| Back In Black | AC/DC targets |
| War Machine | Left spinner |
| Rock N Roll Train | Left ramp |
| Hells Bells | Bell shot |
| Thunderstruck | Ramp targets |
| TNT | TNT targets |
| Highway to Hell | Right ramp |
| Hell Ain't A Bad Place To Be | Right loop |
| Let There Be Rock | ROCK targets |
| Whole Lotta Rosie | AXE lanes |
| You Shook Me All Night Long | Bumpers |

**Songs carry over indefinitely between balls** until you change them — there's no rush to finish a song before draining (other than the jackpot-loss risk below).

### Changing Songs

Light 8 of the non-current songs on the jukebox to enable a song change via the right loop/saucer shot. You can also choose to "stay," which resets the jukebox progress. **Completing all twelve songs unlocks the Encore wizard mode.**

### Song Jackpot

- **Base value:** 1,000,000+ points, growing with everything you score while that song is active
- **Collection:** Spell **F-I-R-E** on the inlanes (three letters at a time) to light the cannon, then press the lockdown bar "fire" button at the flashing AC/DC bank target
- ⚠️ **Drain your ball and you lose the entire accumulated song jackpot** — this is the single biggest risk/reward decision in the game: bank it early and often, or let it ride for a monster payout.

### Bell Multiplier (Playfield X)

| Bell Hits | Result |
|---|---|
| 3 hits | 2X playfield for 20 seconds |
| 3 more hits while at 2X | 3X playfield for 20 seconds |
| Any bell hit | Resets the 20-second timer |

> 🔔 **Pro Tip:** The bell is the highest risk/reward shot on the table — a successful hit is still likely to send the ball toward the outlanes. Only take it when you're in rhythm and can afford the drain risk; never force it under pressure.

## 🎶 Multiballs

### Jam Multiball (Ramps)

- **Qualify:** Hit ramp shots (15+ available across all songs)
- **Ball count:** 2-ball
- **Jackpots:** Named jackpots per ramp shot
- **Note:** Always the easiest multiball to light — your default first target

### Album Multiball (Target Banks)

- **Qualify:** Complete all 3 target banks
- **Ball count:** 3-ball
- **Jackpots:** Albums collected in chronological order; 14 jackpots to Super
- **Bonus:** Matching your current song to its correct album era grants a bonus equal to the song jackpot value — pick your song before starting Album multiball if you can

### Tour Multiball (Loops)

- **Qualify:** Loop shots
- **Ball count:** 2-ball
- **Jackpots:** Tours collected in chronological order; 20 jackpots to Super
- **Note:** Hardest to build (most jackpots needed) — start it but don't expect to finish solo

### Starting Multiball

Once a multiball is lit, start it with a **right ramp shot** — no locks required, just shoot when the insert flashes. You cannot progress toward a different multiball while one is running.

### Add-A-Ball

During any multiball, hitting **all five major shots** (two ramps, two loops, the bell) feeds an extra ball — burning a banked VIP pass auto-completes one of these for free.

### Stacking Multiballs

Light more than one multiball before taking the right ramp shot to combine them:

| Stack | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Double** (2 multiballs) | Jackpots doubled; two active jackpot types simultaneously |
| **Triple** (3 multiballs) | Jackpots tripled; cannon Super Jackpot value multiplied accordingly |

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Stacking is the single biggest scoring lever in the game. Even a modest Triple Multiball stack out-scores hours of careful single-multiball play.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Shot | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Bell shot** | Even a clean hit often deflects toward the outlanes | Only attempt when you can live-catch or have flipper control to spare |
| **Right ramp (under pressure)** | Steep angle favors right outlane drains on a miss | Backhand it from the left flipper instead of cross-shooting |
| **Forcing the cannon fire window** | Chasing FIRE letters under a bad ball can lead to panic shots | Build FIRE passively during normal play, don't detour for it |
| **Holding for Advanced Skill Shot with a weak plunge** | Ball can dribble back down the inlane | Practice the plunge strength before relying on this skill shot in competition |

### Safe Control Positions

- **Backhanding** — nearly every major shot on this layout is reachable from either flipper; backhand shots are generally safer than cross-field shots
- **Repeated backhand on one ramp** — an efficient, low-risk way to grind Jam multiball qualification

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1)

1. Take the Advanced Skill Shot if comfortable with the plunge
2. Pick a song and start feeding its key shot — **Hells Bells** or **War Machine** are strong "set and forget" choices since they reward a single repeatable shot
3. Build toward Jam multiball (easiest); don't chase the bell yet
4. Bank your song jackpot once if it's been building a while and you feel drain risk rising

**Target Score:** 5M–15M

### Mid Game (Ball 2)

1. Stack qualifications — try to have Jam and one of Album/Tour ready before hitting the right ramp
2. Use the bell deliberately now for the 2X/3X playfield multiplier window, then immediately go collect jackpots while it's active
3. Switch songs opportunistically when 8 are lit, favoring whichever key shot you're already hitting well
4. Use banked VIP passes for Add-A-Ball during multiball to extend playtime

**Target Score:** 15M–40M

### Late Game (Ball 3+)

1. Go for a Double or Triple Multiball stack — this is your biggest scoring opportunity
2. Time the bell's 2X/3X window to land right as you start collecting jackpots
3. Let your song jackpot ride as large as your nerve allows, then collect via FIRE/cannon before risking another drain
4. If you're close to 8 songs lit, push for the song change and chase Encore only if you're already winning comfortably

**Target Score per ball:** 20M–80M+

## 🏆 Wizard Mode: Encore

**Requirements:** Complete all twelve songs, then perform one final song change.

**Gameplay:** Three balls to hit the shots the game calls out, in the order of the songs you actually played to get there.

> ⚠️ **Competitive note:** Encore is largely impractical to chase in competitive scoring — reaching it requires deliberately ignoring big jackpots in favor of finishing songs efficiently. Only go for it in casual play or if you're confident your score is already secure.

---

## 🔧 Premium/LE Exclusive: Hell Lower Playfield

Premium and LE models add a lower mini-playfield accessed via the "Hell"-titled songs' key shots (Highway to Hell, Hell Ain't a Bad Place to Be). It's a minor mode-building feature — not a significant scoring lever, but worth a visit if your song selection routes you there naturally.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 6.5°–7.0° | Stern factory standard; steeper increases drain speed on the bell miss |
| **Flipper strength** | Factory default | Game is tuned around stock flipper power for ramp/loop reachability |
| **Bell sensitivity** | Check operator settings | Some setups make the bell swing more violently than others |
| **Tilt warnings** | Standard | Nudging the cannon/bell area is common — don't overcorrect |

---

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Ball 1:** Pick a low-maintenance song, build Jam, bank jackpot once.
**Ball 2:** Start stacking multiball qualifications, use the bell with intent.
**Ball 3+:** Chase Double/Triple Multiball stacks, ride song jackpots big, collect before drain risk peaks.

**Target High-Score Ballpark:** **50–100 million points** is a strong, achievable goal; **200M+** requires consistent multiball stacking and disciplined song-jackpot banking.
