# 🤿 Scuba - Strategy Guide

*Gottlieb, 1970 — Designed by Ed Krynski*

> **"Hit the targets. Max the score. The ocean doesn't forgive mistakes."**

Scuba is a 2-player electromechanical Gottlieb machine with a 4-digit score display (max 9,999 points), 7 standup targets, 3 pop bumpers, and a free ball return lane. This is as pure an EM as it gets — no modes, no multiball, no multipliers beyond base scoring. The entire game is: hit the high-value standup targets before you drain. Our goal: **9,500–9,999** (near-maximum possible score) through deliberate target farming.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Identify the highest-value lit standup targets** — they change value as they're hit
- [ ] **Aim for the upper standup targets** — they're generally higher value and harder to reach
- [ ] **Use the free ball return lane** if you see the ball heading left outlane
- [ ] **Pop bumpers are your passive income** — let them run between deliberate shots
- [ ] **Don't drain unnecessarily** — with a 9,999 max score, every ball matters

---

## 🌊 Core Mechanics

### Standup Targets (7 total)

Seven standup targets spread across the playfield. Each scores a fixed value when hit:
- **Upper targets (3):** 100–500 points each (highest values)
- **Mid targets (2):** 50–200 points
- **Lower targets (2):** 10–100 points

Target values on EM machines may vary by operator settings and machine configuration. The upper bank targets are the primary scoring opportunity.

### Free Ball Return Lane

A special lane on the left side that, if the ball rolls through, returns it to the plunger lane for a free re-plunge rather than counting as a drain. This is:
- An automatic feature (no player action required)
- Activated when the ball enters the specific left-side channel
- Effectively extends your play time if you can direct the ball toward it

### Pop Bumpers (3)

Three pop bumpers in the center/upper area. They score small points (10 each, typically) but can add up significantly during extended bumper sessions. They also randomly redirect the ball toward upper targets.

### 3-Ball or 5-Ball Play

Scuba supports both 3-ball and 5-ball game modes (operator-selectable). 5-ball play gives more opportunities to approach the max score. On 3-ball, every ball is critical.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Upper standup targets | 100–500 pts each | Medium — requires precise aim |
| Mid standup targets | 50–200 pts each | Low |
| Lower standup targets | 10–100 pts each | Very low |
| Pop bumpers | 10 pts each | None |
| Free ball return lane | Ball save | Low |

### Drain Paths to Avoid

- **Right outlane** — primary drain; no ball save on right side
- **Gap between bumpers** — ball can thread through directly to drain if bumpers are quiet

### Ball Control Tips

- After bumper action, use flipper timing to redirect toward upper targets
- Soft plunge to start — lands in bumper area for passive scoring before deliberate aim
- Left flipper preference for upper-left target bank; right for upper-right

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### 3-Ball Play
- Ball 1: Target the two highest-value upper targets (100–200 pts each visit)
- Ball 2: Continue upper targets, collect mid-range
- Ball 3: Push for remaining high-value targets; accept bumper scoring

### 5-Ball Play
- Balls 1–2: Upper target farm
- Balls 3–4: Mid targets + aggressive upper target revisits
- Ball 5: Conservative — protect score, accept bumper accumulation

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 2,000–5,000 |
| Solid | 5,000–8,000 |
| Strong | 8,000–9,500 |
| Elite | 9,500–9,999 |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **On a 4-digit EM, every point is significant.** There's no jackpot or multiplier to bail you out — disciplined target farming is the only path to elite scores.

> **The free ball return lane is rarer than it looks.** Don't count on it — treat every shot toward the left as potentially costing you a ball.

> **Pop bumpers can carry you.** On a slow day, 10 bumper hits per ball is 100 passive points — over 5 balls, that's 500+ without effort. Don't dismiss them.
