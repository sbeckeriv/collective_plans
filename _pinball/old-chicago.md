# 🏙️ Old Chicago Strategy Guide
## Bally 1976 | Five-Bumper Chaos in the Windy City

---

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT NOTE FOR MODERN PINBALL PLAYERS**
>
> Old Chicago is a **1976 electromechanical (EM) pinball machine**:
> - ❌ No Modes or Multiballs
> - ❌ No Stacking or Combos
> - ❌ No Digital Displays or DMD
>
> **Maximum score: 99,990 (early production) or 199,990 (modified version)**
>
> Old Chicago features an unusual **5 pop bumper** layout and **no slingshots** — a rare configuration that makes the upper playfield exceptionally active and unpredictable. When your score rolls past 99,990, the machine sounds a celebratory buzzer. Designer Greg Kmiec's Chicago-themed layout with a massive bumper cluster is one of the most distinctive EM playfields of the 1970s.

## 🎯 Core Mechanics

### Playfield Features

| Feature          | Quantity | Notes                                     |
| ---------------- | -------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Flippers         | 2        | Standard lower position                   |
| Pop Bumpers      | 5        | Large cluster — the heart of the game     |
| Slingshots       | 0        | **None** — unique for an EM               |
| Standup Targets  | 3        | Award bonus units                         |
| Kick-out Holes   | 2        | Award points and bonus                    |
| Drop Target Bank | 5-bank   | Primary controlled scoring objective      |
| Spinning Target  | 1        | Fast rotational scoring                   |

> 💡 **The No-Slingshot Difference**: Without slingshots, the ball behaves very differently in the lower playfield. Balls tend to drain more smoothly toward the center but lose the lateral energy injection that slingshots provide. Ball control near the outlanes requires extra attention.

### 🎱 Plunge Strategy

- **Soft Plunge**: Routes into the massive 5-bumper cluster for extended chaos play
- **Medium Plunge**: Tries to thread toward the drop target bank
- **Hard Plunge**: Sends ball toward top rollovers and kick-out positions

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: Old Chicago rewards plunging into the bumper cluster. Five active pop bumpers mean the ball can spend a long time in the upper playfield, scoring points on every hit.

## 💥 The 5 Pop Bumper Cluster — The Windy City

Old Chicago's defining feature is its five-bumper upper playfield:

| Bumper Action     | Points  | Notes                               |
| ----------------- | ------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Single bumper hit | 100 pts | Per hit — adds up fast with 5 bumpers |
| Extended rally    | 500–2,000+ | Ball bouncing between multiple bumpers |

**Bumper Strategy:**
- Route the ball into the cluster as often as possible — 5 bumpers means exponentially more action than typical 2-3 bumper EMs
- The chaotic nature of 5 bumpers means extended stays are common
- Lower the pitch slightly if ball exits too fast (within reason)

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: "The 5 pop bumper cluster on Old Chicago is unlike most EMs — the ball can ricochet up there for a very long time. Be patient and let it work."

## 🎯 5-Bank Drop Targets

The drop target bank is your primary controlled scoring objective:

| Action            | Points   | Bonus Effect        |
| ----------------- | -------- | ------------------- |
| Single target hit | 500 pts  | +1 bonus unit       |
| All 5 down        | Bank resets | Kick-out hole lit |

**Drop Target Strategy:**
- Approach from the appropriate flipper with a deliberate angle
- Without slingshots to worry about, the lower playfield is calmer — use this for targeted shots
- Clear the bank to light kick-out holes, then collect them for combined bonus

- With no slingshots, kick-out ejections are more predictable

## 🎊 The Over-100,000 Buzzer

On early production Old Chicago machines:
- When your score rolls past **99,990**, the machine sounds a **buzzer**
- This was a celebratory design feature to mark an exceptional score
- Modified production runs display up to 199,990 without rolling over

> 💡 **Fun Fact**: Hearing the Old Chicago buzzer is a badge of honor — it signals you've played an excellent game on a machine that caps at a relatively modest 99,990 display maximum.

## 📈 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1–2)
1. 💥 Route ball into the 5-bumper cluster for extended scoring
2. 🎯 Clear the drop target bank when ball exits bumpers
3. 🕳️ Collect kick-out holes after bank clears
4. 📈 Build bonus steadily with standup targets

### Mid Game
1. 💥 Keep feeding the bumper cluster — this is where big scores happen
2. 🎯 Cycle drop bank clears
3. 🌀 Spinner shots for quick supplemental scoring

### Final Ball
1. 📈 Maximize bonus units
2. 💥 One final bumper cluster session
3. 🎊 Push for the 99,990 buzzer if close!
