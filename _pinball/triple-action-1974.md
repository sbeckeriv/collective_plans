# 🎭 Triple Action Strategy Guide
## Williams 1974 | Three-Way Attack

---

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT NOTE FOR MODERN PINBALL PLAYERS**
>
> Triple Action is a **1974 electromechanical (EM) pinball machine**:
> - ❌ No Multiballs
> - ❌ No Modes or Wizard Modes
> - ❌ No Digital Displays or DMD
>
> **Maximum possible score: 199,990 points** (mechanical score reels)
>
> The name "Triple Action" describes the machine's three primary scoring systems working in concert: **4 drop targets**, **3 rollover buttons**, and **spinning posts** — each contributing to a layered scoring strategy. Designed by Steve Kordek, this is a well-balanced mid-70s Williams EM.

### 🎱 Plunge Strategy

- **Soft Plunge**: Routes into the 3-bumper cluster for early accumulation
- **Medium Plunge**: Aims for rollover buttons on the descent
- **Hard Plunge**: Sends ball toward drop target bank

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: Triple Action's scoring synergy rewards routing through all three feature types per ball. A plunge that hits rollovers on the way up and drop targets on the return is ideal.

## 🎯 Drop Targets — The 4-Bank

The four drop targets are your primary controlled scoring objective:

| Action            | Points   | Bonus Effect       |
| ----------------- | -------- | ------------------ |
| Single target hit | 500 pts  | +1 bonus unit      |
| All 4 down        | Bank resets | Bonus lit / special lane lit |

**Drop Target Strategy:**
- A 4-bank is more achievable per visit than a 5-bank — you can often clear all four in 2–3 shots
- Each clear resets for another cycle — multiple clears per ball are possible on good plays
- Drop target deflections often feed the rollover button area below

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: The 4-bank clears quickly. On a controlled shot, you can sometimes take 2 or 3 targets with one good hit off a carom. Be aggressive with the drops — they reset fast.

---

## 🔘 Rollover Buttons — The Triple

Three rollover buttons are positioned across the mid-playfield:

| Rollovers Hit  | Effect                          |
| -------------- | ------------------------------- |
| 1st button     | +1 bonus unit                   |
| 2nd button     | +1 bonus unit                   |
| 3rd button (all 3) | **Bonus multiplier or special award** |

**Rollover Strategy:**
- The ball naturally rolls over buttons when bouncing between slingshots and drop targets
- Completing all 3 buttons is realistic each ball — work toward it
- Completed buttons typically light the bonus multiplier lane

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: The three rollover buttons are arranged so a standard right-flipper shot toward the drop targets will often pass over at least 1–2 of them. Adjust flip power to hit more on each pass.

## 🌀 Spinning Posts

Spinning posts are scattered across the playfield:

| Spinning Post Hit  | Points    | Notes                          |
| ------------------ | --------- | ------------------------------ |
| Clean centered hit | Points per rotation | Fast early accumulation |
| Glancing hit       | Fewer rotations     | Less efficient               |

**Spinning Post Strategy:**
- Spinning posts reward clean, direct hits — don't try to sneak around them
- Natural ball flow through the mid-playfield frequently clips spinning posts
- Stack spinning post scoring with rollover button passes for maximum mid-playfield yield

## 💥 Pop Bumpers

Three pop bumpers in the upper playfield provide steady accumulation:

| Bumper Condition | Points   |
| ---------------- | -------- |
| Per hit          | 100 pts  |

- Three bumpers provide more chaotic action than two — the ball can bounce extended sequences
- Bumper exits frequently send ball toward rollover buttons and drop targets
- Route ball into bumper zone when behind — the point accumulation adds up

## 🎰 Bonus System

### Building Bonus

| Action              | Bonus Added    |
| ------------------- | -------------- |
| Drop target hit     | +1 bonus unit  |
| Rollover button hit | +1 bonus unit  |
| Pop bumper hits     | Minor bonus    |

**Bonus Multiplier:**
- Completing all 3 rollover buttons lights the bonus multiplier lane
- Hit the lit lane to double end-of-ball bonus
- On a 3-ball game, maximizing the multiplier each ball is crucial

**Maximum Bonus:** Working toward maximum bonus before each ball drain is key — end-of-ball collection is where large scores are made or lost.

## 📈 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1–2)
1. 🎯 Clear the 4-bank drop targets — first controlled scoring objective
2. 🔘 Collect all 3 rollover buttons per visit to mid-playfield
3. 🌀 Spinning posts during natural ball flow
4. 📈 Build bonus toward maximum

### Mid Game
1. 🎯 Repeat drop bank cycles
2. 🔘 Keep completing rollover buttons for multiplier
3. 💥 Let pop bumpers work — 3 bumpers accumulate well
4. 2️⃣ Light and hit bonus multiplier lane

### Final Ball
1. 📈 Maximum bonus is the priority
2. 🔘 Complete all 3 rollover buttons for multiplier if not yet done
3. 🎯 One final drop bank clear
4. 2️⃣ **Collect bonus multiplier** before draining
