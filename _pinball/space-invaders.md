# 👾 Space Invaders - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1980 — Designed by Jim Patla*

> **"Four flippers. Captive ball. Spinning target. Invade the score."**

Space Invaders (Bally, 1980) is a licensed widebody solid-state machine capitalizing on the Taito arcade hit. It features four flippers, four passive bumpers (in addition to 3 pop bumpers), a captive ball, a horseshoe lane, a spinner, and a 6-standup target bank. The animated infinity-mirror backglass is one of the most distinctive visual elements of the era. Score cap is 999,950. Our goal: **600,000–900,000** through spinner farming, captive ball multipliers, and drop target completions.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Spin the spinner** on every orbit pass — it's the fastest points-per-second shot on the machine
- [ ] **Hit the captive ball** repeatedly to advance your bonus multiplier
- [ ] **Complete the 3-bank drop targets** for bonus advance and feature lights
- [ ] **Thread the horseshoe lane** at the top for the lane bonus
- [ ] **Use all four flippers** — upper flippers access spinner and captive ball directly
- [ ] **Manage the passive bumpers** at the top — they score 50 pts over 1–2 seconds; don't tilt mid-accumulation

---

## 👾 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

Full plunge — the ball routes through the passive bumpers at the top. Landing in specific horseshoe positions at the top awards lane bonuses. No deliberate skill shot timing is required; focus on the first shot after the ball clears the bumpers.

### Spinner (Spinning Target)

The spinner is the highest-efficiency shot in the game. Located on one of the orbit lanes:
- Each spin = 100 points (or 500 points when lit)
- A full-speed orbit pass through the spinner: 1,000–5,000 points in one shot
- Always pass through it; never sacrifice a spinner trip

### Captive Ball

The captive ball advances your **bonus multiplier** with each hit. It sits in a cage on the playfield — hitting it registers the switch:
- 1 hit = 2× bonus
- 3 hits = 3× bonus
- Continue hitting for 4× and 5× (maximum)

Hit the captive ball **before** your bonus base is large — multiplier advance early compounds everything that follows.

### Horseshoe Lane (Top)

The horseshoe at the top lanes the ball between the left and right upper areas. Rolling through the horseshoe awards the horseshoe lane bonus and feeds into the passive bumpers.

### Passive Bumpers (4)

Four passive bumpers (ringless, stationary) at the top score 50 points each over 1–2 seconds. Unlike pop bumpers, they don't kick the ball — the ball must pass through them. Don't tilt while they're accumulating.

### Pop Bumpers (3)

Three pop bumpers in the center score 10–100 points per hit. They direct the ball erratically — use upper flippers to redirect after a bumper sequence.

### 3-Bank Drop Targets

One 3-bank drop target set. Completing it:
- Advances bonus base
- Lights the solitary drop target for a one-shot bonus
- Resets for a second round (higher value)

### Standup Targets (6)

Six standup targets scattered around the playfield. Completing all 6 lights the extra ball insert and awards a bonus sequence. Individual targets: 100–500 points.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Spinner | 100–500 pts per spin | Low |
| Captive ball | Multiplier advance | Low |
| 3-bank drop targets | Bonus advance | Low |
| Horseshoe lane | Lane bonus | Low |
| Standup targets | 100–500 each | Low |
| Pop bumpers | Passive | None |
| Passive bumpers | 50 pts each (slow) | None |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Left outlane** (widebody) — the extra width makes lateral ball movement larger; nudge to save
- **Right outlane** — same widebody concern; post-spinner balls can overshoot the right flipper
- **Between upper/lower flipper pairs** — four-flipper machines have gaps; watch the ball path between upper and lower positions

### Ball Control (4 Flippers, Widebody)

- After pop bumper chaos, use upper flippers to redirect toward the spinner orbit
- Trap on lower left flipper for the captive ball shot
- Upper right flipper → spinner → upper left flipper → captive ball is the high-value loop

## 🔄 Progression Systems

- **Captive ball:** Max 5× bonus (cumulative within ball; resets on drain)
- **Drop target bank:** 1st completion = feature lights; 2nd completion = higher bonus step
- **Standup targets:** All 6 → extra ball lit

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1 (5-Ball) or Ball 1 (3-Ball)
1. Captive ball × 3 immediately (multiplier to 3×)
2. Spinner loop × 5 passes (1,500–5,000 points)
3. 3-bank drop target completion
4. Horseshoe lane pass

### Mid Balls
1. Captive ball to 5× (max)
2. Spinner farming — most efficient path to large scores
3. Standup target completion for extra ball

### Final Ball
1. Protect multiplier — avoid unnecessary drain risks
2. Spinner passes for final score padding
3. Let passive bumpers run for incidental 50-pt accumulations

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 100K–300K |
| Solid | 350K–600K |
| Strong | 600K–850K |
| Elite | 850K–999,950 |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Spinner efficiency is everything.** Space Invaders rewards players who find the spinner route and stay in it. One focused ball with 20 spinner passes outscores three balls of random target shooting.

> **Captive ball on ball 1, always.** Starting with 5× multiplier means every score afterward is doubled or more. Don't skip it for flashier shots.

> **Don't tilt near passive bumpers.** Those 50-point awards take 1–2 seconds to fully register. An impatient nudge mid-accumulation costs you points.
