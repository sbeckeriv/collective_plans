# 🏺 Pharaoh - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1981 — Designed by Tony Kraemer*

> **"Two levels, two Magna-Saves, two multiballs — and no pop bumpers."**

Pharaoh is a split-level widebody SS machine with four flippers, four 3-bank drop target sets, two kick-out holes, two Magna-Save magnets, and 2-ball multiball. Notably, it has **no pop bumpers** — scoring is entirely player-controlled. The Magna-Save buttons let you electromagnetically deflect draining balls, making ball control a premium skill here. Our goal: **500K–800K** through drop target completion, multiball, and smart Magna-Save use.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Learn the Magna-Save buttons** (side buttons) — activate just before a ball hits the outlane to magnetically divert it
- [ ] **Complete drop target banks** on both levels to build bonus and light features
- [ ] **Lock a ball in the upper playfield** to start 2-ball multiball
- [ ] **Use the upper flippers** to access upper-level drop targets and the lock lane
- [ ] **Hit the captive ball** for bonus multiplier advancement
- [ ] **Collect kick-out hole bonus** when lit via drop target completions

---

## ⚱️ Core Mechanics

### Magna-Save (The Defining Feature)

Two buttons on the cabinet sides each activate an electromagnet in the outlane area:
- Press **just before the ball enters the outlane** — the magnet deflects it back to the flipper
- Each Magna-Save has **limited charges** (typically 1 per ball, replenished by in-game events)
- Conserve for dangerous drain situations; don't waste on casual outlane approaches

> 🎯 **Timing:** Magna-Save must be triggered early — the magnet takes a moment to energize. Press when the ball is near the slingshot, not after it's already in the outlane lane.

### Drop Targets (Four 3-Banks)

| Bank | Location | Completion Reward |
|---|---|---|
| Left lower | Lower left | Bonus advance + feature light |
| Right lower | Lower right | Bonus advance + feature light |
| Left upper | Upper playfield | Lights ball lock |
| Right upper | Upper playfield | Lights kick-out value |

Complete all four banks to light **extra ball** and maximize bonus base.

### Captive Ball

The captive ball on the right side advances your **bonus multiplier** with each hit. Target it early — 3× to 5× multiplier dramatically increases end-of-ball value.

### Kick-Out Holes (2)

Each kick-out hole awards a mid-ball bonus when lit. Light them via drop target completion sequences. The upper kick-out also refeeds into the upper playfield.

### Split-Level Playfield

- **Lower level:** Main flipper area, slingshots, two drop target banks, and the captive ball
- **Upper level:** Two upper flippers, two more drop target banks, ball lock lane, and upper kick-out

Use the upper right flipper to feed the upper left drop targets, and vice versa.

---

## 🎳 Multiball

### 2-Ball Multiball

**How to start:** Lock a ball via the upper playfield lock lane (accessed by the upper kicker lane, lit after completing upper drop target banks).

**Gameplay:**
- Both balls active simultaneously
- All shots score at elevated values
- Jackpot (if applicable per operator settings) via completing all four drop target banks simultaneously

**No built-in save** during multiball — Magna-Save charges remain available if you have them.

### Bonus Ball (Multiplayer Feature)

When two or more players finish the game, the highest scorer gets unlimited balls for a fixed time period — a unique competitive mechanic. In competitive play, protect your score on your final ball.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Captive ball | Multiplier advance | Low |
| Lower drop targets | Bonus + features | Low |
| Upper drop targets | Lock + kick-out value | Medium — upper flip angle |
| Kick-out holes | Mid-ball bonus | Low |
| Upper kicker lane | Access to upper level | Medium — committed shot |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Left outlane** — primary drain threat; prime Magna-Save territory
- **Right outlane** — secondary drain path; slingshot activity increases danger
- **Between upper/lower flippers** — missed upper-level shots can fall into the gap

### No Pop Bumpers = You Control Everything

Without pop bumpers, there are no random ball redirects. Every point is earned deliberately. This means:
- Missed shots drain with no recovery
- Trap and assess frequently
- Never panic-flip

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Captive ball × 3 hits immediately (multiplier to 3×)
2. Lower drop targets × 2 banks
3. Upper flip to lock lane, secure the lock

### Ball 2
1. Start multiball if lock is in place
2. During multiball: target upper + lower banks simultaneously
3. Post-multiball: push captive ball to 5×

### Ball 3–5
1. Complete all four drop target banks for extra ball
2. Collect lit kick-out holes
3. Protect Magna-Save for critical moments

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Magna-Save is a skill, not a safety net.** Activate it early and deliberately — players who save one ball per game with Magna-Save dramatically outperform those who waste it or forget it exists.

> **No pop bumpers means no luck.** Every point requires intention. If you're not aiming, you're draining. Trap frequently and plan each shot.

> **Captive ball first, always.** 5× multiplier on a large base at ball end is often the highest single-ball scoring opportunity in the game.
