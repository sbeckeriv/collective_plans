# 🎯 Target Alpha - Strategy Guide

*Gottlieb, 1976 — Designed by Ed Krynski*

> **"Ten targets. Five targets. Four flippers. Hit them all."**

Target Alpha is a 1976 Gottlieb EM replay machine with four flippers, a 10-bank drop target set, a 5-bank drop target set, two standup targets, and two pop bumpers. It's a straightforward EM drop target machine — the entire game revolves around completing the target banks for bonuses and replays. No score display on some configurations; the replay wheel is the measure of success. Our goal: **maximum replay collection** and high completion counts.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Clear the 10-bank drop targets** as your primary objective each ball
- [ ] **Complete the 5-bank** for bonus and replay lights
- [ ] **Use the upper flippers** to access the upper target banks directly
- [ ] **Hit the standup targets** for bonus multiplier steps
- [ ] **Protect your replay count** — a drain mid-completion is the primary risk

---

## 🎯 Core Mechanics

### 10-Bank Drop Targets (Primary)

A full row of 10 drop targets spanning the upper playfield. Completing all 10:
- Awards the primary completion bonus
- Lights the replay
- Resets the bank for a second run (at higher value)

The 10-bank takes deliberate aiming — the upper left and upper right flippers are needed for the far targets.

### 5-Bank Drop Targets (Secondary)

Five drop targets on one side of the playfield. Completing this bank:
- Awards a secondary bonus
- Advances a feature (varies by operator settings — extra ball, special, or replay on some configs)

### Four Flippers

The four-flipper layout is the mechanical key to this machine:
- **Lower flippers:** Standard play and slingshot management
- **Upper flippers:** Access to the upper portions of the 10-bank and approach to the far standup targets

The upper flippers require deliberate use — passive lower-flipper play won't reach the upper target ranges.

### Standup Targets (2)

Two standup targets positioned for bonus multiplier advancement. Hitting both advances the bonus multiplier by 1 step each, up to a maximum (machine-dependent).

### Pop Bumpers (2)

Two pop bumpers provide passive scoring between deliberate target approaches. They're less dominant than on machines with 3–5 bumpers — don't rely on them as a primary scoring source.

### Replay Wheel

Target Alpha is a replay machine — the goal is earning replays (free games) via completions. The replay wheel shows the current replay threshold. Each bank completion moves the replay wheel.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| 10-bank drop targets (upper) | Primary completion | Medium — wide spread |
| 5-bank drop targets (side) | Secondary completion | Low |
| Standup targets (2) | Multiplier advance | Low |
| Pop bumpers (2) | Passive scoring | None |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Left outlane** — the most common drain path; four-flipper machines have a gap between upper and lower flipper pairs that can catch a fast ball
- **Between upper and lower flipper pairs** — a ball moving quickly from the upper area can thread between the flipper tips if timing is off

### Ball Control (4 Flippers)

- After upper flipper shots, let the ball settle to the lower flippers before attempting another upper shot
- Trap on the lower left flipper between 10-bank approaches
- Don't rush; the bank won't reset until you complete it

## 💰 Scoring Strategy (Replay-Based)

### Ball 1
1. Upper left + upper right flipper → hit far 10-bank targets
2. Complete 10-bank → replay lit
3. 5-bank completion before ball end

### Ball 2
1. 10-bank reset run (higher value)
2. Both standup targets for multiplier
3. 5-bank run

### Ball 3–5
1. Third 10-bank run if achievable
2. Conservative play protecting the replay count
3. Standup targets for multiplier bump on final bonus

---

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The upper flippers are not optional.** Players who ignore them and play only the lower flippers complete at most 60% of the 10-bank per ball. Use all four deliberately.

> **Complete the 10-bank before the 5-bank.** The 10-bank is the replay trigger — prioritize it. The 5-bank is secondary; complete it on the approach to the 10-bank, not instead of it.

> **Trap between banks.** After a bank completion, the ball often kicks sideways. Trap on the lower left flipper and reassess before your next bank approach.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 6.5° — Gottlieb EM standard |
| All 10 drop targets | Verify each resets cleanly — a stuck target makes full bank completion impossible |
| All 4 flippers | Check for equal strength; weak upper flippers block the far target range |
| Replay wheel | Inspect mechanical advance; worn wheels can skip replay awards |
