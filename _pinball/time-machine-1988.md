# ⏰ Time Machine (Data East, 1988) - Strategy Guide

*Data East, 1988 — Designed by Joe Kaminkow & Ed Cebula, Sound by David Thiel*

> **"Travel to the 1950s and watch the score display turn into a chime machine."**

Time Machine (Data East, 1988) is one of pinball's most clever gimmick machines: when gameplay reaches the "1950s" decade, the DMD score display emulates EM scoring reels AND the electronic sounds are replaced by a real physical chime box under the playfield. It also has a left outlane kickback, a cellar hole, a VUK, and 9 standup targets. Our goal: **30M–80M** (Data East early-era scoring) by advancing through decades, using the kickback, and collecting the cellar hole bonus at peak multiplier.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Light the left outlane kickback** early every ball — it's essential for surviving decade advancement
- [ ] **Shoot standup targets** to advance through time periods (1990s → 1980s → 1970s → 1960s → 1950s)
- [ ] **Hit the cellar hole** (VUK-fed) when lit for decade bonus payouts
- [ ] **Reach the 1950s** for the chime box experience — the scoring system changes to EM-style reels
- [ ] **Use the VUK** (right side) to access the upper playfield targets faster
- [ ] **Mini-post in flipper gap** — note its position; it blocks the center drain slightly

## 🕰️ Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Star rollover** | Time plunge to lit rollover | Decade advance + bonus |
| **Standard** | Full plunge | Ball to pop bumpers |

### Decade Progression (1990s → 1950s)

The game tracks a time travel decade progression:

| Decade | How to Advance | Scoring Mode |
|---|---|---|
| 1990s (start) | Standard electronic | Normal display |
| 1980s | Standup target completions | Normal |
| 1970s | More completions + cellar hole | Normal |
| 1960s | All 9 targets once | Normal |
| **1950s (EM Era!)** | Complete full sequence | **EM reel display + chime box** |

**Reaching the 1950s:** The DMD switches to simulated EM reel counters, the electronic sounds cut off, and the physical chime box under the playfield takes over. It's genuinely unique.

### 9 Standup Targets

Nine standup targets drive the decade advancement:
- Each target hit contributes to the decade counter
- Specific patterns advance faster (grouping hits efficiently)
- Some targets score higher than others depending on the current decade

### Cellar Hole (Bottom of Playfield)

The cellar hole is a drain-adjacent hole on the lower playfield:
- Shooting it awards the decade bonus (base × current decade multiplier)
- VUK feeds to it from specific approach angles
- **At 1950s:** Cellar hole awards EM-style high values with chime sounds

### VUK (Right Side)

The vertical up-kicker on the right launches the ball to the upper playfield targets:
- Used to access far standup targets not reachable from lower flippers
- Also serves as a ball save in some configurations (ball launched to upper area rather than draining)

### Left Outlane Kickback

Essential ball save — lights via specific target completions:
- Each qualifying hit = one kickback charge
- Kickback fires from the left outlane back into play
- **Light it first, every ball.** The decade advancement requires many standup hits; some will drain toward the left.

### Mini-Post in Flipper Gap

A small post between the flippers that partially blocks the center gap:
- Reduces center drain frequency compared to machines without it
- Not a full block — the ball can still drain center on a direct path, but slower shots are deflected to a flipper

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Standup targets (9) | Decade advance | Low |
| Cellar hole | Decade bonus collect | Medium |
| VUK | Upper target access | Low |
| Star rollovers | Bonus advance | Low (passive) |
| Pop bumpers (3) | Passive | None |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Left outlane** — primary drain during aggressive target chasing; kickback is your safety net
- **Cellar hole approach** — the hole is near the lower right; off-center shots can hit the slingshot and kick to drain
- **Right outlane** — no kickback on right side

## 🏆 The 1950s Experience

When you reach the 1950s:
1. The DMD switches to emulated EM-style counter reels
2. Electronic sounds stop
3. The physical chime box fires for every score increment
4. The sound is genuinely vintage — bells for 10s, a different bell for 100s

This is more a feature than a strategy advantage, but it's the machine's defining moment. Reach it at least once per session.

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Light kickback (quick standup sequence)
2. Standup targets × 7 (advance to 1970s)
3. Cellar hole collection at 1970s value

### Ball 2
1. Full 9 targets → reach 1960s
2. Cellar hole at 1960s (higher value)
3. VUK for upper target access

### Ball 3
1. Complete to 1950s (if not already)
2. Chime box era: cellar hole at 1950s value (EM-style)
3. Kickback as insurance for final decade push

### Score Targets

| Performance | Score |
|---|---|
| Beginner | 5M–15M |
| Solid | 20M–45M |
| Strong (1950s + cellar) | 45M–70M |

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The chime box experience is the game.** Prioritize reaching the 1950s at least once — it's unlike anything else in pinball. The kickback makes the long decade climb survivable.

> **Group your standup target hits.** 9 targets spread across the playfield — work one side at a time rather than chasing singles across the playfield.

> **Cellar hole at 1950s is your peak collection moment.** Build to the decade first, then collect. The base bonus × 1950s multiplier is substantially higher than earlier decades.
