# 🌴 Travel Time Strategy Guide
## Williams 1973 | Beat the Clock

---

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT NOTE FOR MODERN PINBALL PLAYERS**
>
> Travel Time is a **1973 electromechanical (EM) pinball machine** with a unique twist:
> - ❌ No Modes, Multiballs, or Wizard Modes
> - ❌ No Digital Displays or DMD
> - ⏱️ **TIMED PLAY**: Length of play is controlled by a **playfield clock** — hitting targets adds time
> - 1-player only
>
> Travel Time is not a standard ball-drain EM. The game ends when the **clock runs out**, not when you drain a set number of balls. This fundamentally changes strategy: **your primary goal is to hit targets to extend your play time**, not just score points.

### ⏱️ The Clock System — Everything Changed

Unlike any standard EM, Travel Time plays against a **countdown clock**:

| Clock State        | Effect                                |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------- |
| Clock running      | Timer counting down                   |
| Target hit         | Time added to clock                   |
| Clock reaches zero | **Game ends — regardless of balls**   |
| High score reached | Replay or extra time (operator-set)   |

> 💡 **Core Insight**: In Travel Time, **survival = scoring**. More time = more targets = more time = higher score. The clock creates a positive feedback loop for skilled players. Your first job is never to let the clock expire.

### 🎱 Plunge Strategy

- **Standard Plunge**: Aim for the pop bumper zone to create rapid bumper-to-target interactions
- **Soft Plunge**: Sends ball toward rollovers for early time injection
- **Note**: If the ball drains while clock is still running, a new ball is served — you keep playing as long as the clock has time remaining

## 🎯 Standup Targets — Your Lifeline

With 10 standup targets, Travel Time has one of the largest target arrays of any early-70s EM:

| Target Action     | Effect                     |
| ----------------- | -------------------------- |
| Hit any target    | Points + **time added**    |
| Complete sets     | Bonus and additional time  |

**Target Layout:**
- 10 targets spread across the playfield mean frequent ball interactions
- No slingshots = balls travel more directly and predictably to targets
- The lack of slingshot chaos makes targeting more precise

**Target Priority:**
1. Hit any lit target immediately — time is your currency
2. Work toward completing target groupings for bonus time awards
3. Never let the ball sit at the flippers too long — every second of hesitation costs time

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: Travel Time rewards aggressive, continuous play. There is no benefit to waiting. The moment you catch the ball on a flipper, shoot it immediately toward any target.

- Prioritize it when the ball path naturally leads there

## ⚡ Ball Kicker Between Flippers

The ball kicker between the two flippers is an unusual feature:

| Kicker Action    | Effect                          |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Ball enters kicker | Launches ball back into play  |
| Launch direction | Toward pop bumpers / mid-field  |

- The kicker helps keep the ball in play when it falls between the flippers
- Strategically valuable — a ball that would normally drain is instead relaunched
- Treat the kicker as a free "shot" — it sends the ball toward productive upper playfield areas

## 🕰️ Time Management Strategy

This is the core strategic layer of Travel Time:

### When Clock is Healthy (>60% remaining)
- Play normally, prioritize targets
- Reach for the kick-out hole when the path is clear
- Explore all target groupings

### When Clock is Getting Low (<30% remaining)
- Urgency mode: hit ANY target immediately
- Don't attempt difficult shots — go for the easiest adjacent target
- Use the pop bumper zone if the ball is there — bumpers add incremental time

### When Clock is Critical (<10% remaining)
- PANIC MODE: hit literally anything
- Hope for ball kicker catches if ball falls between flippers
- One kick-out hole can fully rescue a dying clock

## ⚠️ Shot Analysis

### 🔴 Dangerous Patterns

| Hazard              | Risk Level | Mitigation                             |
| ------------------- | ---------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Clock expiring      | CRITICAL   | Hit targets constantly — never stop    |
| Left outlane drain  | MEDIUM     | Ball returns (new ball) while clock active |
| Right outlane drain | MEDIUM     | Same — keep clock alive                |

> ⚠️ **Key Insight**: Draining is less punishing in Travel Time than in standard EMs — you get a new ball as long as the clock hasn't expired. The real danger is letting the clock run down.

### 🟢 Safe & Productive Patterns
