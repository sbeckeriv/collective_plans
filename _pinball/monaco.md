# 🏊 Monaco - Strategy Guide

*Segasa, 1977 — A rule-enhanced remake of Cannes (1976)*

> **"Collect now, or risk it for the double."**

Monaco is a higher-scoring, rule-enhanced Spanish EM remake of Segasa's earlier Cannes, built around a simple but genuinely interesting risk decision: collect your bonus now at face value, or keep spinning for a chance at doubling it. Our goal: build bonus via the spinner and lit lanes, then make a deliberate choice about when to collect versus when to push for the double.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Hit the spinner and lit lanes to build bonus** — the spinner specifically advances bonus roughly every 5-10 spins
- [ ] **Understand the core risk decision**: shooting the bonus collect scores your bonus *without* doubling and resets it to 10K — or you can keep playing to try for **Double Bonus** first
- [ ] **If Double Bonus is already active, adjust your strategy accordingly** — there's no reason to collect early once you've already activated the double
- [ ] **Use the swinging target and rotating spinner** as your main repeatable scoring shots
- [ ] **Decide deliberately, not reflexively** — collecting is the safe play, but pushing for the double is where the bigger scores come from

---

## 🏎️ Core Mechanics

### Bonus Building

- The **spinner** advances bonus roughly **every 5-10 spins**
- **Lit lanes** also contribute to bonus accumulation
- The **swinging target** is a distinctive secondary shot worth incorporating into your rotation

### The Collect-or-Double Decision (Core Strategic Choice)

This is Monaco's signature innovation over its predecessor Cannes:

| Choice | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Shoot the bonus collect now** | Bonus scores **without doubling**, then resets to **10K** |
| **Keep playing instead** | Risk continuing without banking, but working toward **Double Bonus** |

> 🎯 **Pro Tip — Core Strategy:** If **Double Bonus is already active**, there's no reason to rush toward the collect shot — your strategy should shift toward maximizing bonus accumulation before eventually cashing in at the doubled rate. If Double Bonus *isn't* active yet, weigh the risk of continued play against the safety of collecting at face value now.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Pop bumper unpredictability** (2 bumpers) | Standard late-70s bumper chaos | Treat as incidental scoring |
| **Swinging target timing** | A moving target requires different timing than a static one | Practice the swing pattern before relying on it for precision shots |
| **Pushing too long for Double Bonus** | Continuing to risk the ball instead of collecting can cost you everything if you drain | Weigh your current ball control before deciding to keep pushing |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball

1. Start building bonus via the spinner and lit lanes
2. Get comfortable with the swinging target's timing
3. Track whether Double Bonus has activated yet

### Mid Ball

1. If Double Bonus isn't active, decide whether to collect now or keep building toward it
2. If Double Bonus IS active, shift toward maximizing accumulation before your eventual collect
3. Keep working the spinner as your primary repeatable shot

### Late Ball

1. Make your final collect-or-push decision based on remaining ball control and risk tolerance
2. If you're confident in your ball control, push for the double before collecting
3. If risk feels too high, collect safely rather than losing everything to a drain

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Early:** Build bonus via the spinner and lanes, learn the swinging target's timing.
**Mid:** Decide whether to collect now or push toward Double Bonus, based on current activation status.
**Late:** Make your final collect-or-push call based on remaining ball control.

**Target Outcome:** Successfully activating and collecting a Double Bonus, rather than settling for a safe face-value collect, represents a strong, well-played, and appropriately risk-tolerant game on this rare Spanish EM remake.
