# ☢️ Total Nuclear Annihilation (Spooky, 2017) - Strategy Guide

*Spooky Pinball, 2017 — Designed, composed, and engineered by Scott Danesi*

> **"Feel the bass in your chest. Race the reactor. Don't let it melt down."**

Total Nuclear Annihilation (TNA, Spooky 2017) is a one-man machine: Scott Danesi designed, built, composed the music, and wrote core software for it. The result is an unnervingly cohesive experience — pulsing electronic music paired with a 12-inch powered subwoofer that physically vibrates the cabinet, a reactor countdown mechanic that creates constant urgency, 5 slingshots (most machines have 2), and **physically locking drop targets** that require a third hit to release all locked balls into a 3-ball multiball. This machine rewards aggression and speed above everything. Our goal: **maximize reactor feed shots**, trigger multiball repeatedly, and don't let the reactor melt down.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Feed the reactor scoops** continuously — they are the primary point source and reset the meltdown timer
- [ ] **Hit standup targets (11)** to light reactor bonuses and advance the multiplier
- [ ] **Drop the 3-in-line drop targets** to physically lock balls (2 balls lock, 3rd drop = multiball release)
- [ ] **Use the 3rd flipper (upper right)** to access the upper spinner and upper scoop
- [ ] **Spin the spinner** on every pass — it's per-revolution and high-value during reactor burns
- [ ] **Don't let the timer expire** — reactor meltdown ends the ball in penalty mode

## ⚛️ Core Mechanics

### The Reactor (Central Mechanic)

A countdown timer represents reactor temperature. The reactor overheats if you don't feed it:
- **Reactor scoops (2):** Each scoop shot resets/extends the countdown timer AND scores the current reactor value
- As you feed the reactor, the value per scoop increases (reactor burns hotter)
- Let the timer expire → meltdown: the ball drains in a penalty sequence
- The two scoops are at different positions — left scoop feeds one side, right scoop the other

### 11 Standup Targets

Eleven standup targets spread across the playfield:
- Each hit: small reactor value + advances multiplier
- Completing target sets lights the reactor for higher values
- With 5 slingshots (vs. the usual 2), targets near the slingshot zone get hit passively at extremely high rates

### 5 Slingshots (!)

Five slingshots is nearly double the industry standard:
- The additional slingshots create a chaotic, unpredictable lower playfield
- Balls move faster and in unexpected directions
- The extra slingshots passively hit nearby standup targets at high frequency
- **Don't fight the chaos** — keep flippers ready; TNA requires faster reactions than most machines

### 3-in-Line Drop Targets (Physical Ball Lock)

Three drop targets in a vertical line on the center playfield:
- Hit target 1 → physically locks ball #1 (the target holds the ball)
- Hit target 2 → physically locks ball #2
- Hit target 3 → ALL locked balls release simultaneously → 3-ball multiball begins
- This is a unique mechanical design: the targets literally trap balls rather than using a conventional VUK lock

### 3rd Flipper (Upper Right)

The upper right flipper accesses the upper playfield:
- Shoots to the upper spinner (high per-revolution value)
- Feeds the upper scoop for reactor bonus
- Must be triggered deliberately with the right flipper button while the ball is in the upper-right position

### Spinning Target (Center)

A single spinning target on the center playfield:
- Hit face = score; spinning face = multiplied score
- Best value when the reactor is at high burn

## 🎳 Multiball

### 3-Ball Multiball (3-in-Line Drop Release)

**How to start:** Hit the first drop target → ball #1 locks. Hit the second drop target → ball #2 locks. Hit the third drop target → all 3 balls release simultaneously.

**Jackpot structure:**
- 3-ball multiball with the reactor timer running for all 3 balls
- All three balls can simultaneously feed the reactor scoops
- Jackpots via scoop shots — each scoop during multiball scores elevated reactor value
- **The goal:** Feed both scoops rapidly with all 3 balls in play; the multiball window is when the highest scores happen

The physical ball lock is the machine's centerpiece mechanical innovation. When you see a ball stuck against a drop target, the game is working correctly.

### Dangerous Areas

- **5 slingshots** — the extra slingshot coverage means balls deflect at unexpected angles; be ready anywhere
- **Left/right outlanes** — after scoop ejects at speed; ball moves fast
- **Post-multiball drain** — releasing 3 balls simultaneously is chaotic; expect drains during multiball startup

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Continuous scoop feeding (reactor burn)
2. 11 standup targets × partial (multiplier seed)
3. Drop target 1 → ball lock attempt

### Ball 2
1. Feed both scoops; build reactor heat
2. Drop targets 1 & 2 → two balls locked
3. Drop target 3 → multiball release
4. During multiball: both scoops as fast as possible

### Ball 3
1. Keep reactor from melting down
2. Second multiball attempt
3. Spinner cycling via upper flipper during reactor peaks

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **This machine is intentionally loud and physically intense.** The 12" subwoofer is part of the game design — Danesi tuned the music to the bass response of that specific chamber. Play with the volume up.

> **The reactor timer is your enemy when idle.** Never stop shooting. Between drop targets, feed a scoop. Between scoops, hit standup targets. TNA punishes passive play harder than almost any other machine.

> **Drop target locking feels wrong at first.** When a ball seems "stuck" against a drop target, that's by design — the target is physically holding it. Hit the next target to progress the lock.

> **Five slingshots make this machine harder to read.** After a slingshot deflection, the ball can go almost anywhere. Keep both flippers in mid-position (not fully raised) between shots so you can react left or right.
