# 🎱 Eight Ball Deluxe - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1981 — Designed by George Christian*

> **"Clear the rack. Bonus carries the whole game."**

Eight Ball Deluxe is simple target-shooting wrapped in elaborate bonus rules: a 7-bank drop target represents the billiard balls, and your progress clearing that rack **carries over for the entire game** until you actually complete it. Our goal: clear the rack repeatedly, maximize your bonus multiplier each time, and spell D-E-L-U-X-E along the way.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Each pool ball drop target knocked down is worth 7,000 in bonus** — and this progress carries over ball to ball until you actually clear the whole rack
- [ ] **Complete A-B-C-D lanes for two free balls** (everything except the 8-ball) — a safer way to clear the rack, though C-D lanes are notoriously tough to hit intentionally
- [ ] **Collecting all 7 balls lights the 8-ball** in the top right — knock it down to complete the rack
- [ ] **After clearing the rack, choose your reward**: inline drops (bonus multiplier, applies to your entire bonus), the saucer behind the 8-ball (un-multiplied bonus collect), or spell D-E-L-U-X-E on the standups behind the 7 drops
- [ ] **Build your bonus early** — since rack progress doesn't reset until you clear it, there's no rush, but every ball you don't complete is bonus left on the table

## 🎱 Core Mechanics

### The 7-Ball Rack (Drop Targets)

- A 7-bank of drop targets represents the billiard balls **1-7 and 9-15**
- **Each ball knocked down is worth 7,000 points in bonus**
- **Critically, this progress carries over ball to ball** — the bank won't reset for you until you've fully cleared it and hit the 8-ball

### A-B-C-D Lanes (Safer Rack-Clearing Shortcut)

Completing all four **A-B-C-D** lanes awards **two free balls** in your rack (everything except the 8-ball itself). This is a **safer route to clearing the rack** than relying purely on drop target accuracy — though the **C and D lanes are notoriously difficult to hit intentionally**, so don't count on this as your primary strategy.

### Completing the Rack: The 8-Ball

Once all 7 balls are collected, the **8-ball lights** in the top right. Knock it down to **complete the rack**.

### Post-Rack Reward Choice (Pick One)

Once you've cleared the rack, you have three distinct options, each drawing on your **entire accumulated bonus**:

| Option | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Inline drops (4-in-line)** | Apply a **bonus multiplier** to your entire bonus |
| **Saucer behind the 8-ball** | Collect your bonus **un-multiplied**, immediately |
| **Spell D-E-L-U-X-E** (standups behind the 7 drops) | Alternative scoring path |

> 🎯 **Pro Tip — Core Decision Point:** After clearing the rack, the inline-drops multiplier route is almost always more valuable than the immediate un-multiplied saucer collect — but only if you're confident you can complete the inline drop bank before risking a drain. Weigh your remaining ball control against the multiplier upside.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Forcing C-D lane shots** | Notoriously difficult to hit intentionally; chasing them aggressively risks wasted shots | Treat A-B-C-D completion as a bonus opportunity, not a primary strategy — let it happen incidentally when convenient |
| **Pop bumper unpredictability** | Standard early-80s bumper chaos | Treat as incidental scoring |
| **Risking a drain while chasing the inline multiplier** | A drain before completing the inline bank forfeits the multiplier opportunity entirely | Only commit to the inline drops when you have solid ball control |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1)

1. Start working the 7-ball drop target rack — every ball counts toward your carried-over total
2. Pick up A-B-C-D lane completions opportunistically for free rack progress
3. Don't worry about clearing the full rack immediately — progress persists

### Mid Game (Ball 2)

1. Continue building rack progress; remember it carries over from Ball 1
2. If you're close to all 7 balls, push to complete the rack and light the 8-ball
3. Knock down the 8-ball once lit to complete the rack

### Late Game (Ball 3+)

1. After clearing the rack, decide between the inline multiplier, the immediate saucer collect, or D-E-L-U-X-E based on your ball control
2. If confident, go for the inline drops multiplier — it's the highest-value choice when achievable
3. Bank your bonus before the ball ends

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Early:** Work the 7-ball rack, pick up A-B-C-D opportunistically.
**Mid:** Continue building rack progress (it carries over), complete the rack and 8-ball when close.
**Late:** Choose your post-rack reward wisely — inline multiplier if you have good ball control, otherwise the safer saucer collect.

**Target Outcome:** Clearing the full 7-ball rack at least once, ideally followed by a successful inline-drops bonus multiplier, represents a strong, well-played game on this beloved sequel to the best-selling original.
