# 🏚️ Haunted House - Strategy Guide

*Gottlieb, 1982 — Designed by John Osborne*

> **"Go upstairs. Go downstairs. Smack everything while you're there."**

Haunted House was the first pinball machine ever built with three distinct playfields — attic, main floor, and cellar — connected by kickouts, a secret passage, and a trap door. With 8 flippers across all three levels, this is one of the most mechanically ambitious EM-era machines ever made. Our goal: cycle between levels as often as possible, clearing drop target banks for double scoring and a maxed 5X Monster Bonus.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Understand the three-hole plunge**: left hole → lower cellar playfield, center hole → main playfield, right hole → upper attic playfield
- [ ] **Cycle between the upper and lower levels as much as possible** — this is the core strategic loop, since visiting them advances your Monster Bonus multiplier
- [ ] **Clear drop target banks on the upper and lower levels** — these activate **double scoring** on other playfields, not just where you hit them
- [ ] **Monster Bonus multiplier increases each time you visit the upper and lower levels**, up to a maximum of **5X**
- [ ] **Use the secret passage** (a false target that drops down after impact) to access the cellar via an alternate route
- [ ] **Smack targets aggressively while you're on the upper/lower levels** — the entire strategy is built around maximizing your time and target hits on those bonus-feeding levels

## 🏰 Core Mechanics

### Three-Level Playfield Navigation

| Plunge Result | Destination |
|---|---|
| Left hole | **Lower (cellar) playfield**, kicked toward the lower-left flipper |
| Center hole/saucer | **Main playfield**, kicked downward |
| Right hole/saucer | **Upper (attic) playfield**, kicked up-right toward the left flipper |

Each level is themed to its part of the house: **main floor** (main playfield), **cellar** (lower level), **attic** (upper level).

### Secret Passage

A **false target** on the main playfield **drops down after impact**, opening an alternate entrance to the cellar — a unique mechanical feature for routing the ball between levels beyond the standard kickouts.

### Drop Target Banks (Upper & Lower Levels)

Clearing the drop target banks on the **upper** and **lower** levels does double duty:
1. Activates **double scoring** on the *other* playfields (not just the one you're currently on)
2. Contributes to advancing your **Monster Bonus multiplier**

### Monster Bonus Multiplier

- Increases **each time you visit the upper and lower levels**
- Maxes out at **5X**

> 🎯 **Pro Tip — Core Strategy:** The entire game rewards one simple behavior: **keep going upstairs and downstairs as much as possible, and hit everything you can while you're there.** Don't settle into camping the main playfield — the multiplier growth and double-scoring activation only come from cycling between the upper and lower levels repeatedly.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **8-flipper complexity across 3 levels** | Far more flippers to track and time than a standard machine | Learn each level's flipper layout individually before attempting fast level transitions |
| **Trap door mechanism** | Opens for the ball in specific situations — unfamiliarity can lead to unexpected ball routing | Get familiar with when and where the trap door activates |
| **Kick-out hole unpredictability** | Three-level routing depends on kickouts behaving consistently | Approach plunges and saucer shots with awareness of which level you're aiming for |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball

1. Get familiar with all three levels and their respective flipper layouts
2. Start cycling between upper and lower levels via the kickouts and secret passage
3. Begin clearing drop target banks wherever you land

### Mid Ball

1. Keep cycling levels to advance your Monster Bonus multiplier
2. Clear both upper and lower drop target banks for double scoring activation
3. Use the secret passage as an alternate cellar route when convenient

### Late Ball

1. Push your Monster Bonus multiplier toward the 5X maximum via continued level cycling
2. Maximize target hits while double scoring is active
3. Bank your multiplied bonus before the ball ends
