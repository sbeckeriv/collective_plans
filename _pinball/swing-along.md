Swing-Along is a 1963 Gottlieb EM with a 2,000-point score cap (displayed 0–1,999), four pop bumpers, four standup targets, and three spinning targets. This is one of the earliest Gottlieb EMs in our collection — scoring is entirely about shot precision and keeping the ball alive. ## 🎶 Core Mechanics

### Spinning Targets (3)

Three spinning targets (roto-targets) that spin when hit, scoring a displayed value. They are the primary scoring mechanism:
- Each hit scores the current face value shown
- Higher-spinning = potentially higher values
- The targets slow between hits and accelerate when struck

### Standup Targets (4)

Four standup targets around the playfield. Completing all four lights additional features (replay credits, bonus lights). Individual targets score 10–50 points each.

### Pop Bumpers (4)

Four pop bumpers in the center cluster — more than most machines of this era. They score 1–10 points per hit but are reliable and safe. Extended bumper sessions add up over the course of a ball.

### Slingshots (2)

Standard slingshots on both sides. They score small amounts and redirect the ball; no strategic value beyond keeping the ball alive.

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Spinning targets are your priority, always.** On a machine with a 1,999 cap, the spinning targets represent the bulk of achievable points. Every ball should include at least 3–4 spinning target hits.

> **Let the bumpers run.** Four bumpers is excellent for a 1963 machine — each bumper session adds 20–50 points passively. Don't rush to exit the cluster.

> **Replays compound.** Each replay is additional ball time; additional ball time means more spinning target hits. Chase replays via the standup target completion.
