# 🤖 Terminator 2: Judgment Day - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1991 — Designed by Steve Ritchie, Software by Dwight Sullivan*

> **"Come with me if you want to score."**

T2 (Williams, 1991) is one of the best-selling pinball machines of all time — a WPC-era classic with a ball cannon you aim with a gun trigger, an autoplunger skull VUK, a left outlane kickback, video mode, and the T-1000 multiball sequence. Simple on the surface but rich underneath: cannon aiming is the game's central skill expression. Our goal: **200M–600M** through Skynet destruction, the cannon jackpot, and efficient multiball play.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Aim the cannon deliberately** — pull the trigger only when the cannon is pointed at a valuable target (not randomly)
- [ ] **Light the left outlane kickback** early every ball
- [ ] **Collect the CPU chips** (right ramp hits) to advance toward Judgment Day mode
- [ ] **Activate Skynet** by completing the drop target sequences
- [ ] **Lock balls** (kick-out holes × 2) to start T-1000 Multiball
- [ ] **Use video mode** when it's lit — it's free points with zero ball-drain risk

## 🤖 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Full plunge** | Autoplunger fires → skull VUK → ball enters cannon | Cannon positioning |
| **Soft plunge** | Ball doesn't reach skull → right flipper shot | Standard play |

The autoplunger is the default — the skull VUK grabs the ball and launches it into the cannon on the right. **The cannon sequence starts every ball** on full plunge.

### The Cannon (Right Side)

After being loaded by the skull VUK → habitrail, the ball sits in the cannon:
- The cannon oscillates left/right continuously
- **Pull the trigger (shooter rod)** to fire
- Five targets on the left side of the playfield — each with a different value
- **Top target (highest value):** Hard to aim but worth 3M–10M per hit
- **Bottom target:** Easy aim, 500K–1M per hit

> 🎯 **Wait for alignment.** The biggest T2 mistake is pulling the trigger as soon as the cannon loads. Wait. Watch the cannon sweep to the target you want, THEN fire.

### CPU Chips (Right Ramp)

The right ramp advances your CPU chip collection. Chips are used to:
- Qualify Judgment Day mode
- Increase multiball jackpot values
- Light the extra ball

Shoot the right ramp consistently throughout the game.

### Skull VUK → Habitrail → Cannon

The skull VUK under the playfield center launches the ball through a habitrail to the cannon. This sequence triggers:
- At game start (autoplunger)
- When the ball rolls over the skull during play
- From the kick-out holes in some configurations

Each cannon loading is an opportunity for a high-value aimed shot.

### Video Mode

When lit (via right ramp chip collection), the video mode activates on the DMD:
- A simple button-press game where you shoot T-800 Terminators marching toward you
- **Zero ball drain risk** — the ball is locked during video mode
- Average video mode score: 5M–20M depending on performance
- Always take it — free points with no risk

### Left Outlane Kickback

The left outlane has a standard kickback:
- Light it by hitting the left orbit
- Each orbit hit gives you one kickback charge
- Multiple orbit hits = multiple charges (rare but possible)
- **Light it immediately on ball 1.** This is non-negotiable.

## 🎳 Multiball (T-1000 Multiball)

### How to Start

Lock 2 balls in the two kick-out holes on the left side (lit after advancing Skynet/mode progression):

**Jackpot structure:**
- 3-ball multiball
- **Cannon jackpot:** Load the cannon (skull VUK) during multiball → aim cannon → fire at the lit jackpot target. Jackpot starts at 10M–20M and escalates to 50M+ per collection.
- **Super Jackpot:** Collect 3 cannon jackpots in one multiball → skull VUK → center target → 75M–200M+
- Right ramp during multiball = CPU chips + elevated values

> 🏆 **Cannon in multiball is everything.** The cannon jackpot is 10–50× the value of any other shot. Prioritize loading the cannon (skull VUK) during multiball over all other shots.

- **CPU chips:** Accumulate across the game — video mode, Judgment Day, and super jackpot all require chips
- **Skynet progression:** Each Skynet step increases multiball jackpot values
- **Kickback charges:** Replenish via left orbit — maintain at all times

## 🧙 Wizard Mode

### Judgment Day

**Requirement:** Collect full CPU chip set (typically 5–8 ramp completions).

**Gameplay:** All shots lit at 5× values. Cannon during Judgment Day fires the Judgment Day jackpot (100M–300M+ depending on Skynet level). The highest-scoring event in the game outside of super jackpot.

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Light kickback (left orbit)
2. Full plunge → cannon → aim top target
3. Right ramp × 3 (CPU chips)
4. Left kick-out lock (ball 1)

### Ball 2
1. Right ramp × 2 more chips → video mode (if available)
2. Second kick-out lock → T-1000 Multiball
3. During multiball: skull VUK → cannon jackpot × 3

### Ball 3
1. Super jackpot if multiball continues
2. Chase Judgment Day (CPU chip completion)
3. Right ramp + cannon for final scoring

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Wait on the cannon. Always.** The worst T2 players pull the trigger the instant they can. The best T2 players fire maybe 1–2 shots per cannon load, perfectly aimed. One top-target cannon hit beats 10 rushed bottom-target hits.

> **Video mode is a gift.** It appears mid-game and is frequently ignored. Never ignore it. 15M free points with zero drain risk.

> **The kickback is a lifeline.** With it active, drain attempts are survivable. Without it, any left-side drain ends a potentially great ball. Relight it constantly.
