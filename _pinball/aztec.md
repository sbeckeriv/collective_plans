# 🗿 Aztec Pinball Machine - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1976 — Designed by Gordon Horlick*

> **"Spell it. Double it. Bank it."**

Aztec is a classic mid-70s electromechanical machine — one of the best-selling EMs ever made — built around spelling A-Z-T-E-C across six standup targets, growing your bonus, and doubling it before the ball drains. With only two flippers and a simple ruleset, success here comes down to letter discipline and avoiding the machine's genuinely dangerous bumper-fed outlanes. Our goal: a strong **150K–300K** game.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Take the skill shot**: hit the top center lane to light the spinner for 1,000 points
- [ ] **Spell A-Z-T-E-C deliberately** — each lit letter is worth 5,000–10,000 points
- [ ] **Prioritize the T and Z standups** over C and E — they also light Double Bonus
- [ ] **Max your bonus to 100,000 first**, then go for Double Bonus via the Z or T target
- [ ] **Only chase C or E if extra balls are enabled** and you specifically need one to complete A-T-Z or A-C-E for the extra ball light
- [ ] **Respect the bumpers** — rebounds out of the pop bumpers are notoriously deadly, especially toward the left outlane

## 🗿 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

Hit the **top center lane** on the opening shot to light the spinner for **1,000 points**. After that, alternate between lit side lanes (right/left) to keep advancing bonus.

### Spelling A-Z-T-E-C

Six standup targets spell out AZTEC. Each lit letter scores **5,000 or 10,000 points**. You don't need to complete the whole word in order — light letters as they're convenient, but prioritize with intent (see below).

### Letter Priority: T and Z First

**T and Z are the most valuable letters to chase**, because completing either one also lights the **spinner lane and chute for Double Bonus**. C and E carry no such secondary benefit.

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Unless extra balls are enabled, you can often skip C and E entirely and still have a great game — funnel your shots toward A, T, and Z instead.

### Bonus System

- **Base bonus maxes at 100,000 points**
- Once maxed, shoot the **Z or T standup** to light the spinner lane and chute for **Double Bonus**
- Double Bonus is collected at end of ball, doubling your accumulated 100K to **200,000**

### Extra Ball (If Enabled)

Completing **A-T-Z** or **A-C-E** lights the center standup for an extra ball. Since T and Z are already priorities for Double Bonus, **A-T-Z is the natural combo to chase** — you rarely need to detour for C or E unless you're specifically hunting the extra ball via A-C-E.

> 💰 **Pro Tip:** Once you've banked both Max Bonus and Double Bonus, *then* consider mopping up the remaining AZTEC letters if extra balls are on — there's no rush before that.

### Key Feed Technique (Advanced)

On some machines, the chute kickout can be nudged so the ball rebounds repeatedly off the upper-right bumper back into the chute. Done several times in a row with multiple letters already lit, this is a recognized technique for racking up easy points quickly — but it depends on your specific machine's kicker strength and bumper response, so test it carefully before relying on it.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Shot | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Pop bumper rebounds** | "Notoriously deadly," especially toward the **left outlane** | Keep the ball away from extended bumper time; use controlled flipper shots rather than letting the ball linger in the bumpers |
| **Candy-cane chute grazing shots** | Frequently drains straight down the middle on a miss | Commit fully to the shot or don't take it — half-hearted attempts are the most dangerous |
| **Standup target rebounds** | Can kick back into outlane territory | Approach standups with a clean, deliberate shot rather than a glancing one |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball (Ball 1)

1. Take the skill shot for the spinner light and 1,000 points
2. Begin spelling AZTEC, prioritizing T and Z
3. Use the right flipper for the spinner and left flipper for the candy-cane chute as your two main shot lanes

**Target Score:** 30K–60K

### Mid Ball (Ball 2)

1. Push bonus toward the 100,000 maximum
2. Light Double Bonus via T or Z once maxed
3. If extra balls are enabled, work toward A-T-Z for the extra ball light

**Target Score:** 60K–120K

### Late Ball (Ball 3+)

1. Bank Max Bonus + Double Bonus as your priority — this is worth more than any single additional letter
2. Mop up remaining AZTEC letters only after bonus is secured
3. Use the bumper-rebound feed technique cautiously for quick incidental points if your machine supports it reliably

**Target Score per ball:** 30K–80K

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 6.0°–6.5° | Standard for mid-70s Williams EM machines |
| **Bumper/kicker strength** | Verify before relying on rebound feed technique | Inconsistent kicker strength changes how reliably the bumper-feed trick works |
| **Flipper strength** | Default | Right flipper sees the most action (spinner); keep both flippers consistent |

---

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Ball 1:** Skill shot, start spelling AZTEC (T/Z priority).
**Ball 2:** Max bonus to 100K, light Double Bonus.
**Ball 3+:** Bank Max + Double Bonus, mop up remaining letters, chase extra ball if enabled.

**Target High-Score Ballpark:** **150K–300K** is a strong, competitive score on Aztec — built almost entirely on disciplined letter priority and a maxed, doubled bonus.
