# 🦖 Jurassic Park (Data East, 1993) - Strategy Guide

*Data East, 1993 — Designed by Joe Kaminkow, John Borg & Ed Cebula*

> **"Life finds a way... straight into the T-Rex's mouth."**

This Data East original follows the film's plot beat-for-beat: opening modes give way to chaos as the dinosaurs escape, culminating in the **System Failure** wizard mode recreating the park's power outage. The signature toy is a mechanical T-Rex head that tracks the ball and snatches it from its saucer. Our goal: stack Computer Room modes, build through CHAOS into the 6-ball Tri-Ball, and reach System Failure.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Take the launch video skill shot** — it has built-in randomness on later code versions
- [ ] **The T-Rex saucer's first shot is worth 15 million**, and every shot after that builds toward a **bounty jackpot**
- [ ] **Hit the Control Room scoop** to activate stackable Computer Room modes, relit via the right scoop or in-lanes
- [ ] **Spell CHAOS and hit a dual jackpot** (ramp or helicopter loop) to start the 3-ball **Chaos Tri-Ball**
- [ ] **Spell CHAOS again during Tri-Ball** to progress to the full **6-ball multiball** — at that point, hitting **both jackpot shots** is worth enormous points, **multiplied by the number of balls in play**
- [ ] **The right ramp is notoriously tough** — don't force it under pressure

## 🦕 Core Mechanics

### T-Rex Saucer (Signature Feature)

- **First shot: 15 million points**
- **Every shot after that builds toward a bounty jackpot**
- The T-Rex toy head mechanically tracks the ball, and when it lands in its designated saucer, the T-Rex bends down and "snatches" it in its mouth
- When lit for Tri-Ball start, **hitting it always requires exactly one hit** to begin multiball, regardless of how many times you've shot it before

### Computer Room Modes

- Activated via the **Control Room scoop**
- **Stackable** — and relightable via the right scoop or in-lanes
- Each mode has its own value; part of the strategic decision is whether to **stack multiple modes together** or focus elsewhere
- The **Smart Missile** can be used strategically within modes to boost progress

### Multiball Progression

| Stage | Requirement | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **Raptor 2-Ball** | Shorter, less valuable multiball | Emphasizes ball control over scoring |
| **Chaos Tri-Ball** | Spell CHAOS + hit a dual jackpot (ramp or helicopter loop) | 3-ball |
| **Full 6-Ball Multiball** | Spell CHAOS again during Tri-Ball | 6-ball; both jackpot shots required for max points |

> 💰 **Pro Tip — Core Scoring Strategy:** During the 6-ball stage, **both jackpot shots together are worth a massive amount, multiplied by the number of balls currently in play**. This is explicitly the game's primary scoring opportunity — prioritize keeping balls in play and landing both jackpots over almost anything else once you're at 6-ball multiball.

---

## 🏆 System Failure (Wizard Mode)

**Requirement:** Complete all Computer Screen modes to light the Control Room.

- A **6-ball wizard mode** lasting **45 seconds**
- **Every shot on the playfield is worth 1,000,000 points**
- Typically nets **150–300 million points** in a successful run
- Reaching it generally requires **strategic mode stacking and Smart Missile usage** beforehand

---

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Right ramp** | Explicitly described as notoriously difficult ("T-O-U-G-H") | Don't force it under pressure; wait for a clean, controlled attempt |
| **Raptor Pit during certain modes** | While it offers ball-save capability in specific modes, don't assume universal protection | Verify which mode is active before relying on Raptor Pit safety |
| **6-ball multiball chaos** | Tracking 6 balls simultaneously is genuinely difficult | Prioritize landing the dual jackpot shots over precise control of every ball |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1)

1. Take the skill shot and start working the T-Rex saucer for its 15M first hit
2. Begin activating Computer Room modes via the Control Room scoop
3. Start building toward CHAOS letters

**Target Score:** 30M–80M

### Mid Game (Ball 2)

1. Spell CHAOS and hit a dual jackpot to start Chaos Tri-Ball
2. Stack additional Computer Room modes for bonus value
3. Build T-Rex bounty jackpot progress between mode attempts

**Target Score:** 80M–200M

### Late Game (Ball 3+)

1. Spell CHAOS again during Tri-Ball to escalate to full 6-ball multiball
2. Prioritize both jackpot shots for the balls-in-play multiplier bonus
3. If all Computer Screen modes are complete, run System Failure for the 1M-per-shot finale

**Target Score per ball:** 100M–350M+

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 6.5°–7.0° | Standard for early-90s Data East solid-state machines |
| **T-Rex toy mechanism** | Verify smooth tracking and "snatch" animation | Signature feature and central scoring element |
| **Right ramp calibration** | Check carefully — known difficulty area | Even with good calibration, expect this to remain a hard shot |

---

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Ball 1:** Hit the T-Rex saucer, start Computer Room modes, build toward CHAOS.
**Ball 2:** Start Chaos Tri-Ball, stack modes, build T-Rex bounty value.
**Ball 3+:** Escalate to 6-ball multiball, land both jackpots for the balls-multiplier, run System Failure if modes are complete.

**Target High-Score Ballpark:** **150–350 million points** is a strong, competitive game; reaching System Failure marks completion of the full Computer Room mode set.
