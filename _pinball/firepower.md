# 🚀 Firepower - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1980 — Designed by Steve Ritchie*

> **"The first electronic multiball — and still a clean, classic build-and-collect game."**

Firepower holds genuine pinball history: the first game with Lane Change, the first electronic multiball, and the first animated displays. Underneath the firsts is a straightforward bonus-multiplier game built around spelling F-I-R-E and a spinner that only pays well once the center standups are cleared. Our goal: max your multiplier at 5X, light the lucrative spinner, and use the (admittedly shallow) multiball for bonus feature collection.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Complete the F-I-R-E rollover lanes** for escalating bonus multipliers: 2X → 3X → 4X → 5X
- [ ] **Once at 5X, completing F-I-R-E again lights an Extra Ball** instead of advancing further
- [ ] **Clear all 6 Center Stand-Up Targets** to activate the spinner at its lit value
- [ ] **The spinner pays 100 unlit, but 1,000 when lit** — a 10x difference, so clearing the center bank is a high priority
- [ ] **Light the outlanes for Special, or rely on the left outlane kickback ("the Shield")** for drain protection
- [ ] **Lock all three balls** in the locks/eject holes to start the 3-ball multiball — useful for feature collection, but don't expect a jackpot payoff

## 🚀 Core Mechanics

### F-I-R-E Bonus Multiplier

Completing the four F-I-R-E rollover lanes advances your multiplier:

| Completion | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| 1st | 2X |
| 2nd | 3X |
| 3rd | 4X |
| 4th | **5X (max)** |
| 5th+ (after max) | Lights an **Extra Ball** |

**Base bonus** ranges from 1,000 to 29,000 points, multiplied by your current F-I-R-E stage.

### The Spinner (Center Targets Gate Its Value)

| State | Value |
|---|---|
| Unlit | 100 points |
| **Lit** (after clearing all 6 Center Stand-Up Targets) | **1,000 points** |

The spinner also serves as a signature shot route toward the **Upper Right Lock**.

> 🎯 **Pro Tip — Core Strategy:** Clearing the 6 center standups to light the spinner is one of the highest-leverage things you can do early — it converts your most repeatable shot from a minor 100-point trickle into a real 1,000-point-per-spin scoring engine.

### Multiball (Shallow, But Useful)

1. Lock all **three balls** in the locks/eject holes to start
2. **Honest assessment:** multiball here has **no jackpot objective** — it's "a bit shallow" by design standards of later games
3. Its real value is **increased playfield activity for points and feature collection** rather than a dedicated scoring system

> 💰 **Pro Tip:** Don't structure your whole strategy around multiball — treat it as a fun bonus phase where you keep flippers active and pick up whatever incidental scoring comes your way, not a goal in itself.

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Left and right outlanes** | The primary hazards on this layout | Light outlanes for Special when possible, or rely on the left outlane kickback ("the Shield") for protection |
| **Pop bumper unpredictability** | Standard early-80s bumper chaos with 4 bumpers on this layout | Treat as incidental scoring, not a shot to actively chase |
| **Center standup bank misses** | Wastes a shot that's otherwise high-value (unlocking the spinner) | Approach with controlled, deliberate shots rather than rushing |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Ball

1. Start working the F-I-R-E lanes toward your first multiplier increase
2. Clear the 6 Center Stand-Up Targets as early as possible to light the spinner
3. Keep the left outlane kickback (Shield) active for drain protection

### Mid Ball

1. Push F-I-R-E completions toward the 5X maximum
2. Grind the now-lit spinner for steady 1,000-per-spin scoring
3. Work toward locking all three balls for multiball

### Late Ball

1. Take the Extra Ball if you've completed F-I-R-E past 5X
2. Enjoy multiball for feature collection and incidental scoring once locks are complete
3. Bank your maxed bonus (up to 29,000 × 5X) at end of ball
