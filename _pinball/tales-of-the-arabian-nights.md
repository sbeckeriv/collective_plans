# 🧞 Tales of the Arabian Nights - Strategy Guide

*Williams, 1996 — Designed by John Popadiuk*

> **"Three magnets, two captive balls, and spike rings that save your ball. One wild ride."**

Tales of the Arabian Nights (TOTAN, Williams 1996) is one of the most mechanically inventive WPC machines ever made. It features three electromagnets, two captive balls, Newton ball posts, spinning posts, and spike rings that rise from the inlanes to trap and save draining balls. The game's theme is lush 1001 Nights fantasy with Sinbad, Scheherazade, and the Genie, and the rules reward players who use the unique hardware creatively. Our goal: **300M–800M** through Genie Multiball, story completion, and magnet trick shots.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Light the Genie Lamp** by hitting the left ramp → left orbit combo to start Genie Multiball
- [ ] **Complete the story modes** by shooting the center scoop (rotates through Sinbad's stories)
- [ ] **Use the spike rings** — when lit, the inlane spikes rise and stop the ball from draining; press the flipper at the right moment to capture and save it
- [ ] **Hit the captive balls** for Newton ball chain reactions and bonus multipliers
- [ ] **Shoot the kick-out hole** to collect accumulated story bonuses
- [ ] **Exploit the three magnets** — they activate during specific modes to redirect the ball

## 🌙 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Lamp skill shot** | Short plunge to hit the lamp target | Genie multiball advance + points |
| **Standard plunge** | Full plunge | Ball to left flipper |

### Spike Rings (Inlane Ball Saves)

The most distinctive feature: metal spikes rise from the inlane circles to temporarily cage the ball:
- Activated when lit (via story progression or random events)
- **Press the flipper when the spikes are up** to hold the ball in the spike ring
- Release the flipper to let the ball drop to the flipper for a fresh shot
- This is a controllable ball saver — learning it is the #1 skill gap on TOTAN

> 🎯 **Spike ring timing:** The window to activate them is about 0.5 seconds. Watch for the insert to flash and press immediately as the ball enters the inlane.

### Three Electromagnets

Three magnets are embedded in the playfield. They activate during:
- Genie Multiball (magnets grab and redirect balls)
- Specific story modes (magnet "tricks" redirect toward high-value shots)
- The ball does NOT follow normal physics when magnets are active — anticipate this

### Captive Balls + Newton Ball Posts (2 each)

Two captive balls sit in cages against Newton ball posts:
- Hit the captive ball → momentum transfers through the post → post strikes switch → score
- Useful for building bonus multiplier steps
- Chain both captive balls in sequence for Newton combo bonus

### Story Modes (Center Scoop — Scheherazade)

The center scoop starts and advances stories from 1001 Nights:
- **Sinbad's Voyage:** Left orbit shots
- **Ali Baba:** Center combo targets
- **The Genie:** Left ramp → right orbit
- **Aladdin:** Ramp + captive ball sequence

Completing each story awards a story bonus (25M–100M) and fills the storybook (visible on the backglass).

### Spinning Posts (2)

Two posts that spin when the ball makes contact. They can redirect the ball in unexpected directions — treat them as a chaotic element and keep both flippers ready when the ball is near them.

## 🎳 Multiballs

### Genie Multiball (Primary)

**How to start:** Light all three lamp stages (left ramp → left orbit combo, repeated). The Genie appears in the lamp.

**Jackpot structure:**
- 3-ball multiball with 3 magnets active
- Magnets grab balls and redirect them — you don't fully control where the ball goes during multiball
- Jackpots via left ramp + center scoop combo
- **Super Jackpot:** Complete a story sequence during Genie Multiball → center scoop → 75M–200M+
- The magnets extend multiball by preventing drains — exploit this by staying on the left side

### 4-Ball Multiball (Advanced)

Achievable by completing specific story arcs before Genie Multiball starts. The 4th ball adds a significant scoring opportunity but also increases chaos with 3 active magnets.

## 🔄 Progression Systems

- **Storybook:** Each completed story fills a page. Complete all stories → full storybook wizard mode
- **Bonus multiplier:** Captive ball + Newton post hits advance multiplier (up to 5×)
- **Extra ball:** Earned by completing 3 stories

## 🧙 Wizard Mode

### The Final Story (Full Storybook)

**Requirement:** Complete all story modes across the entire game.

**Gameplay:** Scheherazade finishes the 1001st night — all shots lit at 10× values. The Genie grants an unlimited multiball for 90 seconds. Elite territory — average to reach is playing this machine extremely well for an entire game.

| Left ramp | Genie advance + story | Low |
| Left orbit | Genie advance + story | Low |
| Center scoop | Story start/advance | Low |
| Right orbit | Story objectives | Low |
| Captive balls (2) | Newton chain + multiplier | Low |
| Kick-out hole | Story bonus collect | Medium |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Spinning posts** — chaotic ball direction; always be ready on both flippers near them
- **Magnet zone during Genie Multiball** — the magnets can hold the ball briefly then release it at unexpected angles; don't commit a flipper until you see where it lands
- **Left outlane** — primary drain; spike rings protect the right inlane more than the left

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **The spike rings are the #1 skill separator.** Players who learn to use them reliably play 30–40% longer per ball. Practice the timing obsessively.

> **Magnets are your allies during Genie Multiball, not enemies.** They prevent drains — let them catch the ball and then work with the redirected path rather than fighting it.

> **Center scoop is safer than it looks.** Many players avoid it fearing rejects, but the TOTAN scoop is well-designed. Shoot it confidently from the right flipper.

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 6.5° |
| All 3 magnets | Test activation strength — weak magnets don't catch fast-moving balls |
| Spike rings (2) | Verify mechanical rise/fall; stuck rings block the inlane |
| Newton posts | Inspect captive ball range of motion; restricted travel reduces combo scoring |
| Spinning posts | Check for stiff rotation — they should spin freely on contact |
