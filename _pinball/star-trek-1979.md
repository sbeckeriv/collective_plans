# 🖖 Star Trek (Bally, 1979) - Strategy Guide

*Bally, 1979 — Designed by Gary Gayton*

> **"Boldly go where no pinball has gone before... the kick-out hole."**

Star Trek (Bally, 1979) is a licensed solid-state machine built around a 4-bank drop target set, 5 standup targets, a kick-out hole, a free ball return lane, and the classic Bally SS bonus multiplier system. This is pre-widebody, focused Bally SS design — clean layout, deliberate bonus management, and drop target farming. Our goal: **150,000–300,000** through multiplier maximization and kick-out hole bonus collections.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Complete the 4-bank drop targets** to advance your bonus multiplier
- [ ] **Collect the kick-out hole** when lit for mid-ball bonus payouts
- [ ] **Hit all 5 standup targets** to light the extra ball
- [ ] **Use the free ball return lane** (left) to rescue near-drain balls
- [ ] **Build bonus base** through star rollovers and pop bumpers before collecting
- [ ] **Repeat drop target completions** — each reset gives another multiplier step

---

## 🚀 Core Mechanics

### Bonus Multiplier (Drop Targets)

| Completion | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| 1st completion | 2× |
| 2nd completion | 3× |
| 3rd completion | 4× |
| 4th completion | 5× (max) |

The 4-bank drop targets reset after each completion. Shoot them as a priority — 5× multiplier on a large base is your primary win condition.

### Kick-Out Hole

The kick-out hole lights and collects bonus when:
- A certain number of drop target completions are reached
- Specific standup target combinations are hit

When lit, shoot it to collect bonus mid-ball. **This is the most valuable single shot** when the multiplier is 4× or 5×.

### Standup Targets (5)

Five standup targets scattered around the playfield. Completing all 5 lights the extra ball insert. Individual targets score 100–500 points each. After completing all 5, they stay down for a bonus award.

### Star Rollovers (2)

Two star rollover lanes advance the bonus base value. Roll through them every pass — they're passive progress.

### Free Ball Return Lane

Located on the left side. When the ball enters this specific lane rather than the standard outlane, it's returned to the plunger for a free replay ball rather than counted as a drain. It's not lit or activated by player action — it's a geometric feature of the playfield. Aim to route the ball toward it when approaching the left drain.

### Pop Bumpers (3)

Pop bumpers in the center score 10–100 points per hit and build bonus base passively. Let them run after any upper-playfield action.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| 4-bank drop targets | Multiplier advance | Low |
| Kick-out hole | Mid-ball bonus collect | Medium |
| Standup targets | Extra ball + base points | Low |
| Star rollovers | Bonus base | Low (passive) |
| Pop bumpers | Base + passive | None |
| Free ball return lane | Ball save | Situational |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Right outlane** — primary drain; no rescue on right side
- **Post-drop-target rebound** — balls can kick sideways after target hits; be ready on both flippers
- **Kick-out hole exit** — the ejection angle can vary by machine; anticipate a side kick

---

## 🔄 Progression Systems

- **Multiplier** is the core progression: 1× → 5× via drop target completions
- **Standup target completion** lights extra ball (once per game)
- **Bonus base** accumulates from rollovers and bumpers; paid out at ball end × multiplier

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Ball 1
1. Drop targets × 1 (multiplier to 2×)
2. Standup targets × 3 (progress toward extra ball)
3. Kick-out hole collection when lit

### Balls 2–3
1. Drop targets to 5× multiplier
2. Standup target completion → extra ball
3. Kick-out collection at 5× — this is your peak scoring shot

### Final Balls
1. Protect the 5× multiplier — avoid unnecessary risks
2. Star rollover passes for base bonus building
3. Collect kick-out when lit

### Score Targets

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Hit the kick-out at maximum multiplier.** Wait until you're at 4× or 5× before chasing the kick-out — the same base bonus at 5× is worth 2.5× what it is at 2×.

> **The free ball return lane is geographic, not lit.** You can't see it flashing at you — you have to know where it is and direct the ball there when threatened.

> **Drop targets first, always.** Every ball starts the same: multiplier building via the 4-bank is the foundation of all subsequent scoring.
