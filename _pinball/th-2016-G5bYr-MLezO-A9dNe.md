# 🧙 The Hobbit (LE) - Strategy Guide

*Jersey Jack Pinball, 2016 — Limited Edition Widebody*

> **"There and back again. Now do it faster."**

The Hobbit LE (Jersey Jack, 2016) is one of Jersey Jack's most ambitious machines — a widebody with a massive LCD display, five ramps, three character multiballs (Smaug, Trolls, and Goblin King), and a deep quest-based progression through the events of all three Hobbit films. The LE edition adds a spinning Smaug figure over the playfield. Our goal: **1B–3B** by completing quests, stacking character multiballs, and triggering Barrel Escape wizard mode.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Shoot the middle ramp** (the Lonely Mountain) — it's the primary quest advance shot
- [ ] **Hit Smaug** (LE: the spinning dragon figure overhead) for Smaug mode activation
- [ ] **Complete Trolls' Camp** by hitting the troll target sequence for Troll Multiball
- [ ] **Advance through The Quest** (main mode) via the center scoop
- [ ] **Lock balls** in the barrel lock for Barrel Escape mode
- [ ] **Collect Dwarves** (character inserts) — completing the full company unlocks wizard mode

## 🐉 Core Mechanics

### Skill Shot

| Skill Shot | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Thorin's Map** | Short plunge to lit rollover | Quest advance + character unlock |
| **Arkenstone** | Full plunge to center lane | Arkenstone bonus (huge early points) |

### The Quest (Main Progression)

The game tracks a quest through The Hobbit's storyline. Quests are started via the center scoop and advance through:
- **There and Back Again** (Bag End through Rivendell)
- **Misty Mountains** (Goblin tunnels, Riddles in the Dark)
- **Mirkwood** (Spiders, Wood-elves)
- **Laketown + Lonely Mountain** (Barrel escape, Smaug)

Each quest completion awards escalating jackpots and advances the film progression on the LCD.

### Five Ramps

The LE has five distinct ramp shots:
| Ramp | Location | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|
| Far left | Thorin orbit | Character unlock |
| Left ramp | Gandalf approach | Quest chapter 1 |
| Middle ramp | Lonely Mountain | Primary quest advance |
| Right ramp | Mirkwood | Quest chapter 3 |
| Far right | Laketown | Barrel escape lead-in |

The middle ramp is your primary, but diversifying across all 5 is required for quest completion.

### Dwarves Collection (13 Dwarves)

Each of the 13 company dwarves is collected by specific shots. Collecting all 13:
- Unlocks Thorin mode (mega-jackpot opportunity)
- Permanently raises multiball jackpot ceiling for the game
- Required for wizard mode

### Smaug (LE Spinning Figure)

The spinning Smaug dragon figure rotates overhead. When lit:
- Hit Smaug during his vulnerable window (his underbelly exposed)
- Each hit advances the Smaug mode sequence
- Completing Smaug mode = massive single-shot jackpot + Smaug Multiball qualification

## 🎳 Multiballs (3)

### Smaug Multiball

**How to start:** Complete the Smaug mode sequence (multiple Smaug hits).

**Jackpot structure:**
- 3-ball multiball
- Smaug figure fires flame blasts during multiball (visual/audio)
- Jackpots via middle ramp and far right ramp alternating
- **Super Jackpot (Black Arrow):** Complete all jackpot shots → center scoop → 100M–400M+

### Troll Multiball

**How to start:** Hit the Troll target sequence (left side standup sequence — Bert, Tom, William in order).

**Gameplay:** 3-ball multiball with elevated left ramp values. Trolls also award character bonuses when hit during multiball.

### Goblin King Multiball

**How to start:** Complete the Misty Mountains quest chapter.

**Gameplay:** 4-ball (LE exclusive) multiball with elevated multi-ramp values. The most lucrative multiball in terms of shot count.

- **Quest chapters:** Permanent within game; each completed chapter increases jackpot base
- **Dwarves:** Permanent within game; collect steadily throughout
- **Arkenstone:** Collecting all Arkenstone shards (scattered shots) unlocks a mystery mega-bonus

## 🧙 Wizard Mode

### Barrel Escape

**Requirement:** Complete all quest chapters + collect 10+ dwarves.

**Gameplay:** All 5 ramps lit simultaneously at maximum values. The barrel sequence (right-to-left ramp cascade) awards the Barrel Escape jackpot — typically 300M–800M in a single run.

## 📊 Shot Analysis

| Shot | Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Middle ramp (Lonely Mountain) | Quest advance (primary) | Low |
| Smaug figure (LE) | Smaug mode | Low (timing-based) |
| Troll targets (left) | Troll MB qualify | Low |
| Center scoop | Quest chapter + super jackpot | Medium |
| Far right ramp (Laketown) | Barrel escape + jackpot | Low |
| All 5 ramps | Widebody coverage | Medium overall |

### Dangerous Areas

- **Widebody gaps** — the extra width creates larger gaps between flipper pairs; slower ball handling required
- **Post-Smaug shot** — the figure spins and can deflect the ball irregularly on contact

## 💡 Pro Tips

> **Collect dwarves as you naturally shoot.** Most dwarf inserts are tied to shots you'd make anyway (left ramp, right ramp, orbits). Don't go out of your way — the collection completes itself with consistent play.

> **Smaug's underbelly window is generous.** The LE Smaug figure rotates at a manageable speed — watch one full rotation before shooting. You'll see the window coming.

> **The middle ramp is the game's spine.** Just like AC/DC centers on one key shot per song, Hobbit centers on the Lonely Mountain ramp. Default to it between deliberate sequences.
