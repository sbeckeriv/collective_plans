# 📱 Dialed In! - Strategy Guide

*Jersey Jack Pinball, 2017 — Designed by Pat Lawlor*

> **"Every SIM card you collect makes everything else worth more."**

Dialed In!'s entire scoring engine runs through SIM Cards: each one you collect acts as a **permanent multiplier for every mode you play for the rest of the game**, so the earlier you start banking them, the more your later scoring compounds. Eleven disaster modes, a Big Bang shortcut, and multiball feed into the Armageddon and Showdown wizard modes. Our goal: stack SIM Cards early, then cash in disaster modes at high multiplier value.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Understand SIM Cards are the whole game**: each one (100K base) acts as a permanent multiplier for every mode played afterward — a mode completed with 7-8 SIM Cards can be worth up to **1 million points**
- [ ] **Hit QED enough times to light the cell phone border green**, then shoot the **scoop below it** to start a mode
- [ ] **Choose your mode via the phone display**, rotating selection with the flipper button before launching
- [ ] **Look for a lit Big Bang target** — hitting it auto-completes your current mode and lights the SIM card instantly, no need to finish the mode shots
- [ ] **Prioritize lit SIM card shots or a lit Big Bang target from the upper flipper** over side ramps or quick mode-start flips
- [ ] **Extra ball lights (orange BOB left orbit) after 3 modes played**

---

## 📞 Core Mechanics

### SIM Cards (The Entire Scoring Engine)

- Each SIM Card is worth **100K points** on its own
- **Critically, every SIM Card you hold acts as a permanent multiplier for all modes played for the rest of the game**
- A mode completed while holding 7-8 SIM Cards can be worth up to **1,000,000 points**

> 💰 **Pro Tip:** Because SIM Cards multiply *future* mode scoring, collecting them early in the game is dramatically more valuable than collecting them late — every mode you complete after banking a SIM Card benefits from it.

### Starting & Choosing Disaster Modes

1. Hit **QED** enough times to light the cell phone border **green**
2. Shoot the **scoop below it** to bring up mode selection
3. **Rotate your mode choice with the flipper button** before launching — the phone display shows which mode will start
4. Among the 11 disaster modes, **Meteor Storm, Flash Fires, and EMP** (with theatre magnet assistance) are commonly cited as the easiest to complete

### Big Bang Shortcut

The **Big Bang target** (beneath the ramps) requires repeated hits to light. Once lit, **hitting it auto-completes your current mode and lights the SIM card** — a valuable shortcut when you don't have a clean shot at the mode's actual required targets.

> 🎯 **Pro Tip — Shot Priority:** Always prioritize a lit SIM card shot or a lit Big Bang target (ideally from the upper flipper) over side ramps or a quick flip at mode start — those secondary shots are only worth taking when neither priority shot is available.

---

## 🎁 Multiball Modes

- **Under Attack Multiball**: fun when it happens, but not a major point source — treat it as an opportunity for incidental SIM card collection rather than a primary scoring target
- Other multiball variants include a **moving-target jackpot mode**, a **diverter-dodge ramp jackpot mode**, and an **add-a-ball mode** (shoot the phone scoop 3 times to add a ball)
- **Quantum Mania Multiball** starts after completing four component parts

---

## 🏆 Wizard Modes

| Wizard Mode | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Armageddon** | Play all 11 disaster modes — starts a timed multiball |
| **Showdown** | Collect 8 SIM Cards by completing disasters and other features |

Playing modes is what advances you toward both — there's no shortcut around mode completion for reaching either wizard mode.

---

## 🎯 BOB Targets & Extra Ball

- **Extra ball lights** (orange BOB left orbit light) after **3 modes have been played**
- Completing the **B-O-B targets** relights the kickback and lights the left orbit for **Crazy Bobs** awards

---

## ⚠️ Dangerous Shots & Drain Patterns

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Chasing side ramps over priority shots** | Lower point value and not worth the risk when better shots are lit | Stick to the SIM card / Big Bang priority order described above |
| **Moving target / diverter multiball shots** | Require precise timing against moving mechanisms | Watch the mechanism's pattern before committing to the shot |
| **Quick-flip mode starts under pressure** | Can lead to careless shots | Only take a quick-flip start if your priority shots are genuinely unavailable |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game (Ball 1)

1. Start collecting SIM Cards as early and often as possible — this is the highest-leverage decision in the whole game
2. Light QED and run an easy mode (Meteor Storm, Flash Fires, or EMP) to bank your first SIM Card
3. Watch for a lit Big Bang target as a shortcut to completing modes faster

**Target Score:** 5M–15M

### Mid Game (Ball 2)

1. Keep stacking SIM Cards — by now you should be running modes at a meaningful multiplier
2. Work toward your 3-mode extra ball via BOB
3. Build toward Quantum Mania or other multiball qualifications

**Target Score:** 15M–50M

### Late Game (Ball 3+)

1. Run modes at maximum SIM Card multiplier for the biggest individual mode payouts
2. If you've played all 11 disasters, push for Armageddon
3. If you've banked 8 SIM Cards, push for Showdown

**Target Score per ball:** 20M–80M+

## ⚙️ Setup Recommendations

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Playfield pitch** | 6.5°–7.0° | Jersey Jack factory standard |
| **Magnet calibration** | Verify all 5 magnets function correctly | Central to several disaster modes (e.g., EMP's theatre magnet assist) |
| **Big Bang target sensitivity** | Check consistent registration | Core shortcut mechanic for mode completion |

---

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Ball 1:** Bank SIM Cards early via easy modes, learn the Big Bang shortcut.
**Ball 2:** Keep stacking SIM Cards, chase the 3-mode extra ball, build toward multiball.
**Ball 3+:** Run modes at high multiplier value, push for Armageddon (11 disasters) or Showdown (8 SIM Cards).

**Target High-Score Ballpark:** **40–100 million points** is a strong, competitive game — built almost entirely on collecting SIM Cards as early as possible to multiply everything that comes after.
