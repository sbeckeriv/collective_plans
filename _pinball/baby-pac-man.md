# 👶 Baby Pac-Man - Strategy Guide

*Bally Midway, 1982 — Video/Pinball Hybrid*

> **"Trick the ghosts. Earn the energizer. Eat them all."**

Baby Pac-Man is a true hybrid: a video maze on the cabinet screen feeds into a physical pinball table below it, and the two halves constantly trade control back and forth. The video maze starts with **no energizers at all** — you have to earn them on the pinball table — which makes pinball-side play the real engine behind a big maze-side score. Our goal: chase the highest-value ghost combos and stack fruit upgrades for a strong overall score across both halves of the cabinet.

---

## 🎯 Quick-Start Checklist for New Players

- [ ] **Remember: the maze starts with zero energizers** — you cannot eat ghosts until you earn one on the pinball table
- [ ] **Light P-A-C-M-A-N drop targets** to spawn an energizer in the maze (location depends on which of the 4 light sets you complete)
- [ ] **Light F-R-U-I-T-S** (top-left tunnel) to upgrade the maze's bonus fruit toward the high-value Banana
- [ ] **Light T-U-N-N-E-L** (top-right tunnel) to speed up Baby Pac-Man's warp-tunnel travel — up to 8 stacks
- [ ] **Use the saucers strategically** — entering one locks the ball and sends you back to the maze with a specific energizer active
- [ ] **Chase ghost combos in succession** — eating all 4 ghosts back-to-back is worth far more than picking them off one at a time
- [ ] **Don't slam the cabinet** — Baby Pac-Man has a hard anti-slam tilt that ends the game immediately, no warning

## 🕹️ Core Mechanics

### How the Two Halves Connect

1. Every game **starts in the video maze**, controlled by the joystick
2. Two chutes at the bottom of the maze drop you into **pinball mode** on the physical table
3. Flippers keep the ball alive on the pinball table; if it drains, you're sent back to the maze (and that chute seals until you lose a life or finish the maze)
4. **Saucers** beneath the F-R-U-I-T-S and T-U-N-N-E-L tunnels can lock the ball and send you back to the maze early, with a specific energizer now active depending on which saucer/energizer combo triggered it

> ⚠️ **Anti-slam tilt:** Slamming the cabinet causes an **immediate Game Over**, regardless of remaining lives — be gentle with nudges.

### Video Maze Side

- **No energizers at the start** — you must earn them via the pinball table's P-A-C-M-A-N drop targets
- Up to **4 energizers** can appear per maze, each tied to a specific corner (1 = top-left, 2 = bottom-left, 3 = bottom-right, 4 = top-right)
- **Side tunnels** warp Baby Pac-Man across the maze; a T-U-N-N-E-L speed upgrade from the pinball side makes this warp faster
- Ghosts are more aggressive than in standard Pac-Man — they always target Baby Pac-Man directly and can make instant 180° turns. Red is faster than Baby, Blue/Green match his speed, and Pink is slower
- **Maze completion bonus:** 10,000 points, increasing by 10,000 for each subsequent maze completed
- Maze walls become invisible starting on round 7 — a significant late-game difficulty spike

### Pinball Side

| Feature | Effect |
|---|---|
| **P-A-C-M-A-N drop targets** (4 light sets) | Completing a set spawns an energizer in a specific maze corner |
| **F-R-U-I-T-S tunnel** (top-left) | Advances the maze fruit toward higher value (Cherry → Banana); also lights an Extra Baby arrow |
| **T-U-N-N-E-L tunnel** (top-right) | Increases warp-tunnel speed (stacks up to 8x); also lights an Extra Baby arrow |
| **Extra Baby arrows** | Light progressively from drop target/tunnel hits; completing all of them (with correctly-timed final flash) awards an extra life |
| **Saucers** (under the two tunnels) | Lock the ball, return you to the maze with a specific energizer active depending on side/energizer combo |

## ⚠️ Dangerous Moments & Mistakes

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Slamming the cabinet** | Causes immediate Game Over — always nudge gently, never strike the machine |
| **Excess tilting** | Locks the flippers and ends pinball mode early, sending you back to the maze without finishing your pinball-side scoring |
| **Entering the maze with zero energizers** | You're defenseless against ghosts — prioritize earning at least one energizer via P-A-C-M-A-N targets before re-entering if you have the choice |
| **Invisible maze walls (round 7+)** | Memorize maze layouts in advance since you can no longer see walls visually |

## 💰 Scoring Strategy

### Early Game

1. Enter pinball mode early and work the P-A-C-M-A-N drop targets to guarantee at least one energizer before facing ghosts
2. Pass through F-R-U-I-T-S once or twice to start upgrading fruit value
3. Eat dots efficiently in the maze (100 each) while avoiding ghosts without an energizer active

**Target Score:** 20K–50K

### Mid Game

1. Make repeated pinball-mode trips to push fruit value higher and stack T-U-N-N-E-L speed
2. Lure ghosts into tunnels for big 4-combo eats once you have an energizer
3. Bank maze completion bonuses — they escalate by 10K each time

**Target Score:** 50K–120K

### Late Game

1. By round 7+, rely on memorized maze layouts since walls go invisible
2. Keep cycling pinball mode for Extra Baby arrow completions — a banked extra life is worth more than almost any single scoring opportunity
3. Maximize ghost combos every chance you get; high fruit tier + 4-combo ghost eats are your biggest point sources

**Target Score per life:** 30K–80K+

## 📋 Quick Reference: Strategic Priorities

**Early:** Earn your first energizer via P-A-C-M-A-N targets before risking the maze.
**Mid:** Stack fruit tier and tunnel speed via repeated pinball trips; chain ghost combos.
**Late:** Memorize invisible maze walls, keep earning Extra Baby arrows, maximize every 4-ghost combo.

**Target High-Score Ballpark:** **80K–150K** is a strong, well-rounded game across both maze and pinball halves of this rare hybrid cabinet.
